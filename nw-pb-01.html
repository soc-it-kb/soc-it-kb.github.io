NW-PB-01: Suspicious Network Activity / Intrusion Attempt

Standardised decision and response framework for detecting, investigating, and responding to suspicious or malicious network activity — including intrusion attempts, anomalous traffic, and early indicators of lateral movement or command-and-control.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a standardised decision and response framework for detecting, investigating, and responding to suspicious or malicious network activity, including intrusion attempts, anomalous traffic patterns, and early indicators of lateral movement or command-and-control (C2).

This playbook acts as the network equivalent of EP-PB-01, and is the escalation target for firewall / IDS / NDR alerts, MDR network escalations, and SOC-identified anomalous traffic.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • Firewall, IDS/IPS, NDR alerts
  • Suspicious inbound or outbound traffic
  • East-west (internal) network anomalies
  • Network activity linked to endpoint or identity incidents
  • On-prem and cloud network telemetry

❌ Out of scope

  • Pure endpoint malware execution → EP-PB-02
  • Identity-only attacks with no network anomaly → ID playbooks
  • Confirmed ransomware encryption → EP-PB-05
Section 3
Trigger Conditions

Initiate this playbook when any of the following occur:

  • Firewall or IDS detects an intrusion attempt
  • NDR flags anomalous traffic or beaconing behaviour
  • MDR escalates suspicious network behaviour
  • SOC observes unusual outbound connections, unexpected internal scanning, or traffic to known malicious IPs/domains
  • Endpoint or identity incident reveals associated network indicators
Section 4
Initial Validation (Decision Logic)

Validate whether the activity is potentially malicious:

  • Is the traffic expected for the system's role and function?
  • Is the destination known, trusted, and business-justified?
  • Does the timing align with normal operational patterns?
  • Is the traffic associated with approved tooling or services?

If YES — legitimate explanation confirmed → Document and close as Benign Network Activity
If NO or UNCLEAR → Proceed with network threat assessment (Section 5)

Section 5
Network Threat Assessment

Assess intent, direction, and risk. Presence of any indicator = Suspicious Network Activity:

Repeated blocked or allowed intrusion attempts
Unexpected outbound connections to unknown destinations
Internal scanning or port sweeps observed
Traffic to known malicious infrastructure confirmed
Beaconing or periodic timed connections detected
Section 6
Severity & Escalation Criteria

Escalate to High Severity if any of the following apply:

🔴 Traffic confirms an active exploitation attempt
🔴 Internal lateral movement suspected or confirmed
🔴 Endpoint compromise likely or confirmed via correlated telemetry
🔴 Privileged systems or critical servers involved
🔴 MDR confirms attacker-controlled traffic

Notify immediately
👔
SOC Leadership
High severity awareness
🌐
Network / Infra Team
Blocking and isolation support
🤝
MDR Partner
If applicable
Section 7
Response Strategy

The following actions must be orchestrated based on threat assessment:

Network traffic analysis — source, destination, protocolRequired
Source and destination identificationRequired
Containment decision — block or isolateRequired
Scope expansion across network telemetryRequired
Endpoint correlationConditional
Identity correlationConditional

If network activity links to endpoint compromise → escalate immediately to EP-PB-01, EP-PB-02, or EP-PB-03 as appropriate

Section 9
Containment Decision Logic

Choose the appropriate containment approach based on risk and confidence:

🔴 Immediate blocking

  • Known malicious IP or domain confirmed
  • Active exploitation attempt observed
  • C2 traffic confirmed by MDR or tooling

🟡 Monitored containment

  • Behaviour is suspicious but not yet confirmed malicious
  • Risk of business disruption from blocking is high

All containment actions must be documented and approved per IR governance before execution.

Section 10
Scope Expansion Logic

Scope expansion is mandatory for all NW-PB-01 incidents:

Search actions

  • Search for same indicators across firewalls, NDR, and proxy logs
  • Identify additional systems communicating with the same destination
  • Check historical data for recurring patterns

Correlation actions

  • Correlate with endpoint telemetry
  • Correlate with identity sign-in telemetry
  • Cross-reference with open MDR incidents
Section 11
Communication & Coordination
  • Coordinate all blocking actions with the network team before execution
  • Avoid alerting the attacker through premature or visible containment
  • Align timing with endpoint and identity containment actions
  • Maintain MDR ticket synchronisation throughout the incident
Section 12
Closure Criteria

The incident may be closed when all of the following are confirmed:

  • Malicious or suspicious traffic fully contained
  • No further indicators observed across network telemetry
  • Affected endpoints and identities validated clean
  • Root cause identified (external probe, malware, misconfiguration)
  • All actions documented and incident record completed
Section 13
Evidence to Capture
Source and destination IPs
Ports and protocols
Firewall / IDS / NDR alerts
Traffic volumes and timing
Correlated endpoint or identity events
Timeline of containment actions
Section 14
Post-Incident Actions
  • Tune firewall, IDS, and NDR detection rules
  • Update threat intelligence feeds with confirmed indicators
  • Improve east-west network visibility
  • Review network segmentation adequacy
  • Feed indicators into threat hunting use cases
  • Update network hardening guidance
