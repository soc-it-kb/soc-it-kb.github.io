GRC-SOP-04: Cyber & IT Risk Management Procedure

End-to-end procedure for identifying, analysing, evaluating, treating, and monitoring cyber and IT risks across the organisation. Aligned with ISO 31000, ISO 27005, ISO 27001:2022 Annex A, and NIST CSF 2.0.

Section 1
Purpose & Scope

This procedure defines how cyber and IT risks are identified, analysed, evaluated, treated, monitored, and communicated across the organisation. It establishes a consistent, defensible methodology that satisfies internal governance requirements and external audit expectations under ISO 27001:2022 and NIST CSF 2.0.

In scope: All cyber security risks, IT operational risks, third-party (supplier) cyber risks, compliance risks with a cyber or IT dimension, and business continuity risks arising from cyber or IT cause. Applies to all assets, systems, services, and data within the organisation's IT and cyber estate, including hosted, on-premises, SaaS, and managed-service environments.

Out of scope: Enterprise-wide non-cyber risks (financial, market, strategic, reputational risks unrelated to cyber/IT), which are managed under the Enterprise Risk Management framework.

Section 2
Risk Management Process (ISO 31000)

The risk management process follows the ISO 31000 lifecycle. Each stage is mandatory and produces records held in the Cyber & IT Risk Register (GRC-REG-RISK).

1
Establish Context
Define the internal and external context, stakeholders, criteria, and the scope for which risks are being assessed (e.g. enterprise-wide annual review, new project, post-incident review)
Confirm risk appetite and tolerance levels approved by the Risk Committee
2
Risk Identification
Identify cyber and IT risks through threat modelling, vulnerability scans, incident history, audit findings, supplier assessments, and stakeholder workshops
For each risk, capture the threat source, vulnerability, asset/process affected, and potential consequence
Submit each new risk via GRC-FRM-13 (Risk Assessment & Submission Form)
3
Risk Analysis
Score the inherent risk (before any controls) using the 5×5 likelihood × impact matrix in Section 4
Document existing controls applied to the risk
Score the residual risk (after current controls)
4
Risk Evaluation
Compare residual risk against the organisation's risk appetite and tolerance
Determine whether the risk is acceptable, requires further treatment, or must be escalated
Map the risk to NIST CSF 2.0 outcomes and ISO 27001:2022 Annex A controls
5
Risk Treatment
Select a treatment option: Mitigate, Accept, Transfer, or Avoid (see Section 6)
Define the treatment plan, target residual score, named owner, and target date
For Accept decisions, record in the Risk Acceptance Log (GRC-REG-RISK-LOG) with formal approval
6
Monitor & Review
Review risks at the cadence defined in Section 8 (Critical: monthly, High: quarterly, Medium: semi-annually, Low: annually)
Re-score residual risk when controls change, when threats evolve, or when incidents occur
Close risks that are no longer applicable; archive with rationale
7
Communicate & Consult
Report risk posture to the Risk Committee and executive sponsor at the agreed cadence
Engage stakeholders during identification, treatment selection, and review
Share material changes (new Critical risks, treatment overruns) immediately
Section 3
Risk Categorisation

Every risk is assigned to one primary category. Categories drive ownership, review cadence, and reporting.

Cyber Operational — day-to-day SOC, detection, response Cyber Strategic — programme-level, long-horizon IT Operational — availability, capacity, change Third-Party — supplier, vendor, sub-processor Compliance — regulatory, contractual, framework Business Continuity — cyber/IT-driven disruption
Section 4

Likelihood and impact are each scored on a 1–5 scale. The product (Likelihood × Impact, range 1–25) determines severity.

2 · Unlikely 2 4 6 8 10

Section 5
Risk Appetite & Tolerance

The organisation's cyber and IT risk appetite is set annually by the Risk Committee. The default position, applied where no specific tolerance is documented, is:

  • Critical (20–25): Not tolerated. Treatment plan with target completion within 30 days mandatory; executive sponsor notified within 5 working days.
  • Very High (15–16): Not tolerated without formal acceptance. Treatment plan within 60 days; Risk Committee approval required to retain.
  • High (10–12): Tolerated only where treatment is in flight. Treatment plan within 90 days; reviewed quarterly.
  • Medium (5–9): Tolerated with active monitoring. Reviewed semi-annually; no formal acceptance required unless persistent.
  • Low / Very Low (1–4): Tolerated. Reviewed annually; treated where cost-effective.
Section 6
Treatment Options

One of four treatment decisions is selected for every risk. The default expectation is Mitigate.

🛡️ Mitigate

Apply additional or strengthened controls to reduce likelihood, impact, or both. Default response. Document the new control set and target residual score.

📋 Accept

Tolerate the risk at its current residual score. Permitted only with formal approval recorded in the Risk Acceptance Log (GRC-REG-RISK-LOG). Time-bound with mandatory review date.

🔄 Transfer

Shift the risk through insurance, contractual indemnity, or service-provider responsibility. Document the transfer mechanism and any retained residual risk.

🚫 Avoid

Eliminate the activity, technology, or supplier giving rise to the risk. Document the decision, the alternative chosen, and any transitional risks.

Section 7
Roles & Responsibilities

Risk Owner

Accountable for the risk and its treatment. Approves residual risk score and treatment plan. Typically a senior business or technology leader.

Control Owner

Responsible for designing, implementing, and operating the controls that mitigate the risk. Reports control effectiveness to the Risk Owner.

GRC Team

Maintains the register, facilitates analysis and review, ensures methodology is applied consistently, and prepares reports for the Risk Committee.

Risk Committee

Approves risk appetite, reviews material risks, endorses risk acceptances above defined thresholds, and provides governance oversight.

Executive Sponsor

Provides senior accountability for the cyber and IT risk programme. Receives escalation of Critical risks. Reports to the Board on material risk posture.

Internal Audit

Provides independent assurance over the design and operating effectiveness of the risk management process. Does not own risks.

Section 11
Records & Audit Evidence

The following records are produced and retained as audit evidence:

Cyber & IT Risk Register (GRC-REG-RISK) — current state of all identified risks
Risk Acceptance Log (GRC-REG-RISK-LOG) — formally accepted risks with approver and expiry
Risk Assessment & Submission Forms (GRC-FRM-13) — initial assessment for each new risk
Review records — minutes or notes from periodic risk reviews
Risk Committee minutes — endorsement of appetite, escalations, acceptances
Treatment plan tracking — progress against target dates and target residual scores
Section 12
Procedure Triggers

This procedure is initiated whenever any of the following occur:

Annual enterprise risk review cycle New project, system, or supplier onboarding Material incident or near-miss requiring lessons-learned Audit or assessment finding requiring response Regulatory or framework change affecting controls Threat-landscape shift (e.g. new high-profile exploitation) Material change in business activity or technology stack
