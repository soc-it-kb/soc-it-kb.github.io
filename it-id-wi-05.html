IT-ID-WI-05: Privileged Identity Governance

The eligibility layer above PIM activation. Who is eligible for which privileged role, why, for how long, reviewed by whom. Privileged role catalogue. Eligibility duration limits. Two-person approval for Tier-0. Separation of duties at Tier-0. Standing-privilege minimisation. Quarterly eligibility review. Privileged access request workflow. PAW-correlated activation. Tier-0 activity to immutable SIEM. Operationalises §4.5 of IT-ID-SOP-01.

Prerequisites

Before applying this WI:

  • IT-CL-WI-01 (Tenant Identity & Conditional Access) in place — PIM activation mechanism configured at tenant level; this WI is the governance layer above
  • IT-SI-WI-04 (PAM Platform Hardening) in place where on-prem privileged credentials are vaulted
  • IT-SRV-WI-DC-01 (Tier-0 Administrative Access Enforcement) in place — the technical enforcement of tier separation for AD
  • IT-EP-SOP-PAW-01 (PAW) in place — Tier-0 activation needs PAW for trustworthy administrative source
  • Core concepts (Tier 0/1/2/3 model) understood across IAE, GRC, and the Privileged Access Authority
  • Privileged Access Authority constituted — named individuals or named group acting as approval authority (typically CISO + Head of IT + nominee)
  • Centralised logging in place for all administrative activity — cannot govern what cannot be observed
  • Break-glass accounts documented per IT-CL-WI-01; counted, named, monitored — this WI tracks them but does not create them
This WI is the governance of who is eligible; the activation mechanics live in IT-CL-WI-01 Step 7 (Entra PIM activation) and IT-SI-WI-04 (PAM session for on-prem). Activation = how someone temporarily elevates. Eligibility = whether they should be allowed to. The two layers cooperate; don't conflate.
Step-by-step instructions

The steps below operationalise IT-ID-SOP-01 §4.5.

1
Maintain the privileged role catalogue
Every privileged role (Tier-0 and Tier-1) is documented with scope, currently-eligible individuals, eligibility duration, last-review date.

This is the privileged-identity-focused subset of IT-ID-WI-03's role catalogue. Same record format, deeper review cadence, more constrained eligibility list.

  • Tier-0 roles: Global Administrator (Entra), Privileged Role Administrator (Entra), Enterprise Admin / Schema Admin / Domain Admin (AD), Tenant Admin equivalents across platforms.
  • Tier-1 roles: Subscription Owner / Contributor at high scopes, AD admin accounts not in T0 groups, Exchange Admin, SharePoint Admin, security tooling admin roles (Defender, CrowdStrike administrative consoles), backup-platform admin.
  • Per-role record: role identifier, scope (what it grants), tier, named individuals currently eligible (not assigned), eligibility duration per individual, last-review date, last-reviewer.
  • Source-of-truth: catalogue is authoritative; directory state reconciled monthly against catalogue (Step 9 of IT-ID-WI-03 drift check feeds here).
  • Tier-0 catalogue snapshots retained for 1 year minimum — "who was eligible for what when" is forensics gold during incident response.
Verify. Catalogue exists; T0 / T1 roles enumerated with per-individual eligibility; counts reconciled against directory state within tolerance (drift items in flight).
NIST 800-53 AC-6(7) · CIS Controls 6.8 · ISO 27001 A.8.2
2
Eligibility, not assignment — activation required
Privileged role membership is held as activation-required (PIM eligible) rather than as standing assignment, except for documented break-glass accounts.

Standing privileged assignment is the absence of the control. If a role is "always on" for an identity, every compromise of that identity is automatically a privileged compromise.

  • Cloud (Entra): Privileged roles assigned via Entra PIM as eligible; activation requires the user to request through PIM with MFA, justification, and (for high-tier roles) approval.
  • On-prem AD: Privileged group membership controlled via PAM platform (IT-SI-WI-04) where vaulted-credential checkout serves as activation; or via shadow-group / time-bounded membership patterns where direct PIM-equivalent isn't available.
  • Break-glass exception: documented break-glass accounts (per IT-CL-WI-01) hold standing Global Admin; these are the exception and counted (Step 6).
  • Any other standing assignment: an exception per IT-GOV-SOP-01; named, justified, time-bounded, reviewed quarterly.
  • Direct-assignment detection: monthly check (per IT-ID-WI-03 Step 9 drift check) catches privileged direct-assignments outside eligibility; investigated and remediated.
Verify. Tier-0 role memberships: PIM-eligible identities >> PIM-active identities. Standing assignments equal to (or fewer than) documented break-glass count. No unexplained standing privilege.
NIST 800-53 AC-6(1) · CIS Controls 6.8 · MITRE ATT&CK M1026
3
Time-bound eligibility duration
Eligibility itself has a fixed end-date; renewal requires fresh justification, not automatic continuation.

"Eligible permanently" is "standing privilege" wearing a different hat. Eligibility must expire.

  • Tier-0 eligibility: maximum 12 months. Renewal requires re-justification with current need; Privileged Access Authority decision.
  • Tier-1 eligibility: maximum 18 months. Renewal requires re-justification; approver per role definition.
  • Project / time-bounded eligibility: where the privileged role is tied to a finite project, eligibility duration matches project end date; not extended past project closure.
  • Eligibility expiry alerts: 30 days before expiry, 7 days before, 1 day before, on expiry. Sent to the eligible individual, their manager, and Privileged Access Authority.
  • On expiry: eligibility automatically removed by PIM / tooling; manual cleanup where automation absent. No silent extension.
  • Reinstatement: requires fresh approval per Step 4; not silent re-grant on the same approval as the expired eligibility.
Verify. Sample 5 Tier-0 eligibilities; each has a documented end-date within 12 months of grant; renewal events have fresh justification records. Eligibility expiry alerts firing.
NIST 800-53 AC-6(7) · ISO 27001 A.8.2
4
Approval workflow with two-person rule for Tier-0
Granting privileged eligibility requires approval; Tier-0 requires two-person approval; both approvals are logged and retained.
  • Tier-0 approval: requesting manager + Privileged Access Authority. Two separate decisions, two separate records. Neither approval alone suffices.
  • Tier-1 approval: single approver per role definition (typically system owner or Privileged Access Authority depending on the role).
  • Approval evidence: requestor, justification, approvers, decisions, timestamps. Retained at minimum 3 years (longer per regulatory requirement).
  • Approval workflow tool: ServiceNow, Entra IG entitlement packages with multi-stage approval, or equivalent. Manual workflow as fallback with the same evidence captured.
  • Forbidden patterns: self-approval, approver-of-approver (the same individual approving in both Tier-0 stages), approval by an individual who would be a beneficiary of the access.
  • Emergency grants: a documented expedited path for incident response. The grant happens; the approval is contemporaneous (not retrospective); approval is by a designated emergency-approver (typically the on-call CISO / Head of IT). Post-incident review verifies the grant was justified.
  • Approver availability: deputies designated for each approver so that absence doesn't block legitimate requests; deputies are themselves named and limited to a deputy list (not "anyone can stand in").
Verify. Recent Tier-0 grants have two-person approval evidence. No self-approval events. Deputy list current. Emergency grants in last quarter (if any) have contemporaneous approval and post-incident review evidence.
NIST 800-53 AC-2(1) · CIS Controls 6.8 · SOC 2 CC6.1
5
Separation of duties at Tier-0
No single individual holds Tier-0 eligibility across functionally separated domains; cross-domain Tier-0 is a finding requiring explicit approval.

Tier-0 separation of duties is more constrained than the general SoD policy (IT-ID-WI-03 Step 6).

  • Functional domains at Tier-0: cloud identity (Entra Global Admin / Privileged Role Admin), on-prem identity (AD Enterprise / Domain / Schema Admin), security operations (SIEM admin, EDR tenant admin), audit / compliance (read-only across all). One individual eligible across two of these is a finding.
  • Cross-domain Tier-0 permitted only with explicit Privileged Access Authority approval, documented compensating controls (enhanced activity logging, frequent attestation), and time-bound duration (typically ≤6 months).
  • Detection: monthly cross-tabulation of Tier-0 eligibility across functional domains; matches flagged for review.
  • Common legitimate case: very small agencies where staffing genuinely requires one individual to hold multiple Tier-0 eligibilities; documented as exception with named approver and compensating controls.
  • Documented anti-pattern: individuals holding T0 eligibility "for redundancy" or "in case of emergency" without compensating controls; these get reviewed and removed.
Verify. Cross-domain T0 cross-tabulation report current. Identified matches either documented exceptions with compensating controls, or in remediation.
NIST 800-53 AC-5 · ISO 27001 A.5.3 · MITRE ATT&CK M1018
6
Track and minimise standing privileged assignments
Standing privileged assignment count is monitored monthly; target is the break-glass count only.
  • Monthly standing-privilege count: directory query for Tier-0 and Tier-1 roles assigned as active (not eligible); cross-referenced with break-glass list.
  • Target: count equals break-glass count (typically 2–4 accounts). Any other standing privilege is an exception requiring remediation.
  • Trending: month-over-month standing privilege count; upward trend triggers IT Ops review.
  • Standing-privilege exception register: per IT-GOV-SOP-01; named identities, named approvers, justifications, time-bound expiries.
  • Tier-0 standing privilege governance: even the break-glass accounts (which need standing privilege by design) are governed: hardware MFA, monitored, accessed only under documented break-glass scenarios.
  • Quarterly report to Risk Committee: standing privilege count, trend, exceptions, break-glass usage events.
Verify. Monthly standing-privilege count = break-glass count, or exceptions documented. Quarterly Risk Committee paper available. Break-glass usage events in last quarter (if any) post-event-reviewed.
NIST 800-53 AC-6(7) · ISO 27001 A.8.2
7
Quarterly eligibility review
Every privileged eligibility is re-examined quarterly by the Privileged Access Authority; unjustified eligibility is removed.

This is the privileged-specific subset of IT-ID-WI-02 recertification, with stricter rules.

  • Per-quarter the full list of T0 and T1 eligibilities is reviewed by the Privileged Access Authority.
  • Per-eligibility decision: certify (continue), revoke (remove), modify (adjust scope or duration). Bulk-approve forbidden.
  • Per-eligibility justification re-examined: is the original business reason still current? has the individual's role changed? is the duration still appropriate?
  • Activity correlation: identities with eligibility but zero activations in the last quarter are scrutinised — either the access isn't needed, or the user is going around the eligibility (which is its own problem). Decision: revoke or address the workflow gap.
  • Eligibility-vs-activity discrepancy reports: which eligibilities are active and unused, which activations come from individuals whose eligibility is about to expire, which approval-required activations are repeatedly approved — all signals informing the review.
  • Annual deeper review: in addition to quarterly, one quarterly review per year is enhanced — full justification re-attestation, manager re-confirmation, cross-domain SoD re-check.
  • Evidence retention: per-eligibility decision record with justification, decision, decider, timestamp; retained 3 years minimum.
Verify. Most recent quarterly review complete; decision records exist per eligibility; revocations executed in directory; annual deep review schedule on calendar.
NIST 800-53 AC-2(j) · CIS Controls 6.7 · ISO 27001 A.8.2
8
Privileged access request workflow
Requests for new privileged eligibility follow a documented workflow with capture of requestor, justification, approver, decision; the workflow is the audit trail.
  • Entry points: ServiceNow request catalogue, Entra IG entitlement-package request, equivalent in agencies without those tools. One entry point per agency; ad-hoc email / IM requests rejected and redirected.
  • Required fields: requestor (auto-from-identity), requested role, scope, justification (business reason), proposed duration, sponsoring manager.
  • Approval routing: per Step 4 (single approver for T1, two-person for T0).
  • Workflow states tracked: submitted, in-approval, approved, granted, expired, revoked. Each transition timestamped.
  • SLA: standard requests decided within 5 business days; expedited path for legitimate urgency (documented in justification).
  • Workflow is the audit trail: a properly captured request is the audit evidence; no separate logging needed beyond directory state changes which are platform-logged anyway.
  • Forbidden out-of-band grants: an admin who grants privileged eligibility outside the workflow (direct PIM modification, AD group add) triggers a high-priority alert; investigated as potential compromise or as procedural violation requiring backfill.
Verify. Sample 3 recent privileged grants; each has a workflow record with required fields and approval evidence. No out-of-band-grant alerts in last 30 days, or those that fired have been investigated and resolved.
NIST 800-53 AC-2(1) · ISO 27001 A.5.18
9
Tier-0 activation correlated with PAW usage
Tier-0 administrative actions originate from PAW devices (per IT-EP-SOP-PAW-01); non-PAW Tier-0 activity generates alerts.

The PAW is a trustworthy administrative source. Tier-0 activation outside that source defeats the model.

  • Device-of-origin captured at PIM activation: device identifier from Entra Conditional Access; correlated with PAW device registry (per IT-EP-SOP-PAW-01).
  • SIEM rule: Tier-0 activation from a non-PAW device fires an alert; SOC investigates as potential compromise or as procedural violation (Tier-0 admin using their standard workstation).
  • Conditional Access enforcement: where Entra Workload Identities Premium is licensed, Conditional Access policy can restrict Tier-0 activation to compliant PAW devices — making the rule preventive, not just detective. Configured per IT-CL-WI-01 Step 7.
  • Break-glass exception: break-glass accounts have their own access path with hardware MFA; PAW requirement adapted per the documented break-glass procedure.
  • Off-network legitimate cases (admin travelling, emergency response): documented exceptions; admin uses their PAW remotely; remote-PAW-access path documented and tested.
Verify. SIEM rule active; recent non-PAW T0 alerts investigated to resolution; PAW device registry current; Conditional Access enforcement applied where licensed.
NIST 800-53 AC-3(7) · MITRE ATT&CK M1026 · M1027
10
Tier-0 administrative activity to immutable SIEM
Tier-0 administrative actions are logged to the central SIEM with tamper-resistant retention; review monthly.
  • Log sources: Entra audit log (administrative actions), Entra sign-in log (activations, MFA events), AD security log (privileged-group changes, Tier-0 authentication), Azure activity log (subscription-level actions).
  • SIEM destination: central SIEM (QRadar currently, planned migration documented elsewhere); retention ≥1 year for Tier-0 activity (longer per regulatory requirement).
  • Immutability: SIEM retention configured so that Tier-0 events cannot be deleted by administrators of the source systems; segregation of duties between log producer and log custodian.
  • Detection rules: Tier-0 activation outside business hours, Tier-0 from new device, Tier-0 from new location, Tier-0 with anomalous duration, repeated Tier-0 activation by same user.
  • Monthly review: SOC reviews Tier-0 activity for the month; cross-references against approved change records and incident records; unexplained activity investigated.
  • Tier-0 logging gaps are themselves a finding: if a Tier-0 action happened but didn't log, the logging configuration is broken — investigated as high priority.
Verify. Recent T0 activity visible in SIEM; retention configured per target; detection rules active; monthly SOC review evidence available.
NIST 800-53 AU-2 · AU-12 · ISO 27001 A.8.15 · MITRE ATT&CK D3-PAM
Validation & verification

Validate the privileged identity governance programme.

Catalogue & eligibility

  • Privileged role catalogue current; T0/T1 roles and per-individual eligibility documented
  • Eligibility durations: T0 ≤12 months, T1 ≤18 months
  • Eligibility expiry alerts firing; expiries enforced (no silent extensions)
  • Standing-privilege count = break-glass count (or exceptions documented)

Approval & SoD

  • Tier-0 grants in last quarter have two-person approval evidence
  • No self-approval events
  • Deputy approver list current
  • Cross-domain T0 SoD check current; identified matches resolved or exception-tracked
  • Emergency grants (if any) have contemporaneous approval and post-incident review

Review & activity

  • Quarterly eligibility review complete; decision records exist; revocations applied
  • Eligibility-vs-activity correlation report reviewed (zero-activation eligibilities scrutinised)
  • Annual deep review on schedule
  • Privileged access request workflow active; out-of-band-grant alerts firing
  • Non-PAW T0 activation alerts firing; investigations to resolution
  • T0 activity in SIEM; retention per target; immutability validated; monthly SOC review evidence

Rollback & troubleshooting

Privileged identity governance changes are operationally consequential. Wrong revocation can lock out legitimate admins; wrong eligibility grant can over-empower. Stage carefully.

Common scenarios

  • Eligibility revoked through quarterly review, but the individual needed it — cause: reviewer judged based on incomplete information; individual is a legitimate ongoing user. Recovery: emergency re-grant through workflow (with documented urgency); investigate why the review didn't surface the legitimate need; improve activity-correlation reporting for next review.
  • Eligibility expired automatically with no warning reaching the individual — cause: alert routing failed; individual was on leave; manager change in last 30 days. Recovery: emergency grant; investigate alert routing; ensure manager change updates the alert distribution.
  • Two-person approval blocked a legitimate emergency grant — cause: second approver unavailable, deputy chain didn't resolve. Recovery: emergency-approver path used (documented in Step 4); post-grant review verifies legitimacy. Update deputy chain to handle the recurring case.
  • Cross-domain T0 holdings are exceeded due to one individual covering two functions during reorganisation — cause: transitional state during agency restructure. Recovery: documented time-bound exception with compensating controls (enhanced logging, monthly activity review); planned remediation by reorganisation end date.
  • Standing-privilege count exceeded break-glass count from a forgotten T0 service account — cause: a Tier-0-scoped service account not classified as such (per IT-ID-WI-04 Step 10). Recovery: re-classify; bring under privileged governance; either reduce scope or document as standing exception.
  • Non-PAW T0 alert misfired — cause: legitimate emergency response from on-call admin's standard device because PAW wasn't available. Recovery: post-event review of legitimacy; if legitimate, document and update the remote-PAW-access path so the case is handled correctly next time.
  • T0 logging gap discovered retrospectively — cause: log forwarder failed silently; SIEM index dropped events. Recovery: restore logging; back-fill where possible; gap period flagged for SOC retrospective review.

Rollback procedure

  1. Identify the affected eligibility / grant / revocation and the impact
  2. Apply the corrective change via the standard workflow (no out-of-band edits)
  3. Document the rollback as a discrete event in the audit trail
  4. Investigate root cause — was the original control too strict, too loose, or correctly applied?
  5. Adjust the relevant procedure if systemic; communicate; close the loop with affected individuals
Common errors & tips
  • Eligibility is the control. Activation is the action. Confusing the two is the most common conceptual error. Don't treat PIM activation as if it were governance — activation is the mechanism that uses the governance defined here.
  • Standing privilege is the failure mode. Every standing assignment is the absence of the control. Push everything possible to eligibility; the friction of activation is the control surface.
  • "Always-eligible" is standing privilege. If eligibility has no expiry, it never expires; if it never expires, it never gets re-justified; if it never gets re-justified, the access continues past its legitimate need. Time-bind eligibility itself.
  • Two-person approval at T0 catches social-engineering. An attacker who has compromised one approver still has to compromise a second; the second approver is selected for being independent of the first. This is the single highest-leverage control in privileged governance.
  • Self-approval is automatic. If your tooling allows self-approval, an attacker who has compromised one privileged identity can grant themselves more. Disable self-approval in the tool, not just in policy.
  • Cross-domain T0 is the lateral-movement freeway. An attacker who pivots through the cloud-identity admin role can do far more if that admin also holds AD Enterprise Admin. Keep them separate.
  • Quarterly review without activity correlation is hollow. Reviewing "should this person be eligible" without "are they actually using it" misses the most important signal: zero-activation eligibilities are either unneeded (revoke) or being routed around (workflow gap).
  • Out-of-band grants are how the workflow stops mattering. If admins can grant T0 eligibility by directly editing the directory, the request workflow is theatre. Detect, alert, investigate, backfill, prevent.
  • PAW is the trustworthy administrative source. T0 activity from non-PAW devices — even by the legitimate admin — defeats the model because compromise of the admin's standard workstation is now a T0 compromise.
  • T0 logging is non-negotiable. Every T0 action must be logged to immutable destination. Logging gaps are not "we'll fix it later" — they are forensic blind spots that incidents will inevitably fall into.
  • Break-glass is the controlled exception, not the convenient bypass. Break-glass accounts have standing privilege by design; they are the smallest possible set; their use is monitored; they are not a way to avoid PIM activation for routine work.
  • Emergency grants need a post-event review. The expedited path is necessary; without it, real emergencies get blocked. The post-event review is what keeps it from becoming a routine bypass — the urgency justification gets checked after the fact.
  • Privileged non-human identities deserve this treatment too. Per IT-ID-WI-04 Step 10. Service principal with Global Admin equivalent scope is a T0 identity even though no human signs in to it.
