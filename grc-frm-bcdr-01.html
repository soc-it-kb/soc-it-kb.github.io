GRC-FRM-BCDR-01 — Business Impact Assessment Worksheet

Template for conducting a Business Impact Assessment per GRC-BCDR-WI-01. Populated at agency level for each critical business process identified by the BIA. Output drives both BCP (continuity plans, RTOs) and DR (recovery tier classification).

Per-process worksheet 5 impact dimensions 6 time horizons Dependency map
How to use this template. Complete one worksheet per business process under assessment, following the procedure in GRC-BCDR-WI-01. Fields shown as [Fill] are placeholders the agency replaces with content. The BCP Coordinator owns the populated worksheet; the named Business Process Owner signs off in Section 9.

2 — Impact assessment

For each of the five dimensions, score the impact of process unavailability at each of the six time horizons using the rubric below. The methodology is in GRC-BCDR-SOP-01 §3.

ScoreDescription
5 — CatastrophicBeneficiary harm or death; existential financial / reputational damage; criminal liability; loss of operating licence
4 — MajorMaterial beneficiary harm; severe financial / reputational damage; regulatory sanction; significant data breach
3 — ModerateBeneficiary inconvenience; moderate financial / reputational impact; regulatory notification required; operational disruption requiring extensive recovery
2 — MinorLimited beneficiary impact; minor financial / reputational impact; manageable operational disruption
1 — NegligibleInternal-only impact; minimal financial / reputational consequence; routine operational handling

Important — beneficiary safety is the trump card. Any process with a beneficiary-safety score of 4 or 5 at any time horizon is Mission-critical (Tier 1) regardless of other dimensions.

3 — Recovery objectives

Derived from the impact assessment. Use the standard bands in GRC-BCDR-SOP-01 §4. Avoid “immediate” or “no downtime” — these are not RTOs.

Process criticality classification
[Mission-critical / Business-critical / Important / Standard]
Mission-critical if beneficiary safety score ≥ 4 at any time horizon; otherwise drive from the highest sustained score across dimensions.
RTO (Recovery Time Objective)
[≤ 4h / ≤ 24h / ≤ 72h / ≤ 7d]
The time within which the process must be restored to at least the MBCO.
RPO (Recovery Point Objective)
[≤ 15m / ≤ 4h / ≤ 24h / ≤ 7d]
Maximum tolerable data loss measured backwards from the disruption.
MTPD (Maximum Tolerable Period of Disruption)
[24h / 7d / 14d / 30d / other — with rationale]
Point beyond which the disruption causes unrecoverable damage. Must be longer than RTO with margin.
MBCO (Minimum Business Continuity Objective)
[Concrete description of the minimum acceptable service level during disruption — e.g. “50% normal volume via manual fallback”, “emergency-only operations with paper records”, “reduced feature set with workaround for X”]

Where this worksheet feeds next
  • Tier-1 / Tier-2 processes become targets for a Business Process Continuity Plan populated using GRC-FRM-BCP-01.
  • The dependency map (Section 4.1 IT services) is handed to the IT DR Coordinator to drive recovery tier classification per GRC-DR-WI-01.
  • Specific disruption risks (Section 6) with weak mitigation feed the agency’s risk register submissions per GRC-SOP-04.
  • Gaps (Section 7) are tracked through the appropriate route (risk register, DR remediation, exception register).
  • The consolidated BIA (all worksheets for the agency, with ranked critical processes list) is submitted to the BCDR Steering Committee per WI-01 step 9 and registered in the central BCDR portfolio inventory.
