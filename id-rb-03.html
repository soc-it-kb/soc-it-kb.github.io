ID-RB-03: Review Sign-In Logs for Account Compromise

Review authentication activity to identify indicators of account compromise — covering anomalous IPs, impossible travel, legacy authentication, MFA bypass, and Conditional Access outcomes.

🔐 Identity threats Tier 1 / 2 Investigation
📋 ID: ID-RB-03
·
👤 Author: Paulo Ferreira
·
📅 Published: 22 December 2025
·
🏷️ Category: Identity Threat
·
Active
Section 1
Purpose

Review authentication activity to identify indicators of account compromise following phishing, BEC, or credential theft.

Key anomalies to look for

Impossible travel

Sign-ins from geographically distant locations within unrealistic timeframes

Legacy auth

Basic auth or SMTP/POP/IMAP — bypasses MFA and Conditional Access

MFA bypass

Successful sign-in after MFA failures, or approval from unknown location

Unknown device

Sign-in from unregistered, non-compliant, or unfamiliar device

High-risk IP

Known VPN, TOR, proxy, or threat intel flagged IP address

Section 2
Required Access
📊 Entra ID sign-in logs 🔒 Conditional Access visibility
Section 3
Inputs Required
👤 Username / UPN 🕐 Incident timeframe
Section 4
Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Open the user's sign-in logs in Entra ID (Microsoft Entra admin center)
2
Filter logs by the incident timeframe — start slightly before the suspected compromise time
Focus primarily on successful sign-ins, not just failures — attackers who got in will show as successful
3
Review the following fields for each sign-in entry:
  • IP address — check reputation against threat intelligence
  • Geolocation — identify unexpected countries or cities
  • Device details — registered vs unregistered, compliant vs non-compliant
  • Authentication method — modern auth vs legacy auth
4
Identify anomalies using the reference cards above — flag any of the following:
  • Impossible travel between two sign-in locations
  • Legacy authentication protocol usage
  • MFA bypass or suspicious MFA approval
  • Sign-in from high-risk or anonymised IP
5
Review Conditional Access policy results — identify any policies that were bypassed or failed to apply
6
Correlate sign-in activity with mailbox activity (rule creation, forwarding) and endpoint telemetry
7
Record all anomalous entries, IP addresses, and findings in the incident ticket
Section 5
Expected Outcome

✅ Clear determination of compromise likelihood based on sign-in evidence
✅ Evidence to support escalation or closure decision
✅ Anomalous IPs and sign-in patterns documented as IOCs

Section 6
Validation Checks
  • No active suspicious sessions visible in the identity platform
  • MFA enforced and confirmed on the account
Section 7
Common Errors & Tips
Focus on successful sign-ins, not just failures — a failed attempt means the attacker didn't get in; a successful one means they did
Legacy authentication (SMTP, POP, IMAP, Basic Auth) is a frequent red flag — it bypasses MFA and Conditional Access entirely
Cross-reference IP addresses against threat intelligence — even a single high-risk IP in a successful sign-in warrants investigation
Section 8
Output Artefacts
Sign-in log export
Anomalous IP list
Conditional Access results
Compromise determination
