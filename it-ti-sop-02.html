IT-TI-SOP-02: Indicator of Compromise (IOC) Management

Defines how Indicators of Compromise are ingested, validated, enriched, actioned across security controls, and retired when no longer relevant.

Section 1
Purpose

This SOP defines the end-to-end process for managing Indicators of Compromise — from ingestion through validation, enrichment, deployment to controls, monitoring, and retirement. It ensures that only validated, relevant IOCs are deployed to security controls, and that stale or false-positive indicators are removed promptly.

⚠️ Deploying unvalidated IOCs to production controls can cause false positives, service disruption, and alert fatigue. All IOCs must be validated before deployment.

Section 3
IOC Lifecycle
1
Ingestion
Receive IOCs from approved sources — feeds, advisories, incident response, or manual submission. Log source, date, and context for every IOC.
2
Deduplication
Check for existing IOCs before processing. Remove duplicates. Update existing entries with new source or context rather than creating duplicates.
3
Validation
Verify the IOC is malicious and not a false positive. Cross-reference with VirusTotal, threat intel platforms, and internal telemetry. Assign confidence score: High / Medium / Low.
4
Enrichment
Add context — threat actor, malware family, campaign, first/last seen, MITRE ATT&CK mapping, affected sector. Richer context improves triage speed during incidents.
5
Prioritisation
Assess relevance to the organisation's environment and sector. High-confidence, relevant IOCs from active campaigns are prioritised for immediate deployment.
6
Deployment
Deploy to appropriate controls per IT-TI-WI-03. Document deployment targets, timestamps, and any exceptions. Follow change management for production control changes.
7
Monitoring
Monitor deployed IOCs for hits. Investigate any matches. Track false positive rate. Review IOC effectiveness periodically.
8
Retirement
Retire IOCs that are expired, superseded, or generating excessive false positives. Document retirement reason. Remove from controls within defined SLA.

Section 5
IOC Retention & Expiry
IOC typeDefault retentionReview trigger
IP addresses30 days active — review before renewalNo hits in 14 days, or false positives detected
Domains90 days active — review before renewalDomain resolves to legitimate service, or no hits in 30 days
File hashes12 months — low false positive riskHash confirmed benign by vendor, or no hits in 6 months
Email indicators60 days active — review before renewalPattern matching legitimate traffic
CVEsUntil patched or risk acceptedPatch applied, exception granted, or vendor advisory revised

⚠️ Stale IOCs — particularly IP addresses — generate false positives and alert fatigue. IP space is recycled frequently. Always review IP-based blocks before renewing past 30 days.
