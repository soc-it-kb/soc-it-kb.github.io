EM-WI-04: Remove Malicious Emails from Mailboxes

Tool-agnostic procedure for safely removing confirmed malicious emails from user mailboxes — preventing interaction, stopping credential harvesting or malware execution, and reducing dwell time.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a consistent, tool-agnostic procedure to safely remove confirmed malicious emails from user mailboxes — preventing interaction, stopping credential harvesting or malware execution, containing active campaigns, and reducing dwell time.

📌 Scope boundary: This WI does not authorise email removal. It defines how to remove emails correctly once approved by Tier-2 or via SOAR.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
🚫 Mandatory constraints
Do NOT remove emails without Tier-2 approval
Do NOT expand scope beyond what is explicitly approved
Do NOT notify users before removal unless instructed
Do NOT rely on subject line alone as the removal identifier

⚠️ Prefer search-based removal using Message-ID over subject-only searches. Subject lines are not unique and can match legitimate emails.

Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Validate Email Indicators
1
Review the approved indicators — Message-ID (preferred), or sender + subject + timestamp combination
2
Confirm indicators uniquely identify the malicious email and match the reported sample or gateway analysis
If ambiguity exists — pause and escalate before proceeding.
2
Identify Target Mailboxes
1
Confirm the approved scope — single user, defined list, or all mailboxes in tenant
2
Ensure the scope matches exactly what was approved — do not expand unilaterally
3
Execute Email Removal
1
Search for the malicious email using the approved indicators
2
Remove from all of the following locations:
  • Inbox
  • Junk / Spam folder
  • Any other folders where the message may have been moved
3
Confirm deletion completed successfully and record method, timestamp, and operator
4
Validate Removal
1
Re-run the search using the same indicators and confirm zero results remain across all target mailboxes and folders
2
If instances persist — do not retry repeatedly. Escalate to Tier-2.
5
Preserve Evidence
1
Ensure at least one copy of the email is retained in:
  • SOC evidence store
  • Secure designated mailbox
  • Exported sample per policy
2
Do NOT delete preserved evidence when cleaning up
6
Monitor for Follow-On Activity
1
Post-removal, monitor for:
  • Additional phishing reports from other users
  • User credential compromise indicators
  • Link click or attachment open events before removal
  • Similar emails with variant indicators
7
Document Actions
1
Update the incident record with indicators used, scope of mailboxes affected, removal timestamp, validation outcome, and any exceptions
2
Attach screenshots, logs, or admin confirmation where available
Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Malicious email removed from all approved mailboxes and folders
✅ Risk of user interaction reduced
✅ Evidence preserved — at least one copy retained
✅ Clear audit trail documented in the incident record

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Removing emails based on subject line only — subjects are not unique and can match legitimate messages
Expanding scope beyond the approved target list without Tier-2 authorisation
Forgetting Junk or Deleted folders — the email may have been auto-moved before removal
Failing to preserve at least one evidence copy before removal
Not validating that removal was successful — always re-run the search
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Email removal fails or results are partial after execution
Additional campaign variants are discovered during removal
Users report having interacted with the email before removal completed
Business-critical or legitimate mail appears to be impacted
Sender or domain appears to be widely used legitimate infrastructure
