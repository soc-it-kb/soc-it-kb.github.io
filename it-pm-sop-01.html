IT-PM-SOP-01: Patch Management — Core Standard Operating Procedure

Consistent, risk-based, and resilient approach to patch management across all systems, platforms, and environments — defining minimum requirements for identifying, prioritising, deploying, validating, and governing security patches.

Section 1
Purpose

This SOP defines a consistent, risk-based, and resilient approach to patch management across all systems, platforms, and environments. It establishes the minimum recommended requirements for identifying, prioritising, deploying, validating, and governing security patches to reduce exposure to known vulnerabilities and strengthen overall cyber resilience.

Section 2
Scope

Applies to all systems owned, operated, or managed by the institution: endpoints (workstations, laptops, mobiles), servers (physical and virtual), network and infrastructure devices, security platforms and tooling, business applications, and operating systems, firmware, and middleware. Applies across centrally, regionally, and locally managed environments regardless of maturity level.

Section 3
Patch Management Principles

🎯 Risk-based prioritisation

Patches prioritised based on exploitability, asset criticality, and exposure

🔄 Consistency over perfection

A repeatable baseline process preferred over ad-hoc patching

🛡️ Fail-safe execution

Patching must include rollback and recovery considerations

✅ Verification is mandatory

All patching activities require post-deployment validation

Section 4
Patch Prioritisation & SLAs

Critical

Actively exploited / CVSS ≥ 9
≤ 72 hours

High

CVSS 7–8.9
≤ 14 days

Medium

CVSS 4–6.9
≤ 30 days

Low

CVSS < 4
≤ 90 days

⚠️ Emergency patching may bypass standard change windows where justified by risk. See IT-PM-SOP-02 for emergency patching procedures.

Section 5
Patch Lifecycle
1
Patch Identification
Monitor vendor advisories, threat intelligence, and vulnerability scanning outputs for applicable patches.
2
Risk Assessment & Prioritisation
Assess exploitability, asset criticality, and exposure level. Assign SLA tier.
3
Deployment Planning
Define patch window, rollback plan, and owner. Coordinate with business-critical system owners.
4
Patch Deployment
Deploy using approved methods. Staged or phased rollouts recommended. Backups in place before deployment.
5
Validation & Verification
Confirm patch applied, system functionality intact, no unintended degradation.
6
Reporting & Governance Review
Monthly metrics review — compliance rates, SLA adherence, exception volumes, failure rates.
Section 6
Roles & Responsibilities
RoleResponsibility
Global Cyber Security TeamDefine patching standards, maintain SOP, oversee exceptions, monitor compliance
IT Operations (Global / Regional / Local)Execute patching, validate deployment, escalate failures
Asset / System OwnersConfirm business impact, approve windows, participate in exception decisions
Risk & Governance FunctionsReview and approve risk acceptances, ensure audit alignment
Section 7
Framework Alignment & Governance

Supports NIST CSF 2.0, CIS Critical Security Controls, and ISO/IEC 27001. SOP Owner: Global Cyber Security Team  ·  Review Frequency: Annual or following major incidents
