How to use this library

The TTX library complements the Exercise & Testing Programme methodology in GRC-BCDR-WI-02. The methodology covers how exercises are scoped, sequenced, and run; the library provides concrete scenarios to run.

  • Run as-is — the scenarios are populated with sufficient detail for a facilitator to deliver them with minimal preparation. Adapt the timing, role names, and country-specific details to your agency’s context.
  • Adapt — change the inject content, swap the named SaaS / region / process, or extend with additional injects. The template provides the structure.
  • Author new scenarios — for scenarios specific to your agency’s estate, use the template (GRC-FRM-TTX-01) and look at the populated scenarios for examples of depth and pacing.

Each scenario lists the documents it tests; you should bring those documents to the exercise. Inability to find or use them under exercise conditions is itself one of the most common findings.

Worked-example scenarios
GRC-TTX-01
Ransomware Affecting Clinical Systems
Ransomware renders clinical EHR unavailable during an active operating day. Tests joint IRP + BCP invocation, SOC recovery-threshold under clinical pressure, clean-up vs clean-tenant decision, regulator notification.
Mixed Cyber + BCDR 120 min
GRC-TTX-02
Entra Tenant Compromise
SOC detects attacker presence in Entra tenant including privileged role manipulation and unrecognised app registration. Tests in-tenant vs clean-tenant decision, break-glass readiness, Microsoft Support escalation, SOC + DR + Identity coordination.
Technical Cyber (identity) 120 min
GRC-TTX-03
Regional Disaster — Sustained Site Evacuation
Multi-day evacuation of a major operating site with secondary impacts on transport, telecoms, and staff. Tests BCP at scale, alternative-site readiness, degraded-comms fallback, multi-day CMT cadence, cross-agency coordination.
Executive BCDR (natural) 90 min
GRC-TTX-04
Critical SaaS Provider Sustained Failure
SaaS provider holding agency personal data discloses cyber incident and suspends customer access for 2+ weeks. Tests controller-side response, data-exposure assessment, 72-hour regulator notification, supplier-relationship decisions, cross-AKDN coordination.
Mixed BCDR + cyber 90 min
GRC-TTX-05
Azure Region Failure During Business-Critical Period
Azure declares major service event affecting primary region during peak operations. Tests per-workload failover runbook execution, cross-workload sequencing, region-scope confirmation, RPO-window data handling, failback decision.
Technical Cloud DR 90 min

Suggested rotation

The BCDR Exercise programme (per GRC-BCDR-WI-02) requires Tier-1 services / processes to be exercised annually. The scenarios above are not all run every year — a healthy rotation:

  • Annually rotate across scenario types — cyber, BCDR, hybrid — so the portfolio is exercised over a 2-3 year window
  • After any major change to a relevant document or capability, re-run the relevant scenario to validate the change
  • After a near-miss or real event, use the relevant scenario to extract additional learning beyond the AAR
  • Combine scenarios occasionally — run TTX-03 and TTX-04 together for a regional-disaster-that-causes-supplier-disruption multi-day exercise; this is realistic and tests sustained capability
