GRC-POL-03: General Supplier Security Assessment Policy

Minimum requirements for assessing suppliers that do not fall within the SaaS/Cloud or On-Prem Application Support assessment models.

Purpose
Purpose

This policy defines the minimum requirements for assessing suppliers that provide goods, products, licences, equipment, maintenance, professional services, or other services that do not fall within the SaaS/cloud or internally hosted application support supplier assessment models. The purpose of this policy is to ensure that broader supplier security, privacy, operational, and supply chain risks are identified and treated proportionately, even where the supplier does not host organisational data and does not directly administer internally hosted applications.

Scope
Scope
  • Hardware and infrastructure components
  • Accessories, peripherals, and end-user technology items
  • Software licences, resold software, and packaged products with no hosted service component
  • Maintenance or break-fix services with little or no system administration scope
  • Professional services, consultancy, implementation, or project support
  • Telecommunications, printing, logistics, facilities technology, physical equipment, and other non-IT or mixed services
  • Use where the supplier does not clearly fall under the SaaS/Cloud or On-Prem Application Support models
Policy statements
The Institution shall
  • Assess general suppliers using a proportionate, risk-based model
  • Identify whether the supplier handles Institutional information, accesses premises, connects equipment, or provides products that may introduce supply chain or security risk
  • Determine whether the supplier should remain under this model or be escalated to one of the more specific supplier assessment tracks
  • Record outcomes, conditions, and reassessment requirements where appropriate
  • Require higher scrutiny where the supplier may handle sensitive information, connect to networks, access restricted areas, or introduce technology into the environment
Minimum assessment areas
At a minimum, the assessment shall consider
  • Supplier profile and service type
  • Information handling and confidentiality
  • Delivery, logistics, and supply chain assurance
  • Site access and physical security implications
  • Product integrity, licensing, and provenance
  • Vulnerability, patching, and security update expectations where technology products are supplied
  • Subcontractors and third-party dependencies
  • Incident notification and issue escalation
  • Contract end, returns, disposal, and residual data considerations where relevant
