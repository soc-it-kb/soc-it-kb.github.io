GRC-POL-01: Supplier Security Assessment Policy

Minimum requirements for assessing the security, privacy, resilience, and governance capability of all in-scope suppliers.

Purpose
Purpose

This policy defines the minimum requirements for assessing the security, privacy, resilience, and governance capability of suppliers that provide services, systems, or processing activities to the Institution. The purpose of the policy is to ensure that supplier risk is identified, assessed, treated, and governed in a consistent and proportionate manner before onboarding, during contract life, and when material change occurs.

Scope
Scope
  • Suppliers that process organisational or personal data
  • Suppliers providing SaaS, hosted, managed, or outsourced services
  • Suppliers that connect to organisational systems or networks
  • Suppliers with remote, logical, physical, or privileged access
  • Suppliers that support business-critical processes or regulated activities
  • Applies to new suppliers, contract renewals, material service changes, and periodic reassessments
Policy statements
The Institution shall
  • Perform supplier security assessments before onboarding in-scope suppliers
  • Apply a proportionate, risk-based approach based on service criticality, data sensitivity, and access profile
  • Require suppliers to provide accurate responses and supporting evidence where appropriate
  • Review supplier security, privacy, resilience, and third-party dependency controls before approval
  • Maintain records of assessment outcomes, conditions, and residual risks
  • Require reassessment at defined intervals or on material change
  • Escalate material control deficiencies for risk treatment or formal risk acceptance
Minimum assessment areas
At a minimum, the assessment shall consider
  • Service scope and supplier role
  • Governance and assurance
  • Identity and access management
  • Privacy and data protection
  • Encryption and secrets management
  • Logging and monitoring
  • Vulnerability and patch management
  • Secure development where applicable
  • Incident response and breach handling
  • Resilience, backup, and disaster recovery
  • Sub-processors and supply chain dependencies
  • Contractual safeguards and exit arrangements

Compliance
Policy Compliance

Non-compliance with this policy shall be recorded, risk-assessed, and escalated through the relevant governance route.
