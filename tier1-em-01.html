Tier-1 Quick Checklist

EM-01: Phishing

Use this checklist to triage a reported or detected phishing email. The key question is whether the user interacted — that determines your escalation path.

📧 Email threats Tier 1 Published 20 Dec 2025
🔔 When to use this checklist
User-reported phishing email / email gateway alert / MDR escalation involving a suspicious or confirmed phishing message.

Step 3 — Apply decision logic
✅
No interaction + low risk — Close as P3. Preserve the email and document findings.
⚠️
Clicked but no credentials entered — Escalate to Tier-2 with interaction evidence attached.
🔴
Credentials entered OR attachment opened — Escalate to Tier-3 immediately. Do not wait.
Step 4 — Mandatory actions
Preserve the original email — do not delete or move it
Capture user interaction confirmation — ask the user directly what they did
Search for other recipients of the same email
Attach the email evidence and user confirmation to the incident ticket
Do NOT delete the email before evidence is captured
⚠️ Common Tier-1 mistakes to avoid
Closing the ticket without confirming whether the user interacted with the email
Deleting the email before capturing evidence — always preserve first
Treating QR code phishing as low risk — QR codes bypass most URL scanning and are high risk
Not escalating when credentials were entered — this requires immediate Tier-3 response
💡
Analyst Tip
If a phishing email is convincing enough to fool one user, assume others may also have received and interacted with it — always search for additional recipients before closing.
