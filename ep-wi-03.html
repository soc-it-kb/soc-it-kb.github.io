EP-WI-03: Remove Malware Artefacts

Tool-agnostic procedure for safely removing confirmed malware artefacts from an isolated endpoint — eradicating malicious files, processes, and persistence mechanisms once approved.

💻 Endpoint threats Tier 1 / 2 Requires approval Eradication action

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a consistent, tool-agnostic procedure to safely remove confirmed malware artefacts — eradicating malicious files, processes, and configurations, preventing reinfection or persistence, and restoring endpoint integrity following containment.

📌 Scope boundary: This WI executes eradication only after approval. It does not authorise containment or rebuild decisions. Follow with EP-WI-04 to validate recovery readiness.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
🚫 Mandatory constraints
Do NOT proceed unless endpoint is confirmed isolated (EP-WI-02)
Do NOT remove artefacts on an uncontained endpoint
Do NOT remove OS or system files without Tier-2 approval
Do NOT proceed on servers or critical systems without Tier-2 oversight
Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Validate Endpoint & Artefact Scope
1
Confirm endpoint identity matches the incident. Review the approved artefact list — files, processes, and persistence mechanisms. If ambiguity exists — pause and escalate.
2
Terminate Malicious Processes (If Required)
1
Identify and terminate running malicious processes only if approved. Confirm they do not automatically restart.
If processes respawn — stop all action and escalate. Respawning indicates active persistence.
3
Remove Malicious Files
1
Remove approved malicious files. Verify correct file paths — no wildcard or bulk deletion. Confirm files are no longer present.
If file removal fails — do not force delete. Escalate to Tier-2.
4
Remove Persistence Mechanisms (If In Scope)

If approved artefacts include persistence mechanisms, remove all applicable locations:

Scheduled tasks Services Registry autoruns Startup folder entries WMI subscriptions Login scripts

⚠️ If persistence reappears after removal — escalate immediately. Do not continue removal blindly — the endpoint may be reinfected or a second persistence mechanism exists.

5
Validate System State
1
Confirm malicious files are absent, processes are no longer running, and persistence entries are removed. Perform a targeted re-scan if available.
Do NOT reconnect the endpoint to the network yet — proceed to EP-WI-04 for recovery validation first.
6
Preserve Evidence & Document
1
Preserve copies of removed artefacts — hashes, file metadata, logs, and screenshots. Do not delete preserved evidence.
2
Notify Tier-2 that eradication is complete and await instruction for reconnection, rebuild, or extended monitoring. Update the incident record with artefacts removed, timestamps, and validation outcome.
Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Confirmed malware artefacts removed
✅ Persistence mechanisms eliminated (if in scope)
✅ Endpoint remains contained pending Tier-2 instruction
✅ Evidence preserved and audit trail documented

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Removing files without first terminating associated processes — files in use cannot be fully removed
Deleting artefacts without documented Tier-2 approval
Forgetting to check all persistence locations — one missed mechanism causes reinfection
Reconnecting the endpoint before recovery validation is complete (EP-WI-04)
Removing artefacts on servers or critical systems without Tier-2 oversight
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Artefacts reappear after removal — active persistence or reinfection
New malicious artefacts are discovered during removal
Endpoint shows signs of ransomware or widespread compromise
Removal impacts system stability
Endpoint is critical infrastructure or a server
