IT-TI-SOP-03: Threat Intelligence — Vulnerability & Patch Prioritisation

Defines how threat intelligence context is applied to vulnerability and patch prioritisation decisions — ensuring that actively exploited vulnerabilities are treated with urgency regardless of CVSS score alone.

Section 1
Purpose

This SOP defines how threat intelligence is applied to vulnerability and patch prioritisation, ensuring that active exploitation status, threat actor targeting, and campaign intelligence override raw CVSS scoring when making remediation decisions. A Medium CVSS vulnerability being actively exploited in the wild demands faster action than a Critical CVSS vulnerability with no known exploitation.

📌 CVSS scores measure theoretical severity. Threat intelligence tells you whether attackers are actually using a vulnerability right now, against organisations like yours. Both are needed — but TI context takes priority when they conflict.

Section 4
TI Enrichment Process for Vulnerabilities
1
Check CISA KEV
For every High and Critical vulnerability — check the CISA KEV catalogue first. If listed, escalate to emergency SLA immediately regardless of other factors.
2
Check vendor and commercial TI
Review vendor advisories and commercial TI feeds for exploitation confirmation, PoC availability, and campaign targeting. Note any active threat actors exploiting the vulnerability.
3
Check EPSS score
Retrieve EPSS score from first.org/epss. High EPSS (≥ 0.5) combined with High CVSS should escalate the remediation timeline.
4
Assess asset exposure
Map vulnerability to affected assets. Internet-facing assets, Tier-0/1 systems, and identity infrastructure are always higher priority regardless of other scoring.
5
Apply TI-adjusted priority
Apply the TI-adjusted priority from the table above. Update the vulnerability tracking record with the enriched priority and justification.
6
Communicate to IT Operations
Notify IT Operations of any SLA escalation with TI justification. Emergency escalations follow IT-PM-SOP-02. Standard escalations follow IT-VM-SOP-01.
Section 5
Compensating Controls When Patching Cannot Be Immediate
  • Network segmentation or isolation of affected systems
  • Temporary service or feature disablement
  • WAF or IPS rule to block known exploit patterns
  • Enhanced monitoring and alerting on affected assets
  • Access restriction — reduce exposure surface while patch is prepared

📌 Compensating controls buy time but do not replace patching. Document all compensating controls and set a hard deadline for patch application. Follow IT-PM-SOP-03 for formal exception handling.
