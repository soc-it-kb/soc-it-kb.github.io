NW-PB-04: Data Exfiltration Activity

Standardised framework for detecting, investigating, and responding to suspected or confirmed data exfiltration — focusing on damage limitation, legal and regulatory awareness, and rapid containment.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a standardised decision and response framework for detecting, investigating, and responding to suspected or confirmed data exfiltration, where sensitive data is transferred outside the organisation without authorisation.

This playbook focuses on damage limitation, legal and regulatory awareness, and rapid containment — as exfiltration represents a material business and reputational risk.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • Large or abnormal outbound data transfers
  • Data movement detected by firewalls, NDR, proxy, CASB, or DLP
  • MDR escalations for suspected exfiltration
  • Endpoint or identity investigations that reveal outbound data staging
Exfiltration channels
DNS tunnelling HTTPS upload Cloud storage HTTP FTP / SFTP Custom protocol

❌ Out of scope

  • Legitimate bulk data transfers (approved backups, migrations)
  • Internal data movement without external destination
  • Denial of Service without data loss → NW-PB-05
Section 3
Trigger Conditions
  • NDR flags large or anomalous outbound data flows
  • Proxy or firewall logs show sustained high-volume uploads
  • CASB or DLP alerts indicate sensitive data leaving the tenant
  • MDR escalates suspected data exfiltration
  • Endpoint or identity investigation reveals outbound data staging activity
Section 4
Initial Validation (Decision Logic)

Validate whether the activity could be legitimate before escalating:

  • Is the destination an approved cloud or business partner service?
  • Is the user or system authorised to perform bulk data transfers?
  • Does the timing align with a known project, backup, or migration window?
  • Is the data type consistent with the system's normal role and function?

If YES — legitimate confirmed → Document and close as Authorised Data Transfer
If NO or UNCLEAR → Proceed with exfiltration assessment (Section 5)

Section 5
Exfiltration Assessment

Assess confidence, method, and potential impact. Presence of any indicator = Suspected Exfiltration:

Sustained outbound data transfer over an extended period
Use of an uncommon or newly registered destination
Data transfer directly follows a confirmed endpoint compromise
Encryption or obfuscation of the outbound payload
Repeated retries or throttled uploads suggesting automation
Section 6
Severity & Escalation Criteria

Escalate to High Severity if any of the following apply:

🔴 Sensitive or regulated data is involved (PII, financial, health)
🔴 Exfiltration is confirmed ongoing right now
🔴 Privileged user or critical server is the source
🔴 Exfiltration follows ransomware, C2, or lateral movement activity
🔴 MDR confirms attacker-controlled data transfer

Notify immediately

Response Strategy

Execute the following actions carefully — balancing speed of containment with evidence preservation:

Section 9
Containment Decision Logic

Choose the appropriate containment approach based on impact certainty and legal considerations:

🔴 Immediate blocking / isolation

  • Exfiltration is actively ongoing right now
  • Highly sensitive or regulated data is involved
  • Clear attacker-controlled destination confirmed

🟡 Coordinated containment

  • Blocking may destroy forensic evidence needed for legal purposes
  • Legal or regulatory guidance required before acting
  • Exfiltration already completed — focus shifts to impact assessment

All containment decisions must be logged and approved according to IR governance. Consult Legal before blocking if regulatory notification is likely.

Section 10
Scope Expansion Logic

Scope expansion is mandatory for all NW-PB-04 incidents:

Identification actions

  • Identify all systems communicating with the exfiltration destination
  • Review historical outbound traffic for earlier staging patterns
  • Identify any additional data staging or transfer attempts

Correlation actions

  • Correlate with endpoint compromise timelines (EP-PB-01 → EP-PB-04)
  • Correlate with identity anomalies (ID-PB-01 → ID-PB-06)
  • Cross-reference with open MDR incidents and C2 activity
Section 11
Communication & Coordination
  • Engage Legal, Compliance, and the DPO early — as soon as data exposure is suspected
  • Restrict all communications to need-to-know personnel
  • Align all actions with MDR and the Incident Command structure
  • Do not make any external disclosure without explicit executive approval
Section 12
Closure Criteria

The incident may be closed when all of the following are confirmed:

  • All exfiltration paths blocked or fully neutralised
  • Source systems remediated or rebuilt
  • Credentials reset where exposure was suspected
  • No further abnormal outbound transfers observed
  • Impact assessment completed and documented
  • Required regulatory or legal notifications initiated (if applicable)
Section 13
Evidence to Capture
Source and destination IPs / domains
Volume and duration of data transfer
Protocols and ports used
File names or data types (where visible)
Endpoint and identity correlation evidence
Timeline of detection and containment
Section 14
Post-Incident Actions
  • Review egress filtering and proxy controls
  • Improve DLP and CASB coverage and tuning
  • Restrict outbound access where not operationally required
  • Enhance monitoring for bulk and anomalous transfers
  • Feed indicators into threat hunting use cases
  • Update data handling and security awareness guidance
