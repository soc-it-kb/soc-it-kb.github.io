GRC-FRM-TTX-01 — Tabletop Exercise Scenario Template

Reusable template for authoring tabletop exercise scenarios. Used by the BCP Coordinator, IT DR Coordinator, SOC Lead, or Exercise Director to develop scenarios beyond the worked examples in the TTX library. Populated scenarios are exercised under GRC-BCDR-WI-02.

Scenario design Injects & decisions Multi-audience Reusable
How to use this template. Tabletop exercises are facilitated discussions, not live drills. Participants work through a scripted scenario, responding to a sequence of injects (new pieces of information that change the situation), making decisions, and reflecting on what worked. This template structures one such scenario. Worked examples populated for common situations are at GRC-TTX library (GRC-TTX-01 onwards). Fields shown as [Fill] are placeholders.

4 — Ground rules

Stated at the start of the session by the facilitator. Adapt as needed; defaults below.

  • This is a no-fault learning environment. Findings are about the system and the process, not individuals.
  • Participants respond as themselves in their real role — not as how they wish they would respond, and not as how someone else might.
  • If a participant does not know the answer, that itself is a valid finding.
  • Participants may reference real documents (this is a learning event, not a memory test). Inaccessible documents during the exercise are themselves a finding.
  • The facilitator may pause or adjust the scenario to keep discussion productive.
  • Content of the scenario and the discussion is confidential to the exercise and the AAR distribution list.

8 — Evaluation criteria

How the exercise team judges whether the scenario achieved its objectives. Used in the AAR.

  • Objective 1: [Criterion — e.g. “Invocation decision made within 30 minutes of T+0 with documented basis”]
  • Objective 2: [Criterion]
  • Objective 3: [Criterion]
  • Cross-cutting: were named documents (plans, runbooks, contact lists) accessed and consulted during the exercise? If not, why not?
  • Cross-cutting: were communications consistent with what plans specify, and did fallback channels work?
9 — After-action review framework

Conducted within 14 days of the exercise per GRC-BCDR-WI-02. The exercise debrief at the end of the session is the start of the AAR, not the entirety.

  • What happened (factual recap, no commentary)
  • What was supposed to happen (per documented plans / runbooks / playbooks)
  • Where the two diverged, and why (root cause, not symptom)
  • What worked well (preserve)
  • What did not work (treat — risk register / document update / training need / capability gap)
  • Specific changes to plans, runbooks, contacts (with owner and date)
  • Distribution of the AAR (BCDR Steering Committee, agency CEO, IT DR Coordinator, affected role-holders)

How this template fits with the rest of BCDR
  • Methodology — how exercises are scoped, sequenced, and run lives in GRC-BCDR-WI-02. This template is the artefact that procedure produces.
  • Worked examples — populated scenarios for common situations live in the TTX library (GRC-TTX-01 onwards). Use them directly, or use them as patterns for your own.
  • Real invocations count against the exercise cadence per WI-02. A real ransomware response within the year may substitute for that year’s ransomware tabletop — though doing both is normally healthier.
  • AAR findings feed: plan updates per FRM-BCP-01, runbook updates per FRM-DR-01, BIA refresh per FRM-BCDR-01, the risk register where capability gaps are surfaced, and this template if scenario gaps are found.
