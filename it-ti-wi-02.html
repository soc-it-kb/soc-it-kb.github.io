IT-TI-WI-02: IOC Ingestion & Validation

Step-by-step instructions for ingesting, deduplicating, validating, and scoring Indicators of Compromise before they are considered for deployment to security controls.

Prerequisites
IOC from approved source, incident, or analyst submission Access to validation tools (VirusTotal, MISP, threat intel platform) IOC tracking register or TI platform
Step-by-step instructions
1
Receive IOC — note source, type (IP/domain/hash/email/TTP), and any available context
2
Check for duplicates in the IOC register — if already present, update context and last-seen date rather than creating a new entry
3
Validate the IOC using VirusTotal or your TI platform — check detection ratio, first/last seen, and community comments
4
Cross-reference with at least one additional source — a second corroborating source increases confidence significantly
5
Check if the IOC overlaps with known legitimate services (e.g. CDN IPs, shared hosting) — these require extra caution before blocking
6
Assign confidence score: High (multiple trusted sources, recent, confirmed malicious), Medium (single source, plausible context), Low (unverified or aged)
7
Enrich with available context: threat actor, malware family, campaign name, MITRE ATT&CK technique, affected sector, first/last seen
8
Set expiry date based on IOC type — refer to IT-TI-SOP-02 retention table
9
Record in IOC register with all metadata. Mark as Validated and ready for prioritisation
Validation & verification
IOC validated against at least one external source
Confidence score assigned and documented
Expiry date set
Duplicate check completed
Context and enrichment recorded in IOC register
Common errors & tips
Never deploy a Low confidence IOC to blocking controls — it will generate false positives and erode trust in the TI programme
IP addresses shared across CDNs (Cloudflare, Akamai, Fastly) are very high false positive risk — always check before blocking
VirusTotal is a starting point, not the final answer — a 1/70 detection on VirusTotal for a domain could still be a true positive if the context is right
Always record why you assigned a confidence score — future analysts need to understand the reasoning
NIST CSF 2.0 alignment
ID.RA — Risk Assessment: IOC validation DE.AE — Adverse Event Analysis: indicator quality
