IT-GOV-WI-VM-02: Vulnerability Triage & Prioritisation

Ensure vulnerabilities are accurately assessed, enriched, and prioritised based on risk — not raw severity score alone.

🔍 Vulnerability Management IT operations Published 13 January 2026

👤 Author: Paulo Ferreira

📅 Published: 13 January 2026

Vulnerability Management Procedure
Required access
Vulnerability findings from scan output Asset criticality data Threat intelligence and exploitation context Ticketing or tracking system
Step-by-step instructions
1
Initial Review
Remove obvious false positives
Group duplicate findings across assets
2
Enrich Vulnerabilities
Confirm CVE reference and CVSS score
Identify asset criticality for each affected system
Determine exposure level — external or internal
Check known exploitation status — cross-reference CISA KEV
3
Assign Risk Priority
Override CVSS where active exploitation is confirmed — treat as Critical regardless of score
Apply Critical / High / Medium / Low classification
4
Create Remediation Tickets
Assign remediation owner
Define SLA based on severity classification
Link to change or patch reference where applicable
Expected outcome
Prioritised vulnerability list with risk classification
Clear remediation ownership assigned
Risk-aligned remediation plan with SLAs defined
Common errors & tips
Do not rely solely on CVSS score — a Medium CVE on an internet-facing critical system may warrant Critical priority
Always escalate vulnerabilities with confirmed exploitation in the wild immediately
Suppress recurring false positives with documented justification — do not leave them creating noise
