GRC-SOP-03: General Supplier Security Assessment Procedure

Steps for initiating, reviewing, scoring, approving, and tracking security assessments for suppliers outside the SaaS and on-prem application support models.

Procedure triggers
When to initiate this procedure
Onboarding a new general supplier Purchasing technology hardware, accessories, or packaged software Procuring non-hosted software licences Engaging a supplier for professional services, implementation, or project support Renewing an existing in-scope supplier Introducing a supplier with site access, information handling responsibilities, or product integration implications Carrying out a scheduled reassessment
Procedure steps
Step-by-step process
1
Establish Supplier Profile
Record supplier name, product/service type, business owner, criticality, data handling, site access requirement, network connectivity, subcontractor use, and whether a higher-risk model applies
2
Triage Assessment Track
Determine whether the supplier should remain under the general supplier model or be redirected to the cloud/SaaS or on-prem application support assessment model
3
Issue Questionnaire
Issue GRC-FRM-10 — General Supplier Security Assessment Questionnaire to the supplier
4
Review Responses and Evidence
Review responses for completeness, consistency, and suitability for the product or service being supplied
5
Identify Gaps and Clarifications
Request clarification where responses are vague, contradictory, or indicate higher-risk features
6
Complete Scored Assessment
Complete GRC-FRM-11 — General Supplier Scored Assessment Record
7
Determine Outcome
Recommend outcome based on scored assessment — see outcomes section below
8
Record Conditions and Follow-Up
Where needed, record contractual requirements, product security conditions, delivery and site controls, disposal/return requirements, and reassessment dates
Assessment outcomes
Permitted Outcomes
✅ Approved
⚠️ Approved with Conditions
🟠 Escalate for Further Assessment
🔴 Escalate for Risk Acceptance
🚫 Not Approved
Mandatory escalation criteria
Escalate if any of the following apply
The supplier hosts, processes, or stores organisational data in its own environment
The supplier requires privileged or administrative access to organisational systems
The supplier provides remote support into production systems
The supplier's products connect directly to organisational networks with material cyber exposure
The supplier cannot explain supply chain provenance or authenticity for critical products
The supplier may access restricted sites, sensitive information, or physical security systems without adequate controls
The supplier uses subcontractors with unclear oversight for sensitive services
Evidence expectations
Evidence may include
Corporate security policy summary
Confidentiality or NDA terms
Product authenticity or authorised reseller confirmation
Software licensing documentation
Patch and security advisory process descriptions
Delivery chain controls or logistics assurance summaries
Site access and engineer conduct requirements
Disposal, return, and residual data handling commitments

Records & maintenance
Records Management & Review

Assessment records shall be retained in the appropriate register in accordance with internal retention requirements. This procedure shall be reviewed annually or after material changes to supplier assessment requirements.
