IT-SRV-WI-Win-01: Local Administrator & Privileged Access Hardening

Reduce the risk of credential compromise and lateral movement by tightly controlling administrative access on Windows servers.

🖥️ Server hardening Windows Server IT operations
🔧 WI ID: IT-SRV-WI-Win-01
·
👤 Author: Paulo Ferreira
·
📅 Published: 12 January 2026
·
IT procedure
Prerequisites
Administrative access to server or management platform Approved privileged access model (tiered admin, PAM, break-glass) Server joined to the domain where applicable
Step-by-step instructions
1
Enumerate all members of the local Administrators group
2
Remove all non-approved accounts
3
Ensure no shared local administrator accounts exist
4
Verify domain admin usage is restricted and monitored
5
Confirm service accounts are not members of privileged groups unless required
6
Apply approved PAM or credential protection mechanisms
Validation & verification
Local Administrators group contains only approved accounts
Privileged access events are logged
No persistent elevation for non-admin users
Rollback & troubleshooting
Use approved break-glass credentials if access is lost
Escalate to Regional IT if access cannot be restored
Common errors & tips
Avoid adding accounts for temporary troubleshooting
Never use personal accounts for services
