CL-PB-01: Cloud Control Plane / Tenant Compromise

End-to-end response for suspected compromise of cloud administrative plane, tenant configuration, subscription ownership, app registrations, automation credentials, or privileged cloud identities.

Section 1
Purpose

End-to-end response for suspected compromise of cloud administrative plane, tenant configuration, subscription ownership, app registrations, automation credentials, or privileged cloud identities.

This playbook provides a consistent incident lifecycle for cloud detection, containment, evidence preservation, recovery, and lessons learned.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • Privileged role assignment abuse
  • Cloud tenant or subscription ownership compromise
  • App registration, service principal, or automation credential abuse
  • Unauthorised federation, policy, or conditional access changes
  • Mass resource modification or logging tampering

❌ Out of scope

  • Public storage or exposure without control-plane abuse → CL-PB-02 / CL-PB-04
  • Single workload compromise without admin-plane impact → CL-PB-03
  • Identity-only account compromise with no cloud admin action → ID playbooks
Section 3
Trigger Conditions

Initiate this playbook when any of the following occur:

  • Unexpected privileged role assignment or new admin principal
  • Suspicious policy, federation, conditional access, or subscription ownership change
  • Broad app consent or new service principal credential with elevated access
  • Anomalous administrative API activity or mass resource modification
  • Evidence of logging tampering, disabled controls, or unknown break-glass activity
Section 4
Initial Validation (Decision Logic)

Validate whether the activity is expected, authorised, and business justified:

  • Is the initiating principal known, authorised, and expected for this action?
  • Is there an approved change, deployment, or maintenance record?
  • Does the source IP, device, user agent, and authentication context match normal admin behaviour?
  • Were multiple tenants, subscriptions, or privileged objects touched in a short period?

If a legitimate change or accepted exposure is confirmed, document the evidence and close as benign or approved exception. If unauthorised, suspicious, or unclear, continue with the assessment and escalation sections below.

Section 5
Cloud Control Plane Compromise Assessment
  • Identify the initiating principal, target resources, action set, and first-seen timestamp.
  • Determine whether privilege escalation, persistence, or cross-tenant access exists.
  • Review adjacent subscriptions, automation accounts, service principals, secrets, key vaults, CI/CD integrations, and break-glass access.
  • Preserve audit logs, sign-in records, policy state, role assignment state, and before/after configuration evidence.
Section 6
Severity & Escalation Criteria

Treat as Sev 1 / Critical when privileged control-plane abuse, unauthorised federation, logging tampering, broad data access, production outage, or unknown admin activity is confirmed. Treat as High when suspicious privileged activity is plausible but scope is limited or not yet confirmed.

Section 7
Response Strategy
Preserve evidence before destructive remediation.Required
Revoke risky sessions and disable or quarantine suspect principals.Required
Remove unauthorised privileged assignments, malicious app registrations, rogue credentials, and persistence paths.Required
Protect break-glass access and rebuild trusted administrative state.Required
Rotate impacted credentials and validate conditional access, logging, and policy guardrails.Required

Section 9
Containment Decision Logic

Containment actions

  • Revoke sessions and invalidate tokens for suspect identities.
  • Disable or restrict compromised human and non-human principals.
  • Remove unauthorised role assignments, app grants, federation changes, and credentials.
  • Protect break-glass access and preserve admin audit evidence.

Approval boundary

  • Use read-only validation before changing production state where possible.
  • Obtain incident lead or authorised owner approval for disruptive changes.
  • Record every containment action in the incident or change ticket.
Section 10
Scope Expansion Logic

Expand investigation to

  • Review adjacent tenants and subscriptions.
  • Review service principals, app registrations, automation accounts, secrets, CI/CD integrations, and key vaults.
  • Check for logging tampering, policy changes, and new privileged paths.
  • Validate no unknown admin route remains.

Stop expansion when

  • No related risky configuration, identity, workload, or data access remains.
  • Affected owner confirms business context and residual risk is accepted or removed.
  • Monitoring validates no recurrence after containment.
Section 11
Communication & Coordination
Incident Lead
Owns severity, coordination, and closure
Cloud / Platform Owner
Approves production-impacting actions
Business Owner
Confirms service and data impact
GRC / Legal / Privacy
Engage if data, regulatory, or notification risk exists
Section 12
Closure Criteria
  • Root cause and affected resources are documented.
  • Containment, recovery, and validation actions are complete or tracked as accepted risk.
  • Evidence package is attached to the incident record.
  • Preventive controls or remediation actions are assigned with owners and due dates.

Section 14
Post-Incident Actions
  • Update cloud security baselines, guardrails, and monitoring rules.
  • Correct IaC, policy, ownership, tagging, or review gaps.
  • Feed lessons learned into awareness, engineering backlog, and governance registers.
  • Review whether related runbooks or work instructions require updates.
