Identity Governance & Lifecycle

Lifecycle controls for every identity in the estate: human identities (joiners, movers, leavers) and non-human identities (service accounts, managed identities, service principals, certificates). Covers hybrid environments — Entra ID and on-premises Active Directory — with role design, periodic recertification, and privileged identity governance. Sits above the tenant-level identity controls in IT-CL-WI-01 (which configure the technical platform) by governing who should have access in the first place.

📌 About this domain. Identity governance is the policy and lifecycle layer above the technical identity platform. IT-CL-WI-01 configures Conditional Access, MFA, PIM activation, and break-glass — the how of tenant identity. This domain answers who should have access, for how long, reviewed by whom, and retired when. Together they form the complete identity control surface.
Scope

This domain covers the governance and lifecycle of identities across the estate:

  • Human identities — employee, contractor, student, alumni, guest, and external collaborator identities in both Entra ID and on-premises Active Directory (where the agency operates AD). JML lifecycle (Joiner / Mover / Leaver), access recertification, role assignment, and entitlement management.
  • Non-human identities — service accounts on-premises, Entra service principals and app registrations, system-assigned and user-assigned managed identities in Azure, certificate-based identities, and workload identities. These are increasingly the highest-volume identity class and the most frequently neglected.
  • Hybrid identity — identities synchronised from on-prem AD to Entra ID via Entra Connect, cloud-only Entra identities, and AD-only identities (where on-prem-only resources still depend on them). The governance is unified even where the technical platforms differ.
  • Privileged identities — the eligibility layer above PIM activation: who is eligible to activate which roles, why, for how long, reviewed by whom. The technical activation mechanism (PIM, just-in-time access) lives in IT-CL-WI-01 and IT-SI-WI-04; this domain governs the eligibility itself.

Out of scope:

  • Tenant-level identity platform configuration — Conditional Access policies, PIM activation rules, MFA enforcement, named locations, break-glass accounts, Identity Protection risk policies, SSPR — covered by IT-CL-WI-01.
  • PAM platform hardening — vaulting, session recording, credential rotation mechanics — covered by IT-SI-WI-04.
  • PAW (Privileged Access Workstation) build and lifecycle — covered by IT-EP-SOP-PAW-01 and the PAW Work Instructions.
  • Tier-0 AD administrative access enforcement — covered by IT-SRV-WI-DC-01.
  • Identity-incident response — account compromise triage, sign-in investigation, OAuth abuse, MFA bypass response — covered by SOC SOPs (SOP-ID-01) and the ID-PB / ID-RB content.
  • HR system integration mechanics — the technical integration between Workday / SAP SuccessFactors / equivalent and Entra Connect / SCIM — is referenced but the integration design itself is in HRIS / IT architecture, not here.
Identity Classes

The domain treats identities in five operational classes. Each class has its own lifecycle pattern, governance owner, and review cadence.

👤

Employees & Contractors

Sourced from authoritative HR systems. JML driven by HR events. Access recertification by line manager + system owner. Strongest governance pattern — IT-ID-WI-01.

🎓

Students & Alumni

Education tenants (AKES, AKA). Lifecycle driven by Student Information System. Bulk cohort transitions (enrolment, graduation, alumni transition). Different from staff JML — IT-ID-WI-01.

🤝

Guests & External Collaborators

Entra B2B guests, cross-tenant access, external partner identities. Sponsor-driven lifecycle, time-bounded, periodic re-justification — IT-ID-WI-01 §6.

🔧

Service Accounts (on-prem)

AD service accounts, gMSAs, sMSAs. Often legacy, often over-privileged, often without a named owner. Inventory, ownership, lifecycle — IT-ID-WI-04.

☁️

Non-Human Cloud Identities

Entra service principals, app registrations, managed identities (system and user-assigned), workload identity federation, certificate-based identities. The fastest-growing identity class — IT-ID-WI-04.

Non-human identities deserve special attention. The estate today has many more service accounts and service principals than human accounts. They rarely rotate credentials, often hold standing privilege, frequently lack named owners, and almost never get recertified. The non-human identity WI (IT-ID-WI-04) addresses this category specifically — not as an afterthought.
Core SOP
IT-ID-SOP-01
Identity Governance & Lifecycle
Authoritative policy-level baseline for identity governance across hybrid Entra / Active Directory environments, covering both human and non-human identities. Defines the seven principles (authoritative source, least privilege by default, time-bounded standing privilege, recertification cadence, named ownership, non-human identities are first-class, separation of governance from incident response), JML lifecycle requirements, recertification cadence per access tier, role catalogue governance, and the eligibility layer above PIM. Operationalised by IT-ID-WI-01 through IT-ID-WI-05.
Work Instructions
IT-ID-WI-01
Joiner / Mover / Leaver Lifecycle
Hybrid JML across HR system → AD → Entra ID. Joiner provisioning from authoritative source. Mover with explicit access change discipline (revoke previous role, grant new, do not accumulate). Leaver with same-day account disable, time-bounded retention, and clean termination. Covers staff, contractor, student, alumni, and guest sub-flows.
IT-ID-WI-02
Access Recertification & Attestation
Periodic review of who has what access. Cadence per access tier (Tier 0 quarterly, Tier 1 semi-annual, Tier 2/3 annual). Named reviewer per scope. Evidence retention. Automated where possible (Entra Identity Governance access reviews); manual where not. Revocation of unjustified access; remediation of orphaned accounts.
IT-ID-WI-03
Role Design & Entitlement Management
Role catalogue maintenance. RBAC role design with least-privilege defaults. Group hygiene — naming, ownership, nesting limits, membership review. Entitlement packages for common access bundles (Entra Identity Governance where licensed). Avoidance of standing privilege through eligibility patterns.
IT-ID-WI-04
Non-Human Identity Governance
Service accounts (AD, gMSA, sMSA), Entra service principals and app registrations, managed identities (system and user-assigned), workload identity federation, certificate-based identities. Inventory, named ownership, credential rotation discipline, secret expiry tracking, OAuth scope review, decommissioning of unused identities.
IT-ID-WI-05
Privileged Identity Governance
The eligibility layer above PIM activation. Who is eligible for which privileged role, why, for how long, reviewed by whom. Separation of duties enforcement. Tier-0 eligibility curation. Privileged access request workflow. Quarterly review of eligibilities. Eligibility ≠ activation — the activation mechanics live in IT-CL-WI-01 Step 7 and IT-SI-WI-04; the governance of who is eligible lives here.

Roles & Ownership

Head of IT

Owns the Identity Governance domain. Accountable to the Risk Committee for identity-related control state. Sets reporting cadence. Approves baseline changes and material exceptions.

Identity & Access Engineering

Implements the WIs. Maintains the role catalogue, JML automation, recertification campaigns. Operates Entra Identity Governance where licensed. Co-owns the non-human identity inventory.

HR Information Systems

Owns the authoritative source for human identity attributes. Maintains the HR → identity integration. Triggers JML events. Reviewed quarterly with IAE for data-quality drift.

Line Managers

Recertify direct-report access at the cadence per tier. Sponsor mover access changes. Initiate leaver events when departures are not automated.

System Owners

Recertify access to systems they own. Define the role / entitlement structure for their application. Named contact for access requests outside the standard catalogue.

Privileged Access Authority

Approves eligibility for Tier-0 and Tier-1 privileged roles. Owns the privileged access request workflow. Quarterly eligibility review. Separation of duties enforcement.

Non-Human Identity Owners

Named owner per service account / service principal / managed identity. Responsible for credential rotation, scope justification, decommissioning. Reviewed quarterly.

GRC

Owns governance of the domain. Maintains recertification evidence. Reports access posture to Risk Committee quarterly. Tracks orphaned account remediation. Reviews baseline annually.

Metrics & Oversight

The metrics below measure adherence to the domain. Reviewed monthly by IT Operations and quarterly by GRC:

  • JML SLA conformance — joiner provisioning within 1 business day; mover access updated within 5 business days of role change; leaver account disabled same-day. Target: ≥95% on time.
  • Orphaned account count — accounts with no current employment / engagement record. Target: 0 within 30 days of termination; trend monthly.
  • Recertification campaign completion — percentage of access reviews completed within the campaign window per tier. Target: 100% for Tier-0 and Tier-1; ≥95% Tier-2/3.
  • Recertification revocation rate — percentage of reviewed access actually revoked. Track trends — too low suggests rubber-stamping; too high suggests over-provisioning at grant time.
  • Standing privileged access — count of accounts with permanent (non-eligible) Tier-0 role assignment. Target: ≤4 (break-glass accounts only).
  • Service account inventory coverage — percentage of service accounts and service principals with a named owner and last-review date within 12 months. Target: 100% within FY 2026.
  • Service principal secret hygiene — percentage of service principal credentials with expiry < 1 year and rotated on schedule. Target: ≥98%.
  • Role catalogue health — percentage of roles with documented owner, defined scope, and last-review date within 12 months. Target: ≥95%.
Exception Handling

Where a requirement cannot be met (HR system limitations, legacy service account with unknown ownership, vendor-managed identity not under tenant control), the operating team submits a deviation request via IT-GOV-SOP-01. Deviations are time-bounded, recorded in GRC-REG-EXCEPTIONS, linked from the relevant identity-class record, and reviewed annually.

Three exception categories deserve specific treatment. Orphaned service accounts of unknown lineage: time-bounded discovery before forced decommission. Legacy applications that cannot consume modern identity (LDAP-only, certificate-only without rotation): documented as platform-debt items with remediation roadmaps. HR-system integration gaps that prevent automatic JML: manual JML procedures as transitional state until integration matures.
