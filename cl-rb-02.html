Contain Cloud Identity and Access Abuse

Controlled containment steps for risky cloud identities, sessions, credentials, service principals, and app registrations.

RunbookContainmentRequires approvalIdentity
Operational boundary: This document is tool-agnostic. Use platform-specific consoles or automation only where authorised and record every action in the incident or change ticket.

Procedure
1

Confirm approval

Obtain Tier-2 or Incident Commander approval before disabling production identities or rotating secrets used by live services.

2

Preserve current state

Capture assignments, permissions, sign-in context, credential objects, app consent grants, and owner metadata.

3

Revoke active access

Revoke sessions, invalidate refresh tokens where supported, remove risky conditional exceptions, and block sign-in for compromised human identities.

4

Contain non-human identity

Disable or restrict service principal, rotate secrets/certificates, remove excessive roles, and validate dependent services before production change.

5

Remove persistence

Remove unauthorised app credentials, app roles, delegated permissions, federation changes, and privileged assignments.

6

Validate

Confirm failed reuse attempts, no new privileged path, and affected services remain either contained or recovered under approved exception.

Escalation and evidence

Escalate immediately if privileged access abuse, broad data access, logging tampering, unauthorised federation, production outage, or regulatory notification risk is suspected.

Minimum evidence includes the alert, analyst timeline, affected resources, affected principals, approval record for containment, before/after configuration state, and validation that the risk has been removed or accepted.
