CL-RB-02: Contain Cloud Identity and Access Abuse

Controlled containment steps for risky cloud identities, sessions, credentials, service principals, and app registrations.

Section 1
Purpose

Controlled containment steps for risky cloud identities, sessions, credentials, service principals, and app registrations.

This runbook provides repeatable execution steps for analysts and platform operators while preserving evidence and approval traceability.

Section 3
Inputs Required
Approved incident or containment ticketAffected human or non-human principalCurrent assignments, permissions, sessions, and credentialsKnown business owner and service dependencies
Section 4
Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Confirm approval
1
Obtain Tier-2 or Incident Commander approval before disabling production identities or rotating secrets used by live services.
2
Record the approval and expected business impact in the ticket.
2
Preserve current state
1
Capture assignments, permissions, sign-in context, credential objects, app consent grants, and owner metadata.
2
Export before-state evidence before removing privileges or credentials.
3
Revoke active access
1
Revoke sessions, invalidate refresh tokens where supported, remove risky conditional exceptions, and block sign-in for compromised human identities.
4
Contain non-human identity
1
Disable or restrict service principal, rotate secrets/certificates, remove excessive roles, and validate dependent services before production change.
5
Remove persistence
1
Remove unauthorised app credentials, app roles, delegated permissions, federation changes, and privileged assignments.
6
Validate containment
1
Confirm failed reuse attempts, no new privileged path, and affected services remain either contained or recovered under approved exception.
Section 5
Expected Outcome

Risky access is revoked or constrained, unauthorised persistence is removed, and service impact is documented or accepted by the incident owner.

Section 6
Validation Checks
  • Approval record is attached.
  • Before/after permissions and credential state are captured.
  • Sessions/tokens were revoked where applicable.
  • No unauthorised privileged path remains.
Section 7
Common Errors & Tips
Do not rotate production service secrets without checking service dependency impact.
Keep break-glass access protected and available.
Validate both human and non-human identities; persistence often lives in app registrations or service principals.
Section 8
Output Artefacts

Before/after IAM export
