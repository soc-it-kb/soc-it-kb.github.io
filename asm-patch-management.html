🩹 Patch Management

Governed patching lifecycle ensuring systems are protected against known vulnerabilities, exposure windows are minimised, and operational resilience is maintained across all environments. A core resilience control — not just an IT maintenance activity.

NIST CSF 2.0 — Protect CIS Critical Controls ISO 27001 Routine · Emergency · Zero-day
⚠️ Why patch management matters

Unpatched systems remain the leading cause of successful cyber attacks, ransomware incidents, and service outages. A consistent and governed patch management capability enables the organisation to reduce exposure to known and weaponised vulnerabilities, contain the blast radius of incidents, support rapid recovery and rebuild scenarios, and maintain compliance — even in low-maturity and constrained environments.

Section 1
Scope

Patch Management applies to all endpoints (laptops and desktops), servers (physical, virtual, and cloud-based), infrastructure and platform components, and security tooling and supporting services. It covers routine, emergency, and zero-day patching across centrally and locally managed systems.

Section 5
Risk-Based Prioritisation Model

Patch prioritisation follows a risk-driven model. Emergency patching may bypass standard change windows when justified by risk.

🔴 Vulnerability severity & exploitability

CVSS score combined with known active exploitation (KEV) determines urgency. Critical CVEs with public exploits are treated as emergency patches.

🖥️ Asset criticality & exposure

Internet-facing and mission-critical systems carry stricter SLAs. Internal-only low-criticality assets may follow standard timelines.

🧠 Threat intelligence

Active exploitation in the wild — confirmed by threat intel feeds — triggers emergency patching regardless of CVSS score.

⚙️ Business & operational impact

Patch compatibility, downtime requirements, and service dependencies are assessed before deployment to minimise operational disruption.

Section 8
Exception Handling

Where patches cannot be applied: a formal Patch Exception & Risk Acceptance process (IT-PM-SOP-03) must be followed, compensating controls must be implemented, and exceptions are time-bound with regular review. Untracked or expired exceptions are not permitted.
