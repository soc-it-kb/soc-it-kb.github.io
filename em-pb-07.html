EM-PB-07: Suspicious Mailbox Activity Incident Response

Response guidance for post-phishing mailbox abuse — covering malicious inbox rules, unauthorised forwarding, OAuth abuse, and persistence mechanisms established following account compromise.

📧 Email threats Tier 2 / 3 Identity linked
📋 ID: EM-PB-07

👤 Author: Paulo Ferreira
·
📅 Published: 24 April 2026

🏷️ Category: Email Threat / Identity

Active
Section 1
Purpose

Guide response to post-phishing mailbox abuse, including malicious inbox rules, unauthorised auto-forwarding, and persistence mechanisms established by an attacker following account compromise.

Section 2
Scope
  • Suspicious or malicious inbox rules created without business justification
  • Unauthorised auto-forwarding to external addresses
  • Unusual sign-in activity associated with the mailbox
Section 3
Trigger Conditions
  • MDR escalation involving mailbox persistence indicators
  • Audit log alert for inbox rule creation or forwarding change
  • SOC discovery during triage of EM-PB-01 through EM-PB-04
Section 4
Initial Validation

Confirm suspicious activity by evaluating the following:

  • Was an inbox rule created without a legitimate business need?
  • Is external email forwarding enabled to an unrecognised address?
  • Is there evidence of OAuth application abuse or consent grant?

If YES to any → Treat as active mailbox compromise and proceed
If NO to all → Document and close with monitoring note

Section 5
Impact Assessment

Assess the potential impact across these three dimensions:

Data exposure

Has sensitive email content been forwarded or accessed externally?

Lateral movement

Has the compromised mailbox been used to pivot to other accounts or systems?

Persistence established

Are rules or OAuth grants in place that would survive a password reset?

🔴 If persistence is confirmed → escalate to Tier-3 and invoke ID-PB-01 (Account Compromise) in parallel

Section 6
Response Strategy

Execute the following containment actions:

Remove all malicious or unauthorised inbox rulesRequired
Revoke all active sessions for the affected accountRequired
Reset account credentialsRequired
Disable external forwarding rulesRequired
Review and revoke suspicious OAuth grantsConditional
Expand scope to related accounts or shared mailboxesConditional
Section 7
Runbook Invocation Matrix

Invoke the relevant execution runbooks based on findings:

Review mailbox rules & persistence ID-RB-02 › Disable external email forwarding EM-RB-07 › Account reset / containment ID-RB-01 › Audit sign-in activity ID-RB-03 ›
Section 8
Closure Criteria

An incident may be closed when all of the following are confirmed:

  • All persistence mechanisms removed (rules, forwarding, OAuth grants)
  • Affected user account fully secured and credentials reset
  • Lessons learned documented and detection rules reviewed
Section 9
Evidence to Capture
Email rule artefacts (names, conditions, actions)
File / indicator hashes
OAuth application artefacts (if applicable)
Audit log exports covering the incident window
Sign-in log showing anomalous access
