EM-PB-01: Phishing Incident Response

Standardised decision and response framework for identifying, classifying, and responding to phishing incidents across all SOC tiers and MDR integrations.

📧 Email threats Tier 1 / 2 / 3 MDR integrated
🛡️ This document operates under GRC-IRP-01: Cyber Security Incident Response Plan — see for severity tiers (Sev 1–5), escalation paths, evidence handling, and communication templates.

Section 1
Purpose

To provide a standardised decision and response framework for identifying, classifying, and responding to phishing incidents, ensuring consistent handling across Tier-1 to Tier-3 analysts and MDR integrations.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • User-reported phishing emails
  • Email security gateway alerts
  • MDR escalations related to phishing
  • Suspicious inbound email events

❌ Out of scope

  • Internal email misuse (handled under ID playbooks)
  • Confirmed malware outbreaks (escalate to endpoint playbooks)
Section 3
Trigger Conditions

Initiate this playbook when any of the following occur:

  • User reports a suspicious email
  • Email security platform raises a phishing alert
  • MDR escalates a suspected phishing event
  • SOC analyst identifies suspicious email artefacts during investigation
Section 4
Initial Triage (Decision Logic)

4.1 Validate Event — confirm all three conditions:

  • Is the email external?
  • Is the email unsolicited?
  • Does it exhibit social engineering indicators?

If NO to any → Close as False Positive / Legitimate Email
If YES to all → Proceed to Classification (Section 5)

Section 5
Classification Logic

Classify the email into one of the following categories:

🗑️ SpamNo malicious intent, nuisance only
🎣 Commodity PhishingGeneric lure, no targeted content
🔑 Credential HarvestingLogin pages, MFA fatigue, fake portals
💀 Malware DeliveryAttachments, macro-enabled files
💰 Business Email CompromiseFinancial lure, impersonation, urgency
Section 6
User Interaction Assessment

Determine user impact by confirming:

  • Did the user click a link?
  • Did the user open an attachment?
  • Did the user submit credentials?

If no interaction → containment may still be required
If interaction confirmed or suspected → escalate severity (see Section 7)

Section 7
Risk & Escalation Criteria

Escalate to High Severity if any of the following apply:

  • Privileged or VIP user involved
  • Credentials submitted
  • Multiple recipients affected
  • Evidence of lateral spread or inbox rules abuse
  • MDR confirms malicious activity
Section 8
Response Strategy

Based on classification and impact, determine which actions are required:

Email artefact analysisRequired
Scope expansion (search org-wide)Conditional
Email removal from mailboxesConditional
Account containmentConditional
Threat intelligence enrichmentRequired
MDR escalationAs per SLA
Section 9
Runbook Invocation Matrix

The playbook stops here and invokes the relevant execution runbooks based on the scenario:

Header analysis EM-RB-01 › URL analysis EM-RB-02 › Attachment analysis EM-RB-03 › Remove email from mailboxes EM-RB-04 › Block sender / domain EM-RB-05 › User account containment ID-RB-01 ›
Section 10
Closure Criteria

An incident may be closed when all of the following are confirmed:

  • Email classified and documented
  • Required containment actions completed
  • User notified (if applicable)
  • MDR ticket synchronised (if involved)
  • Incident record updated with findings
Section 11
Evidence to Capture
Email headers
URLs / attachment hashes
Screenshots of lures
User interaction confirmation
Timeline of actions
Section 12
Post-Incident Actions
  • Identify detection gaps
  • Update awareness materials (if campaign-based)
  • Recommend tuning or blocking rules
  • Feed indicators into threat intelligence platforms
