EM-PB-06: Threatening / Harassing Email Campaign

Standardised SOC decision framework for detecting, assessing, escalating, and coordinating response to threatening or harassing emails delivered to multiple recipients or distribution lists.

Section 1
Purpose

Define a standardised SOC decision framework for detecting, assessing, escalating, and coordinating response to email messages containing threats, harassment, or intimidation delivered to multiple recipients or distribution lists, with a strong emphasis on:

  • User safety and well-being
  • Credibility assessment of threats
  • Legal, HR, and leadership coordination
  • Evidence preservation and auditability
Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope — delivery targets

  • Distribution lists
  • Multiple named recipients
  • Defined organisational groups

✅ In scope — content types

  • Explicit threats
  • Implicit intimidation
  • Harassment or coercion
  • Hate speech or discriminatory language
  • Extortion or blackmail

✅ In scope — delivery methods

  • Corporate email infrastructure
  • External email services
  • Forwarded internal communications

❌ Out of scope

  • Single-recipient non-threatening phishing
  • Spam or nuisance emails without intimidation indicators
Section 3
Playbook Entry Triggers

This playbook is triggered when any of the following conditions are met:

  • Two or more users report the same threatening or harassing email
  • An email with threatening language is sent to a distribution list
  • MDR or email security tooling flags a threatening campaign
  • HR, Legal, or Leadership escalates a reported email
  • SOC identifies threatening language during routine email triage
Section 4
Inputs & Preconditions

📥 Required inputs

  • Original email (headers + body)
  • Recipient list or distribution group
  • Time of delivery
  • User reports (if applicable)

✅ Preconditions

  • SOC access to email security tooling
  • Ability to perform mailbox search and purge
  • Incident tracking system available
Section 5
Severity & Risk Classification Model
Classification dimensions

Threat Nature

Harassment, intimidation, or explicit violence

Credibility

Specificity, timelines, personalisation of threat

Scope

Number of recipients and roles targeted

Impact

Safety risk, reputational, or operational impact

Severity mapping
Harassment only, no direct threatP3 — Low
Implicit threats or intimidationP2 — Medium
Explicit threats with personal targetingP1 — High
Threat combined with extortion or violenceP1 — Critical
Section 6
Decision Logic (Playbook Flow)
1
Does the email contain threatening or intimidating language?
No → Exit playbook — handle as standard abuse or phishing
Yes → Continue to step 2
2
Is the email delivered to multiple users or a group?
No → Use single-recipient threat playbook
Yes → Continue to step 3
3
Is there credible risk to user safety or organisational reputation?
Yes → Tier-3 escalation is mandatory
No → Tier-2 containment with monitoring
Section 7
Playbook Actions
TriageValidate email authenticity and determine full scope of recipients
AnalysisAssess threat credibility using classification dimensions (Section 5)
ContainmentRemove email from all affected mailboxes and block sender / domain
EscalationEngage Legal, HR, and Leadership per severity level
CommunicationInform and protect affected users — prioritise well-being
ClosureDocument all actions and retain evidence per legal requirements
Section 8
Outputs
Classified security incident record
Evidence package
Blocked indicators (sender, domain)
User communication artefacts
Lessons learned entry
Section 9
Governance & Compliance
  • Evidence retained per applicable legal requirements and retention policies
  • Escalation actions aligned with crisis management procedures
  • All actions must be auditable and reproducible
