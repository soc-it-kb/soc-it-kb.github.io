CL-WI-03: Disable Risky Cloud Principal

Controlled process for disabling or restricting a risky human, service, managed, automation, or third-party cloud principal with minimum disruption and full evidence traceability.

☁️ CloudOpsTier 2 / CloudOpsRequires approvalIdentity containment

Section 1
Purpose

Define how to restrict or disable a risky cloud principal while preserving evidence, assessing dependency impact, and applying the least disruptive containment action that removes immediate risk.

📌 Authority boundary: Disabling a principal can break applications, automation, access paths, or emergency procedures. Approval and blast-radius assessment are mandatory unless emergency containment is declared.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
Mandatory checks before containment
Do not disable break-glass or critical automation identities without Incident Commander approval.
Preserve assignments, credentials, sign-ins, API activity, app consent, owners, and last-used metadata.
Prefer least disruptive restriction first where it removes immediate risk.
Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Classify Principal and Risk
1
Determine whether the principal is human, admin, service principal, managed identity, break-glass, third-party, or automation identity.
2
Review assignments, group membership, app permissions, resource ownership, workload dependency, and recent activity.
2
Preserve Evidence
1
Export assignments, credentials, sign-ins, API activity, app consent, owners, and last-used metadata.
2
Record the approved containment path, approval authority, and rollback option.
3
Apply Least Disruptive Restriction
1
Block sign-in, revoke sessions, remove a specific role, disable a credential, restrict conditional access, or isolate a dependent workload based on impact.
2
If malicious activity is active or privileged, execute emergency containment according to the incident command decision.
Document why normal approval sequence was compressed.
4
Monitor and Hand Over
1
Monitor failed authentication, service health, alert recurrence, and attempts to create replacement credentials or identities.
2
Record action, timestamp, validation, rollback option, and owner for remediation.
Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Risky principal restricted or disabled
✅ Evidence preserved before containment
✅ Business impact monitored
✅ Remediation owner and rollback path documented

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Disabling a service principal without checking production dependencies.
Removing roles before exporting evidence of assignments and activity.
Applying broad tenant-wide restrictions when a narrower control removes the risk.
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Principal has privileged, cross-tenant, or broad data access.
Break-glass, automation, or production owner identity is involved.
Attacker is actively creating credentials, roles, or persistence.
