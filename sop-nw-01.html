SOP-NW-01: Network Threat Operations

Standard Operating Procedure defining how the SOC detects, triages, investigates, escalates, and responds to network-based threats — treating network telemetry as a primary detection layer for C2, reconnaissance, lateral movement, and exfiltration.

Section 1
Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines how the Security Operations Centre (SOC) detects, triages, investigates, escalates, and responds to network-based threats, ensuring early detection of adversary activity, containment of malicious traffic, and protection of critical network services.

Network telemetry is treated as a primary detection layer for identifying reconnaissance, command-and-control (C2), lateral movement, and data exfiltration that may not be visible at endpoint or identity layers.

Section 2
Scope

Applies to

  • Global SOC (Tier 1–3)
  • Regional SOCs
  • MDR provider
  • Network Security teams
  • Local IT teams supporting remediation

In-scope threat categories

  • Network scanning and reconnaissance
  • Command-and-Control (C2) communications
  • Data exfiltration attempts
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS / DDoS)
  • VPN abuse and unauthorised remote access

In-scope network assets

  • Firewalls (on-prem and cloud)
  • IDS/IPS and NDR platforms
  • VPN gateways and remote access services
  • Load balancers and web gateways
  • Core switching and routing infrastructure

Section 6
Operating Procedure (High-Level)
1
Detection & Alert Intake

Network threats may be detected via firewall, IDS/IPS or NDR alerts, SIEM correlations, MDR escalations, threat hunting activities, or external notifications from ISPs or partners. All alerts must be logged in the incident management platform.

2
Initial Triage & Classification

The SOC must determine traffic type (inbound, outbound, east-west), source and destination context, asset criticality, threat pattern (scan, C2, exfiltration, DoS, VPN abuse), and volume, duration, and recurrence. Classification must follow the applicable NW-PB-xx playbook.

3
Investigation & Analysis

Mandatory investigation steps include: packet or flow analysis where available, review of firewall, proxy, and VPN logs, correlation with endpoint and identity telemetry, validation of known malicious indicators, and assessment of data volume and sensitivity for exfiltration scenarios. Evidence must be preserved for audit, IR, and potential legal or regulatory review.

4
Containment & Mitigation

Containment actions may include blocking IPs, domains, or protocols, disabling VPN accounts or sessions, applying temporary firewall or routing rules, and rate-limiting or blackholing traffic for DoS scenarios. Containment must balance risk reduction and service availability, with Tier-3 approval for high-impact changes.

5
Escalation & Communication

Escalation is mandatory when C2 traffic is confirmed, data exfiltration is suspected or detected, critical services are disrupted, or network activity indicates lateral movement or breach. Communication must follow SOC escalation and incident communication procedures.

6
Recovery & Lessons Learned

For confirmed network incidents: validate that malicious traffic has ceased, remove temporary mitigation controls where appropriate, confirm no residual access paths remain, and update detection logic and network rules where gaps are identified. Lessons learned must be documented.
