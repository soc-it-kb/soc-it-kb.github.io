CL-RB-04: Isolate Cloud Workload / Snapshot Evidence

Runbook for safely isolating a compromised cloud workload while preserving forensic and recovery evidence.

Section 1
Purpose

Runbook for safely isolating a compromised cloud workload while preserving forensic and recovery evidence.

This runbook provides repeatable execution steps for analysts and platform operators while preserving evidence and approval traceability.

Section 2
Required Access
Cloud compute administrationNetwork security controlsSnapshot / image export capabilityWorkload owner or service ownerSOC ticketing system
Section 3
Inputs Required
Affected workload identifier and ownerService criticality and dependency mapCurrent connections, identity, image/package, and deployment contextIncident approval for isolation or snapshot actions
Section 4
Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Confirm business impact
1
Identify workload owner, service criticality, exposure, current connections, data stores, and dependency map.
2
Confirm whether isolation will cause user-facing or production impact.
2
Capture volatile context
1
Record running processes, network connections, identity attached to workload, instance metadata, container image, and recent deployment history.
3
Snapshot before rebuild
1
Create disk snapshot, image copy, configuration export, container manifest, or function/package copy according to platform capability.
4
Isolate network path
1
Remove public ingress, restrict security group/firewall rules, quarantine subnet, or detach workload from load balancer as approved.
5
Protect credentials
1
Disable abused managed identity or rotate secrets after understanding service impact.
6
Hand off
1
Escalate evidence package to incident response or proceed to rebuild under CL-PB-03.
Section 5
Expected Outcome

Workload is isolated or safely constrained, evidence is preserved, and rebuild/recovery can proceed from trusted source.

Section 6
Validation Checks
  • Business impact and approval are documented.
  • Snapshot/export evidence was created before rebuild.
  • Public ingress and risky outbound paths are restricted.
  • Credentials and workload identity were reviewed.
Section 7
Common Errors & Tips
Avoid terminating or rebuilding before evidence capture unless immediate business risk requires it.
Record image, package, and deployment provenance.
Coordinate with the service owner before disabling workload identity.
Section 8
Output Artefacts
Snapshot or image reference
Configuration export
Network isolation evidence
Credential/identity review
Handoff package
