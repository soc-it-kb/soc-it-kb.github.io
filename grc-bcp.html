Business Continuity

How the Institution continues to deliver critical services to beneficiaries and stakeholders when normal operations are disrupted. Business-led framework, methodology, and templates covering the full range of disruption types — cyber, physical, supplier, environmental, regional — with agency-populated continuity plans for each critical business process. The companion to the IT Disaster Recovery suite (GRC-DR) which addresses technology service recovery.

Scope & operating model

The Institution operates across more than 30 countries, through several agencies with materially different mandates: health services (AKHS), education (AKES, AKA), higher education and research (IIS), and the development network itself (AKDN). A central authoring approach to business continuity is not feasible at this scale — a hospital’s continuity needs are not a school’s, and a school in one country may face very different disruption risks than the same school in another.

This domain therefore operates a centre-of-excellence model:

  • Central authorship of the policy, methodology, terminology, exercise standards, and templates — in this domain.
  • Agency-level authorship of the actual continuity plans for each critical business process, populated using the templates and reviewed under the central methodology.
  • Federated assurance: agencies own their plans and exercise outcomes; central GRC tracks completeness, currency, and exercise evidence at portfolio level.

The deliverables under this domain are the central artefacts. Agency-specific Business Process Continuity Plans live in agency document management systems and are referenced by code in the central plan inventory.

Disruption types in scope

Business continuity is cause-agnostic by design. The plan exists to keep critical processes running regardless of what caused the disruption. The scenarios below illustrate the range:

CategoryExample scenariosPrimary owner of response
Cyber Ransomware affecting clinical systems; identity provider compromise; destructive attack on file shares SOC for response (IRP); BCP for service continuity workarounds
Technology Data centre outage; Azure region failure; M365 sustained outage; major application failure IT for recovery (DR); BCP for workarounds during recovery window
Supplier Critical supplier insolvency; cloud provider sustained outage; SaaS vendor compromise; logistics disruption Procurement / vendor management; BCP coordinates business workaround
Physical Building damage by fire, flood, earthquake; loss of campus access; key facility compromise Facilities / security; BCP for relocation, remote operating, alternative-site activation
People Pandemic-scale absenteeism; loss of key personnel concentration; civil unrest preventing staff travel HR / people operations; BCP for cross-training, succession activation, alternative workforce models
Regional Country-level civil disruption; sanction-driven service withdrawal; broad infrastructure failure Country leadership; BCP for cross-country redistribution where possible

The methodology in GRC-BCDR-SOP-01 defines how each agency identifies and ranks its critical processes; the policy in GRC-BCP-SOP-01 defines the agency obligations that follow.

Roles & accountabilities at domain level

Detailed role responsibilities are defined in GRC-BCP-SOP-01 and GRC-BCDR-SOP-01. At domain level the following accountability holds:

  • Group Leadership / CEO sponsorship — accountable for the Institution’s continuity posture overall; receives portfolio reporting on plan currency and exercise outcomes.
  • BCDR Steering Committee — cross-agency governance body; chairs methodology updates, exercise schedule, lessons-learned consolidation.
  • Agency BCP Coordinator — named role per agency; owns the agency’s plan inventory and exercise programme; reports to the Steering Committee.
  • Business Process Owner — named individual per critical business process; owns the process’s continuity plan; reviews and signs off annually; participates in exercises.
  • Crisis Management Team (per agency / per site) — activates the continuity plan during a disruption; coordinates communications, workarounds, and recovery.
  • GRC (central) — maintains methodology, templates, and portfolio inventory; reports completeness and currency; coordinates with IT DR.
  • Internal Audit — independently tests plan design and exercise outcomes; relies on exercise evidence and BIA documentation

Key metrics tracked at portfolio level
  • Plan coverage — percentage of agency-identified critical processes with a current BCP. Target: 100% of Tier-1 critical, 90% of Tier-2.
  • Plan currency — percentage of plans reviewed within the last 12 months.
  • Exercise compliance — percentage of plans exercised within the last 12 months (any exercise type counts).
  • BIA currency — percentage of agencies with a BIA refresh in the last 24 months.
  • Exercise lessons-learned closure — percentage of exercise findings remediated within target window.
  • Open continuity-related exceptions — entries in the exception register with overdue remediation dates.
