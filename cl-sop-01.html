CL-SOP-01: Cloud Security Operations SOP

Standard Operating Procedure defining how the SOC, CloudOps, IT operations, GRC, and incident response teams detect, triage, contain, recover, and govern cloud security events across tenants, subscriptions, workloads, SaaS platforms, and cloud-hosted data.

CloudOps SOC + Cloud teams NIST CSF 2.0 ISO 27035

Section 1
Purpose

This SOP establishes the mandatory operating model for cloud security operations, ensuring cloud alerts and incidents are handled consistently from detection through containment, recovery, evidence preservation, and lessons learned.

The objective is to protect cloud identities, management planes, workloads, storage, SaaS services, and customer or business data while maintaining traceable decision-making and clear authority for high-impact containment actions.

Section 2
Scope

Applies to

  • Global SOC and regional SOC teams
  • CloudOps and IT operations teams
  • MDR provider and incident response teams
  • GRC, Legal, Privacy, and business service owners
  • Teams operating cloud, SaaS, and hybrid workloads

In-scope cloud assets

  • Cloud tenants, subscriptions, projects, and accounts
  • Identity providers, privileged roles, service principals, and API keys
  • Virtual machines, containers, serverless functions, and app services
  • Object storage, databases, snapshots, data lakes, and SaaS repositories
  • Cloud networking, firewalls, security groups, and management interfaces

In-scope event categories

  • Control-plane compromise, suspicious administrative activity, and logging tampering
  • Cloud identity and access abuse, risky OAuth or application consent, and credential exposure
  • Public exposure or misconfiguration affecting services, storage, databases, or management interfaces
  • Cloud workload compromise, malware, suspicious process activity, and unauthorised outbound traffic
  • Cloud data exposure, excessive data access, unusual downloads, and unauthorised sharing

Section 6
Operating Procedure (High-Level)
1
Detection & Alert Intake

Cloud alerts may originate from SIEM, cloud-native security tools, CASB/SaaS logs, MDR escalations, identity detections, CSPM findings, threat intelligence, or external notifications. All alerts must be recorded in the ticketing or incident management platform.

2
Initial Triage & Classification

The SOC must determine affected tenant, subscription, resource, principal, service, data sensitivity, business owner, event timeline, and likely incident category. The applicable CL-PB-xx playbook must drive classification and response.

3
Investigation & Evidence Preservation

Collect control-plane audit logs, sign-in logs, resource activity logs, policy and permission changes, impacted principals, screenshots, configuration state, snapshots where authorised, and analyst timeline. Preserve volatile evidence before destructive remediation.

4
Containment & Mitigation

Containment may include revoking sessions, disabling risky accounts or service principals, rotating keys and secrets, locking down public access, isolating workloads, applying firewall or security group restrictions, and freezing data access. Production-impacting actions require Tier 3 or Incident Commander approval.

5
Escalation & Communication

Escalate immediately when privileged access abuse, broad data access, logging tampering, unauthorised federation, customer data exposure, production outage, or regulatory notification risk is suspected.

6
Recovery, Hardening & Lessons Learned

Restore service using clean configuration, validate that access and exposure risks are removed, rotate affected credentials, update detection logic, and feed lessons learned into cloud baselines and operational runbooks.

Section 7
Severity Classification & Response
SeverityDescriptionResponse

Sev 3 — HighConfirmed malicious activity affecting cloud identities, workloads, or sensitive resourcesSOC-led investigation with CloudOps containment
Sev 4 — MediumSuspicious activity or limited misconfiguration with no confirmed impactSOC triage, remediation tracking, and validation
Sev 5 — LowBenign event, expected administrative activity, or false positiveDocument rationale and close
📌 Severity model alignment. The operational view above supports analyst triage for cloud events. The authoritative 5-tier severity model is defined in GRC-IRP-01 §5; analyst-level triage guidance is in SOP-IM-01. Sev 1 incidents follow the IRP escalation directly.
Section 8
Tooling & Evidence Handling
☁️ Cloud audit and activity logs 🔐 Identity provider logs 🧭 CSPM / CNAPP / cloud security posture tools 📊 SIEM / SOAR 🗃️ Storage, database, and SaaS audit logs 🎫 Ticketing platform

All evidence must be retained per incident response and evidence handling requirements. Analysts must avoid deleting or rebuilding resources before relevant logs, configuration state, and snapshots have been preserved or explicitly deemed unnecessary.

Section 9
Exceptions

Any deviation from this SOP must be justified by operational constraints, approved by the SOC Manager, CloudOps owner, or Incident Commander depending on severity, and documented in the incident or change record.

Section 10
Compliance & Audit
✅ NIST CSF 2.0 ✅ ISO 27001:2022 ✅ ISO 27035 ✅ CIS cloud benchmarks ✅ Cloud baseline governance

Audit evidence includes tickets, cloud logs, identity logs, analyst notes, approval records, before/after configuration state, remediation actions, and documented residual risk or business acceptance.
