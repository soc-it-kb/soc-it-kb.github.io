ASM · Threat Intelligence

🔍 Threat Intelligence

Operational threat intelligence — sourcing, processing, and actioning intelligence to improve SOC detection, accelerate vulnerability prioritisation, and enable proactive threat hunting. Built for a maturing TI capability consuming open-source and selected commercial feeds.

3 SOPs 5 Work Instructions IOC management Threat hunting MITRE ATT&CK aligned
Overview
TI Programme Principles

🎯 Intelligence must be actionable

TI that cannot be acted upon through detection, blocking, or risk decision has no operational value

📊 Relevance over volume

A few high-quality relevant indicators beat thousands of unvalidated feed entries

🔄 Drives decisions

TI actively influences SOC priorities, VM remediation, and patching — not a separate silo

✅ Validate before deploy

No unvalidated IOC is ever deployed to a blocking control — quality gates are mandatory

TI Lifecycle
Intelligence Lifecycle — Direction to Feedback

1 · Direction

Define intelligence requirements — what threats matter to the Institution and sector

2 · Collection

Consume from approved feeds, advisories, ISACs, and internal telemetry

3 · Processing

Deduplicate, normalise, validate, and enrich raw intelligence

4 · Analysis

Assess relevance, correlate with known vulnerabilities and active campaigns

5 · Dissemination

Route intelligence to the right consumer — SOC, VM, IT Ops, management

6 · Action & Feedback

Deploy IOCs, update detections, escalate patches — feed outcomes back
