ID-PB-02: Suspicious Mailbox Rules & Identity Persistence

Standardised framework for detecting, investigating, and remediating identity persistence mechanisms following account compromise — ensuring attackers cannot maintain access after credentials have been reset.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a standardised decision and response framework for detecting, investigating, and remediating identity persistence mechanisms following account compromise, with a focus on mailbox rules, forwarding, and stealth access techniques.

This playbook ensures attackers cannot maintain access after credentials have been reset.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • Suspicious inbox rules
  • External mail forwarding
  • Hidden or obfuscated mailbox rules
  • Persistence discovered during phishing response (EM-PB-01 → EM-PB-05)
  • Persistence discovered during account compromise handling (ID-PB-01)
  • MDR escalations and proactive SOC hunting

❌ Out of scope

  • OAuth application abuse → ID-PB-04
  • Endpoint-only persistence → Endpoint playbooks
Section 3
Trigger Conditions

Initiate this playbook when any of the following occur:

  • Suspicious mailbox rules detected via audit log or tooling
  • External email forwarding identified on a user account
  • MDR flags mailbox persistence indicators
  • SOC discovers rules during an ID-PB-01 investigation
  • User reports missing emails or unusual mailbox behaviour
Section 4
Initial Validation (Decision Logic)

Confirm that the activity is not business-justified by reviewing the following:

  • Was the rule created by the user knowingly and intentionally?
  • Is the forwarding destination an approved and recognised address?
  • Does the rule align with a documented and verifiable business process?

If YES to all → Document and close as Authorised Configuration
If NO or UNCLEAR to any → Treat as Malicious Persistence and proceed

Section 5
Persistence Assessment

Evaluate the type and severity of persistence. Presence of any indicator confirms persistence:

🗑️ Auto-delete rules Emails silently moved to Deleted Items
📤 Auto-forwarding External recipient configured for forwarding
🔍 Keyword-based rules Rules targeting financial or security-related terms
👻 Hidden rules Non-descriptive or obfuscated rule names
⏱️ Timing correlation Rule created at or near time of compromise
Section 6
Risk & Escalation Criteria

Escalate to High Severity if any of the following apply:

🔴 Persistence follows confirmed credential compromise
🔴 External forwarding is currently active
🔴 Privileged or VIP account involved
🔴 Evidence of data exposure via forwarding or rule
🔴 Multiple persistence mechanisms identified simultaneously

Notify immediately

Section 7
Response Strategy

The following actions must be orchestrated across the response team:

Mailbox rule review — enumerate all rulesRequired
Remove all malicious or unauthorised rulesRequired
Disable external email forwardingRequired
Sign-in log reviewRequired
Session revocationConditional
Credential resetConditional
Scope expansion to other accountsConditional
Section 8
Runbook Invocation Matrix

This playbook invokes execution runbooks only:

Review and remove mailbox rules ID-RB-02 › Disable external email forwarding EM-RB-07 › Reset compromised account ID-RB-01 › Review sign-in activity ID-RB-03 › Endpoint follow-up (if needed) EP-RB-01 ›
Section 9
Scope Expansion Logic

Triggers for expansion

  • Rules reference financial or security terms
  • Forwarding destination reused across multiple users
  • Rule creation IP appears in other incidents
  • MDR indicates campaign-level activity

Expansion actions

  • Search for similar rules across the entire tenant
  • Review forwarding settings across all users
  • Correlate sign-in IPs across accounts
Section 10
Communication & Coordination

Inform the affected user of:

  • What persistence mechanisms were found in their mailbox
  • What was removed and when
  • Any actions required from them (e.g. password reset, MFA re-registration)

Coordinate credential reset timing carefully — if an attacker is actively monitoring, premature reset alerts may trigger data destruction. Align with MDR ticket workflow before acting.

Section 11
Closure Criteria

The incident may be closed when all of the following are confirmed:

  • All malicious or unauthorised rules removed
  • External forwarding fully disabled
  • No unauthorised persistence mechanisms remain
  • Sign-in activity reviewed and confirmed clean
  • User confirms normal mailbox behaviour restored
Section 12
Evidence to Capture
Screenshots or exports of malicious rules
Forwarding configuration before and after
Rule creation timestamps
Associated sign-in IP addresses
Timeline of all remediation actions
Section 13
Post-Incident Actions
  • Identify detection gaps in mailbox rule and forwarding alerting
  • Recommend tenant-wide external forwarding restrictions
  • Update SOC hunting queries with new persistence indicators
  • Feed persistence IOCs into threat intelligence platform
  • Reinforce user awareness if phishing was the initial vector
