NW-04: Firewall / IDS Alert

Answer each question YES or NO and apply the decision logic. Escalate with evidence attached — do not reboot or alter the endpoint before escalating.

🌐 Network threats Tier 1 Published 28 April 2026
🔔 When to use this checklist
Firewall, IDS, or IPS alert indicating blocked or detected suspicious traffic — including exploit attempts, policy violations, or anomalous connection patterns at the network perimeter or internally.

Step 2 — Apply decision logic
✅
Traffic blocked, single external source, no known CVE, no internal target — Document, monitor for recurrence, no further action required.
⚠️
YES to questions 2 or 4 — Escalate to Tier-2. Block source IP via NW-WI-02. Investigate targeted service for vulnerability exposure.
🔴
YES to question 1, 3, 7, or 8 — Escalate to Tier-3 immediately. Invoke NW-PB-01. Internal source indicates possible compromise.
🔴
YES to question 5 — Escalate immediately — Tier-0/1 targeting is a critical incident regardless of other answers.
Step 3 — Capture evidence before escalating
Alert name, signature, and severity
Source and destination IP and port
Direction of traffic — inbound or outbound
Number of alert occurrences and timeframe
Whether traffic was blocked or permitted
Targeted service and CVE reference if applicable
💡
Analyst Tip
The most important question with firewall and IDS alerts is the direction and source. An external IP scanning your perimeter is noise — an internal IP triggering IDS signatures is almost always significant. Always check whether the traffic was blocked or only alerted — an IDS in detection-only mode means the traffic went through.
