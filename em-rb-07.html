EM-RB-07: Disable External Email Forwarding

Disable unauthorised external email forwarding — a common post-compromise data exfiltration technique. Covers mailbox-level settings, inbox rules, and tenant-wide policy enforcement.

Section 1
Purpose

Disable unauthorized external email forwarding — a common data exfiltration technique used by attackers following account compromise.

Forwarding can exist at multiple layers — check all

Mailbox settings

Forwarding set directly on the mailbox via OWA or admin portal

Inbox rules

Rules that auto-forward or redirect matching messages

Tenant policy

Organisation-wide forwarding policy — may allow or block by default

Legacy settings

Hidden or older forwarding configurations set via PowerShell

Section 2
Required Access
🔑 Exchange Admin ⚙️ Tenant policy access
Section 3
Inputs Required
📬 User mailbox 📤 Forwarding destination address
Section 4
Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Access the mailbox forwarding settings via the admin portal or Exchange Admin Center
2
Identify all external forwarding addresses configured on the mailbox
Document the forwarding destination before removing — this is evidence
3
Disable mailbox-level forwarding
4
Review the tenant-wide external forwarding policy — confirm it restricts or blocks external forwarding by default
If the policy currently allows external forwarding, recommend restricting it as a post-incident hardening action
5
Review and remove any unauthorised inbox forwarding rules (including hidden or non-descriptively named rules)
See ID-RB-02 for the full mailbox rule review procedure
6
Save all configuration changes and confirm they are applied
7
Record all actions, forwarding destinations removed, and timestamps in the incident ticket
Section 5
Expected Outcome

✅ No external email forwarding active on the affected mailbox
✅ Data exfiltration via email forwarding stopped
✅ Tenant policy reviewed and enforced

Section 6
Validation Checks
  • Send a test email to the mailbox and confirm it does not forward externally
  • Policy enforcement confirmed active in Exchange Admin Center
Section 7
Common Errors & Tips
Forwarding can exist at multiple layers simultaneously — always check mailbox settings, inbox rules, and tenant policy
Always check for hidden rules and legacy PowerShell-configured settings — these may not appear in the standard admin UI

⚠️ Document the forwarding destination before removing it — this is an IOC and may be required for threat intelligence and legal purposes.

Section 8
Output Artefacts
Forwarding destination (IOC)
Removal confirmation
Policy review record
