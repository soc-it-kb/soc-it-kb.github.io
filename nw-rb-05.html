NW-RB-05: Coordinate ISP / Upstream Mitigation

Engage and coordinate with ISPs, upstream providers, or cloud DDoS mitigation services when local mitigations are insufficient to stop large-scale DoS or network abuse.

🌐 Network threats Tier 3 / Incident Command Upstream coordination

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a controlled, auditable procedure to engage and coordinate with ISPs, upstream providers, or cloud DDoS mitigation services when on-premises or local mitigations are insufficient to stop DoS/DDoS attacks or large-scale network abuse.

This runbook executes escalation actions from NW-PB-05 and severe escalations from NW-PB-02 and NW-PB-04 where upstream blocking is required.

Section 2
Required Access
📞 ISP / upstream provider contact details 🛡️ Contractual DDoS mitigation service access 📊 Network telemetry (traffic graphs, alerts) ⚙️ Incident Command escalation channel 🎫 SOC ticketing system
Section 3
Inputs Required
🎫 Incident reference ID 🌐 Affected public IPs, services, or domains ⚡ Attack type and traffic volumes 🕐 Attack start time and timeline 💼 Business impact assessment
Section 4
Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Validate Escalation Threshold
1
Confirm upstream escalation is justified by at least one of:
  • Local mitigations (NW-RB-04) have been applied and are ineffective
  • Traffic volume exceeds on-premises mitigation capacity
  • ISP or MDR confirms a large-scale distributed attack
2
Record justification for upstream escalation in the ticket
2
Activate Incident Command
1
Notify Incident Command / Crisis Management if not already active
2
Confirm the following roles are filled:
  • Technical lead
  • Business owner
  • Communications lead
3
Align escalation timing and external messaging with Incident Command
3
Prepare Evidence Package for ISP

Compile the following before contacting the ISP:

Traffic volume graphs — before and during attack
Targeted IPs, ports, and protocols
Attack start time and current status
Source IP ranges / ASNs (if identified)
Summary of internal mitigations already applied
Requested action (scrubbing, filtering, or null routing)
4
Contact ISP / Upstream Provider
1
Contact via the predefined escalation channel — NOC hotline, security incident contact, or customer portal
2
Provide the incident reference, evidence package, and requested mitigation scope
3
Request confirmation of:
  • Mitigation type to be applied
  • Estimated activation time
  • Monitoring visibility provided to SOC
4
Record all contact details, names, and timestamps in the ticket
5
Coordinate Mitigation Strategy
1
Agree on one or more upstream mitigation actions:
  • Traffic scrubbing or diversion to clean pipe
  • Upstream rate limiting
  • Protocol-specific filtering
  • Temporary null routing (last resort only)
2
Ensure business impact is understood, mitigation is reversible, and duration and review points are defined

🚫 Null routing is a last resort only — it completely drops all traffic to the targeted IP, including legitimate traffic. Only request if all other options are exhausted and service is already down.

6
Monitor Mitigation Effectiveness
1
Monitor traffic volume reduction and service availability restoration
2
Confirm attack traffic is being absorbed or filtered upstream with no unintended service outage
3
Provide feedback to the ISP if tuning is required
7
Coordinate De-escalation
1
When the attack subsides — agree de-escalation timing with the ISP
2
Gradually remove upstream mitigations and monitor for resurgence
3
Confirm stable state before declaring closure
8
Document Actions
1
Update SOC ticket with ISP contacted, mitigation actions taken, and timelines
2
Attach ISP confirmation records, traffic graphs, and de-escalation confirmation. Record lessons learned and any contract gaps identified.
Section 5
Expected Outcome

✅ Large-scale attack mitigated upstream
✅ Core services restored and stabilised
✅ Clear coordination and audit trail maintained

Section 6
Validation Checks
  • Traffic volumes normalised
  • Services fully available
  • No ongoing upstream mitigation required
Section 7
Common Errors & Tips
Null routing should be a last resort only — it drops all traffic including legitimate users and should only be used when the service is already completely unavailable
Ensure legal and contractual approval paths are understood in advance — upstream mitigation often requires pre-existing contracts
Upstream mitigation activation typically takes minutes to hours — set expectations with leadership and Comms before contacting the ISP
DoS attacks may mask secondary intrusions running in parallel — continue monitoring EDR and identity telemetry throughout
Section 8
Output Artefacts
ISP escalation record
Mitigation confirmation
Traffic and availability evidence
Incident timeline update
