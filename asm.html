Attack Surface Management

The ASM section governs how the organisation identifies, understands, and reduces its exploitable exposure across people, technology, and processes. It brings together Security Baselines, Vulnerability Management, Patch Management, and Threat Intelligence into a unified, risk-driven capability aligned to Zero Trust principles and NIST CSF 2.0.

Framework alignments

Attack Surface Management is the continuous practice of discovering, inventorying, and reducing an organisation's exploitable exposure. In a Zero Trust model, every asset is a potential entry point — ASM ensures that no asset is unknown, no vulnerability is untracked, and no patch is indefinitely deferred without documented risk acceptance.

This section bridges Cyber Security and IT Operations. It provides the governance frameworks (SOPs) and operational procedures (Work Instructions) that local, regional, and global IT teams can consistently apply regardless of location or maturity level. All content is tool-agnostic — the what and why are defined here; the how is implemented through platform-specific Work Instructions.

🛡️
Zero Trust alignment
Zero Trust assumes breach and requires continuous verification. ASM provides the foundation — you cannot enforce least-privilege access, detect anomalies, or respond to incidents effectively if you don't know what assets exist, what vulnerabilities they carry, and what threats are actively targeting them. ASM is not a project — it is an ongoing operational discipline.

Framework alignment

🏛️ NIST CSF 2.0

ASM primarily supports the Identify (ID) and Protect (PR) functions — asset management, risk assessment, and platform security. Feeds into Detect and Respond through threat intelligence integration.

📋 CIS Benchmarks

Security Baselines are aligned to CIS Level 1 as the minimum default. CIS Controls also provide the remediation prioritisation model used in Vulnerability Management.

🔐 ISO/IEC 27001

ASM SOPs support Annex A technical controls — asset management (A.8), vulnerability management, and configuration management. Supports audit readiness.

How to use this section

🔐 Cyber Security Teams

Use Vulnerability Management and Threat Intelligence for prioritisation decisions. Reference Security Baselines for posture validation during investigations.

💻 IT Operations

Use Security Baseline Work Instructions to harden devices. Follow Patch Management SOPs and WIs for all patching activities.

📋 GRC & Audit

Use SOPs and exception processes for compliance evidence. Metrics from VM and Patch Management feed audit reporting.

👔 Management

Use domain pages for risk visibility. Review exception registers and SLA compliance metrics for oversight decisions.
