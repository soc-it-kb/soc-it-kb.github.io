NW-WI-01: Analyse Network Traffic

Tool-agnostic procedure for analysing suspicious network traffic to determine whether it is benign or malicious, identify C2, reconnaissance, or exfiltration indicators, and produce evidence for escalation or closure.

🌐 Network threats Tier 1 / 2 Investigation only No blocking actions

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a consistent, tool-agnostic procedure to analyse suspicious network traffic in order to determine whether traffic is benign or malicious, identify indicators of C2, reconnaissance, or exfiltration, correlate network activity with endpoint and identity signals, and produce evidence to support escalation or closure.

📌 Scope boundary: Investigation and evidence collection only. No blocking or containment actions are performed in this WI — those are executed via NW-WI-02 or NW-WI-03.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
🚫 Mandatory constraints — this WI is read-only

Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Validate Alert or Trigger Context
1
Review the alert source — NDR, firewall, proxy/SWG, or MDR escalation. Record the detection logic, severity and confidence, and the timestamp when activity started.
2
Identify Traffic Direction & Scope
1
Determine traffic direction and classify source and destination:
  • Inbound — external to internal
  • Outbound — internal to external
  • East-west — internal to internal (see NW-WI-03)
2
Identify source system role (endpoint, server, appliance) and destination type (internet, cloud, internal segment)
3
Analyse Traffic Volume, Frequency & Patterns
1
Review telemetry and flag any of the following:
  • Highly regular or periodic connections — beaconing pattern
  • Anomalously high or low data volume per session
  • Connection attempts that persist even after repeated failures
  • Short-lived but very frequent connections
4
Analyse Protocol & Port Usage
1
Review and flag anomalies:
  • Non-standard ports for the observed protocol or service
  • Protocol inconsistent with the source system's role (e.g. DNS-over-HTTPS from a workstation to an unknown IP)
  • Encrypted traffic (TLS/HTTPS) to newly registered or unknown destinations
5
Review Destination Reputation & Context
1
Enrich the destination. Flag:
  • Newly registered domain (within last 30 days)
  • Poor or unknown IP reputation
  • Hosting provider or bulletproof hosting ASN
  • Infrastructure consistent with known threat patterns or malware families
6
Correlate with Historical & Lateral Traffic
1
Search historical logs for the same destination. Determine: first-seen timestamp, whether other hosts communicate with the same destination, and whether activity is expanding or isolated. This identifies patient zero and campaign scope.
7
Correlate with Endpoint & Identity Activity
1
Where possible, cross-reference with: endpoint process and execution data (does the traffic match a suspicious process?), identity sign-in events (does the timing align with anomalous logins?), and MDR correlation findings.
8
Build a Traffic Timeline
1
Construct a timeline showing: initial network activity, frequency and evolution over time, and correlated endpoint and identity events. This timeline is the primary artefact for escalation decisions.
9
Assess Verdict & Summarise

✅ Benign

Explainable, expected traffic

⚠️ Suspicious

Anomalies — escalate for review

🟠 Likely Malicious

Multiple signals — invoke playbook

🔴 Confirmed Malicious

Clear indicators — escalate immediately

📌 Prepare a summary with: traffic characteristics, indicators involved, correlations found, confidence level, and recommended next step — monitor, escalate, or invoke NW-RB-02 / NW-WI-02.

Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Clear understanding of network activity characteristics and threat classification
✅ Traffic timeline built and correlated with endpoint and identity signals
✅ Evidence ready to support escalation or closure — no network changes made

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Blocking based on a single indicator without completing analysis — always finish the WI first
Ignoring outbound traffic patterns — most C2 and exfiltration is outbound
Assuming all encrypted traffic is benign — C2 frequently uses HTTPS to blend in
Skipping endpoint and identity correlation — network indicators alone rarely confirm compromise
Failing to build a timeline — without sequencing, it is impossible to identify patient zero or campaign scope
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Beaconing or C2-like periodic patterns observed
Data exfiltration suspected — sustained outbound to unknown destinations
Multiple hosts communicating with the same suspicious destination
Traffic aligns with confirmed endpoint or identity compromise
Network activity persists or escalates over the analysis period
