NW-RB-04: Apply DoS Mitigations

Controlled procedure for applying local denial-of-service mitigations before escalation to upstream ISP or DDoS provider support. Use when attack traffic is affecting service availability but can still be influenced by firewall, WAF, load balancer, rate-limit, or routing controls.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a repeatable and auditable procedure to reduce the impact of denial-of-service activity using local controls while preserving service availability and avoiding unnecessary blocking of legitimate users.

This runbook is typically invoked by NW-PB-05 and precedes NW-RB-05: Escalate to ISP / DDoS Provider when local controls are insufficient.

Section 2
Required Access
🔥 Firewall / edge security gateway🛡️ WAF or reverse proxy⚖️ Load balancer or application gateway📊 Monitoring and traffic analytics🎫 SOC ticketing system
Section 3
Inputs Required
🌐 Target service / public IP / FQDN📈 Traffic volume and pattern🔢 Protocols, ports, URIs, or methods targeted🧭 Source geography / ASN / IP ranges where available🎫 Incident reference ID
Section 4
Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Validate the Availability Impact
1
Confirm whether users are experiencing degraded or unavailable service
2
Validate the issue is attack traffic, not application failure, ISP outage, DNS misconfiguration, or capacity exhaustion
3
Record baseline traffic, error rates, CPU, memory, connection counts, and service health
2
Classify the Attack Pattern
Observed patternTypical local mitigation
High-rate TCP/UDP trafficRate limit, connection limit, block obvious abusive sources, prepare ISP escalation
HTTP flood or repeated URI requestsWAF rule, bot challenge, method restriction, URI rate limit
Authentication or API endpoint abusePer-source rate limit, geo control, temporary access rule, API throttling
Single geography / ASN concentrationTemporary geo or ASN restriction with business-owner approval
3
Select and Approve Mitigation
1
Select the least disruptive control that reduces attack traffic while preserving legitimate access
Prefer rate limiting and application-aware controls before broad network blocking.
2
Obtain emergency change approval where the mitigation may impact users, countries, partners, or production service paths
3
Record intended scope, duration, owner, rollback condition, and monitoring window
4
Apply Local Controls
1
Apply scoped firewall, WAF, load balancer, reverse proxy, or application gateway rule
2
Avoid permanent deny rules unless the indicator has been validated and business impact is understood
3
Tag emergency rules with incident reference, owner, expiry date, and rollback notes
5
Validate Effectiveness
1
Monitor traffic reduction, service availability, error rate, and latency after control deployment
2
Confirm legitimate users can still access the service from expected regions and networks
3
Escalate to NW-RB-05 if local controls are ineffective or traffic volume exceeds edge capacity
6
Rollback or De-escalate
1
When traffic normalises, remove temporary rules in a controlled order
2
Monitor for resurgence for the agreed observation window
3
Convert only validated, recurring protections into permanent hardening rules through normal change control
Section 5
Expected Outcome

✅ Attack traffic reduced or controlled locally
✅ Service availability improved or stabilised
✅ Clear escalation threshold established for upstream mitigation

Section 6
Validation Checks
  • Service health restored or materially improved
  • Legitimate traffic is not unnecessarily blocked
  • Temporary mitigation rules have owner, expiry, rollback, and incident reference
  • Monitoring confirms no parallel intrusion activity hidden by the DoS event
Section 7
Common Errors & Tips
Do not apply broad geo-blocking without business approval — humanitarian, education, or partner operations may depend on cross-border access.
Rate limiting is usually safer than blocking where attack sources are distributed or mixed with legitimate users.
DoS activity may be a distraction. Continue reviewing identity, EDR, and network telemetry for intrusion indicators.
Temporary emergency rules must not become permanent undocumented exceptions.
Section 8
Output Artefacts
Emergency change record
Mitigation rule evidence
