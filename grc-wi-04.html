GRC-WI-04: Reviewer Work Instructions — General Suppliers

Practical guidance for assessing suppliers providing goods, licences, equipment, accessories, professional services, and other products outside the SaaS and on-prem application support models.

Approved
Reviewer focus areas
Focus especially on
Whether the supplier is truly low-risk or whether hidden hosting or support elements exist
Whether products, licences, and components are authentic and supportable
Whether the supplier may handle sensitive information, site access, or network-connected equipment
Whether fulfilment relies on third parties or resellers that are not clearly disclosed
Whether there is a credible route for product security advisories, recalls, defects, and issue escalation
Pre-review checks
Confirm before scoring
  • The exact products or services being supplied are known
  • The business owner is identified
  • Whether any information handling is expected
  • Whether site access or installation activity is expected
  • Whether any supplied item connects to organisational networks or systems
  • Whether this supplier should remain under the general supplier track or be redirected

If this information is incomplete — return for clarification before scoring.

Review method
Completeness, Evidence & Gap Identification

📋 Completeness check — questionnaire should explain

  • What is actually being supplied
  • Whether any hidden service component exists
  • Whether products are authentic and traceable
  • Whether confidentiality and information handling are addressed
  • Whether third parties or resellers are involved
  • Whether there is a route for notifying product or security issues

📄 Acceptable evidence

Authorised reseller confirmation
Licence entitlement documentation
Product authenticity or provenance statements
Confidentiality terms or NDA clauses
Security advisory or patch communication examples
Site access and delivery control procedures
Disposal or return process summaries

🚩 Gap indicators — look for:

Vague statements about 'supply only' where installation or configuration support is actually involved
Unclear origin of hardware, software, or replacement parts
Lack of clarity on who really fulfils or services the order
Products that connect to the network but with no patch or advisory model
Field engineers or delivery personnel with site access but no control explanation
Returns or repairs involving storage media with no data protection arrangement
Outcome determination
Scoring & Outcomes

Use GRC-FRM-11 and apply the 0–5 scale based on control existence, maturity and consistency, evidence strength, and appropriateness for the risk level.

Do not keep the supplier on this track if the responses indicate hosted services or privileged support activity — redirect to the appropriate specialist model.

Reviewer judgement
Apply stronger scrutiny where
  • Provides network-connected equipment, appliances, or embedded technology
  • Supplies firmware, drivers, scripts, or utilities
  • Handles floorplans, site details, asset data, or sensitive operational information
  • Accesses restricted premises, comms rooms, CCTV areas, or security-sensitive sites
  • Relies heavily on resellers, field engineers, or third-party logistics chains
  • May receive returned devices or storage media containing data

🛡️ Internal compensating controls to consider:

Internal network isolation of supplied equipment
Internal licence verification and asset management
Internal escort requirements for site visits
Internal sanitisation before return or disposal
Internal testing before deployment of supplied software or firmware
Suggested follow-up questions
Use where responses are weak
Please confirm whether any hosted or remote managed service element is included.
Please identify whether supplied equipment will connect to our network or require software updates.
Please explain how you verify product authenticity and avoid counterfeit parts.
Please identify all resellers, subcontractors, field engineers, or logistics partners involved.
Please confirm how security advisories, product recalls, or end-of-support notices are communicated.
Please explain how returned equipment, media, or failed components are handled where customer data may exist.
Please confirm whether your personnel require access to restricted sites or sensitive information.
Common pitfalls
Avoid
Treating all goods suppliers as low risk by default
Overlooking embedded software, firmware, or network connectivity
Accepting reseller status without confirming authenticity and traceability
Ignoring site access risk for installers, couriers, or field engineers
Overlooking confidentiality risk for drawings, asset records, and operational details
Failing to redirect suppliers into the correct specialist assessment track when needed
