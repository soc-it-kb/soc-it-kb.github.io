IT-TI-WI-01: Consuming & Evaluating TI Feeds and Sources

Step-by-step instructions for assessing, onboarding, and maintaining threat intelligence feeds and sources — ensuring only relevant, quality sources are consumed.

Prerequisites
Access to TI platform or feed aggregator List of current approved sources Understanding of organisation's sector and technology stack
Step-by-step instructions
1
Identify candidate source — feed, advisory service, ISAC membership, or commercial platform
2
Assess source quality: check publishing frequency, indicator volume, false positive reputation, and sector relevance
3
Check for overlap with existing sources to avoid duplicate ingestion and noise
4
Evaluate format: confirm the source provides structured data (STIX/TAXII, CSV, JSON, MISP) or has an accessible API
5
Run a trial period (2–4 weeks) — ingest in monitoring-only mode, do not deploy to blocking controls
6
Review trial results: measure hit rate, false positive rate, and relevance to your environment
7
If approved — add to the authorised source register, configure automated ingestion, and document refresh frequency
8
If not approved — document the reason and do not onboard. Review the candidate source again in 6 months if circumstances change
9
Review all active sources quarterly — retire sources that are stale, low-quality, or no longer relevant
Validation & verification
Source is documented in the authorised source register with owner and review date
Ingestion pipeline confirmed working — indicators flowing correctly
Trial results reviewed and documented before full onboarding
Refresh frequency configured and tested
Common errors & tips
More feeds does not mean better intelligence — five high-quality, relevant feeds beat fifty noisy ones every time
Always trial in monitoring-only mode first. Blocking based on an untested feed can disrupt legitimate services
CISA KEV, Abuse.ch (URLhaus, MalwareBazaar, ThreatFox), and AlienVault OTX are excellent free starting points
Check if your EDR or SIEM vendor already provides a TI feed — avoid duplicating what you already have
