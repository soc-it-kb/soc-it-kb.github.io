EP-WI-02: Isolate Endpoint Safely

Tool-agnostic procedure for safely isolating a suspected compromised endpoint — preventing lateral movement, C2 communication, exfiltration, and ransomware spread once approved.

💻 Endpoint threats Tier 1 / 2 Requires approval Containment action

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a consistent, tool-agnostic procedure to safely isolate a suspected compromised endpoint — preventing lateral movement, C2 communication, data exfiltration, and ransomware spread.

📌 Scope boundary: This WI does not decide when to isolate. It defines how to isolate safely once authorised by Tier-2 or via SOAR. Isolation is containment only — it does not remediate.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
🚫 Mandatory constraints — confirm before proceeding
Do NOT isolate a domain controller or core server without Tier-2 approval
Do NOT isolate a life-critical or safety system under any circumstances without senior approval
Do NOT isolate without documented approval
Do NOT reboot the endpoint before or during isolation

⚠️ If the endpoint is business-critical — escalate to Tier-2 before isolating. Isolation of servers or shared infrastructure can cause service outages.

Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Validate Endpoint Identity
1
Locate the endpoint in telemetry or asset inventory. Confirm hostname, assigned user, and last seen time match the incident scope.
Isolating the wrong endpoint is a serious operational error — always verify before acting.
2
Confirm Isolation Method

✅ EDR network isolation (preferred)

Fastest, most reliable. Maintains EDR management channel for investigation.

⚠️ Network-based isolation (VLAN / NAC)

Use when EDR isolation is unavailable. Requires Network Ops coordination.

🔴 Manual network disconnection

Last resort only. Loses remote access — use only when other methods fail.

3
Execute Isolation
1
Initiate endpoint isolation using the approved control. Record: isolation type, timestamp, and operator.
2
Confirm the action completed successfully in the platform
4
Validate Isolation State
1
Verify the endpoint is no longer communicating with internal network resources or external internet destinations
2
Confirm the endpoint remains reachable via EDR management channel (if applicable)
If isolation fails — do not retry repeatedly. Escalate to Tier-2 immediately.
5
Preserve Evidence
1
After isolation — do not take any of the following actions:
  • Do not reboot the endpoint
  • Do not remove files or processes
  • Do not notify the user
Isolation is containment only — not remediation. Premature actions destroy forensic evidence.
6
Notify & Document
1
Notify SOC Tier-2, MDR partner (if applicable), and IT Operations (if user impact expected). Use approved internal channels only — do not contact the user directly.
2
Update the incident record with: endpoint identifier, isolation method, timestamp, validation outcome, and any issues. Attach screenshots or confirmation logs.
Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Endpoint fully isolated from the network
✅ Threat spread contained — lateral movement, C2, and exfiltration blocked
✅ Endpoint remains available for investigation via EDR channel
✅ Audit trail documented in incident record

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Isolating the wrong endpoint due to hostname mismatch — always verify identity first
Isolating without documented Tier-2 approval
Rebooting the endpoint before investigation — this destroys volatile evidence
Informing the user prematurely — this can alert an insider threat or cause the attacker to pivot
Isolating servers or shared infrastructure without Tier-2 and Network Ops approval
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Endpoint isolation fails or cannot be confirmed
Multiple endpoints appear affected — possible lateral movement or ransomware spread
Endpoint is a server, domain controller, or critical infrastructure
Isolation causes unexpected business impact
Signs of ransomware or lateral movement increase after isolation attempt
