IT-GOV-SOP-02: Baseline Drift & Periodic Validation

Process for detecting, managing, and remediating baseline drift and conducting periodic validation across all endpoints and servers.

Section 1
Purpose

This SOP defines the process for detecting, managing, and remediating baseline drift, and for conducting periodic validation of IT Operations & Security Baselines across endpoints, servers, and Tier-0 assets.

📌 Baselines are continuous obligations, not one-time activities. Drift is expected — unmanaged drift is unacceptable.

Section 2
Baseline Drift Sources

Baseline drift may occur due to emergency troubleshooting, patching or system upgrades, application changes, manual configuration changes, or legacy system constraints. Not all drift is malicious — but all drift should be visible and assessed.

Section 3
Validation Frequency
Asset ClassMinimum Frequency
Domain Controllers (Tier-0)Monthly + post-change
Windows ServersQuarterly
Windows EndpointsBi-annually
High-Risk EndpointsQuarterly

⚠️ Additional validation is required after incidents, rebuilds, and major configuration or access changes.

Section 4
Drift Detection & Response
Drift TypeRequired Action
ApprovedMonitor until exception expiry
OperationalRemediate or document exception
UnapprovedImmediate remediation or escalation

⚠️ High-risk drift on Tier-0 assets requires immediate Cyber Security involvement.

Section 5
Roles & Responsibilities
RoleResponsibility

Cyber SecurityDefine baselines and assess risk
Asset OwnerOwn remediation timelines
Governance BoardOversight of systemic drift
Section 6
Governance & Review

Owner: Cyber Security / IT Operations  ·  Review Cycle: Annual  ·  Triggers: Major incidents, significant drift trends, baseline updates
