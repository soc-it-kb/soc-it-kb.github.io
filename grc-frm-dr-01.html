GRC-FRM-DR-01 — Disaster Recovery Runbook Template

Template populated by agency IT teams for each Tier-1 and Tier-2 IT service per GRC-DR-SOP-01 and GRC-DR-WI-01. The runbook describes how a specific IT service is recovered: architecture, backup & replication design, recovery procedures by scenario, validation steps, contacts. Owned by the named IT Service Owner; reviewed annually.

Per IT service Tier-1 & Tier-2 Annual review Worked example: WI-03
How to use this template. Complete one runbook per Tier-1 (Mission-critical) and Tier-2 (Business-critical) IT service. Drives from the recovery tier classification in GRC-DR-WI-01. For Tier-3 services a recovery approach is documented (not a full runbook); Tier-4 only requires a backup approach. See GRC-DR-WI-03 for a populated worked example covering five core IT services. Fields shown as [Fill] are placeholders the agency replaces.

The protective state from which recovery proceeds. Specifics matter — “daily backup” is not enough; the recovery plan needs to know what is held where, for how long, and how immutable it is.

Backup type and tooling
[Snapshot, image-level, file-level, application-aware; backup product or platform native capability]
Backup frequency
[Per the tier minimum from WI-01 or tighter — e.g. Tier-1: every 15 minutes; Tier-2: every 4 hours; specifically by data class if different]
Retention
[Retention periods by backup type. Immutable copy retention specifically — minimum 14 days per DR policy §5]
Immutable / WORM copies
[Where the immutable copies live; the mechanism (cloud immutability, tape WORM, isolated repository); the rotation schedule]
Per DR policy §5.3: at least one immutable copy of every Tier-1 / Tier-2 service backup exists outside the production blast radius for cyber resilience.
Replication pattern
[For Tier-1: synchronous or near-synchronous to secondary region/site, with named target. For Tier-2: asynchronous to secondary region/site. Specifically: what is replicated (data layer / application layer / configuration), and what is not (e.g. ephemeral state).]
Backup monitoring & alerting
[How backup success / failure is observed. Alerting thresholds and escalation. Per DR policy §5: Tier-1/2 alert within 4 hours of detection; sustained failure escalates to Service Owner and IT DR Coordinator.]

5 — Recovery procedures by scenario

A runbook addresses multiple scenarios that affect the service differently. Document each plausible scenario; the response sequence is what someone executing under pressure needs.

Scenario 1 — Component / hardware failure
[Steps for a single-component failure within the service. Trigger to invocation, sequence, validation, return to steady state. Where HA is designed in, the procedure may be largely automated; document the human steps regardless.]
Scenario 2 — Software corruption / data loss
[Steps for application-level or data-level loss: recovery from version history, recycle bin, or point-in-time restore. Reconciliation if loss window straddles a critical change. Hand-off to the dependent business process.]
Scenario 3 — Site / region failure
[Failover procedure to secondary site / region. Sequencing relative to foundation services; cross-workload dependencies. For Azure workloads, reference the relevant scenario in GRC-DR-WI-02.]
Scenario 4 — Cyber event affecting this service
[Recovery from ransomware, destructive attack, or compromise of this service. SOC owns the response side under GRC-IRP-01; this scenario documents the recovery-to-operations side that runs in parallel. Includes: do not restore configurations or data that may be tampered without SOC clearance; restore from immutable copies; validate integrity before reopening to dependent services.]
Scenario 5 — Cloud-specific (where applicable)
[For cloud-resident services: tenant lockout, tenant compromise, SaaS provider disruption. Reference the relevant scenarios in GRC-DR-WI-02. Do not duplicate that content here; describe what is specific to this service.]
Additional scenarios
[Any service-specific scenarios not covered above — e.g. certificate expiry, integration failure with a specific upstream / downstream system, regulatory disruption]
Recovery sequencing across scenarios
[When multiple components or scenarios are in play simultaneously, what sequence does the runbook expect? Typically: foundation dependencies first, data layer second, application layer third, integrations last.]
6 — Validation

Concrete checks after recovery. “The service is up” means the validation passes, not just that the platform health endpoint is green.

Functional validation
[Specific transactions or operations that exercise the service end-to-end. Sample sign-in, sample read, sample write; integration with downstream system tested; reconciliation of state with recently-recovered upstream service.]
Data integrity validation
[Spot-checks of recovered data against known-good reference. Backup-vs-source counts; checksum where applicable; sample record verification.]
Security validation
[Authentication enforcement verified; authorisation enforced; logging flowing to SIEM; expected SOC alerting active; no anomalous configuration drift detectable post-recovery.]
Performance & monitoring
[Baseline performance metrics within expected range; monitoring active; alerting functioning. Sustained monitoring window post-recovery before declaring fully recovered.]

Runbook completeness checklist

Before signing off a runbook, the IT Service Owner confirms:

  • The RTO and RPO match the tier classification (or are tighter if driven by a specific Tier-1 dependent process). No silent downgrade to current capability.
  • The backup design meets the tier minima from WI-01 — including the immutable-copy requirement.
  • At least one Tier-1 / Tier-2 recovery scenario has been exercised within the cadence; first runbooks require an exercise within 6 months of publication.
  • A restore test has been performed within the cadence and the result documented.
  • Critical dependencies are listed; any dependency at a lower tier than this service has been raised to the IT DR Coordinator.
  • The cyber-event recovery scenario (Section 5.4) names SOC involvement and integrity-validation steps before restoring.
  • For Tier-1: an offline / out-of-band copy has been produced and is held by the named role.
  • Vendor support contacts include the severity-A path with account ID verified within the last review.
  • Recent material changes (architecture, dependencies, personnel) are reflected.
