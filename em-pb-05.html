EM-PB-05: Ransomware Activity

High-urgency decision and response framework for detecting, containing, and coordinating response to ransomware activity — including blast-radius control, backup protection, and evidence preservation.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a strict, high-urgency decision and response framework for detecting, containing, and coordinating response to ransomware activity, ensuring immediate blast-radius control, protection of backups, and preservation of evidence.

This playbook represents a business-critical incident class and triggers the Cyber Incident Response Plan.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • Confirmed ransomware encryption activity
  • Pre-encryption ransomware behaviour (mass file access, shadow copy deletion)
  • Ransomware detected by EDR or MDR
  • Endpoints and servers with active encryption or ransom notes

❌ Out of scope

  • Suspected malware without encryption behaviour → EP-PB-02
  • Pure data exfiltration without encryption → Data playbooks
Section 3
Trigger Conditions

Initiate this playbook immediately when any of the following occur:

  • EDR detects ransomware behaviour or active encryption
  • MDR escalates confirmed ransomware activity
  • SOC observes mass file modifications or shadow copy deletion
  • Ransom note creation detected on any system
  • Multiple endpoints report simultaneous file encryption
Section 4
Initial Validation (Decision Logic)

Confirm ransomware activity by checking all indicators:

  • Are files being encrypted or renamed with unknown extensions?
  • Is a ransom note present on any system?
  • Are backup or recovery services being disabled?
  • Is encryption occurring across multiple directories or systems?

If NO → Route to EP-PB-02 (malware) or EP-PB-04 (defence evasion)
If YES to any → Proceed immediately with ransomware response — do not delay

Section 5
Ransomware Assessment

Assess impact and progression. Presence of any single indicator confirms a Ransomware Incident:

Active encryption ongoing
Multiple endpoints affected
Server or file share impacted
Backup deletion attempted
Ransom note deployed

Section 7
Response Strategy

The following actions must be orchestrated immediately and in parallel:

Immediate endpoint isolationCritical
Stop encryption activityCritical
Protect backups from encryption or deletionCritical
Credential exposure containmentCritical
Scope expansion across environmentCritical
Incident Command activationCritical

⚠️ Speed of isolation takes precedence over forensic completeness in active encryption scenarios. Isolate first, investigate second.

Section 9
Containment Decision Logic

Containment must be immediate and aggressive. Execute in this order:

  • Immediately isolate all infected endpoints and those showing early encryption indicators
  • Apply network segmentation to prevent access to file shares, domain controllers, and backup systems
  • If encryption is widespread — escalate to environment-level containment and consider controlled shutdown of affected segments
Section 10
Scope Expansion Logic

Scope expansion is mandatory and continuous throughout this incident:

  • Identify all endpoints with encrypted files
  • Search for identical ransomware artefacts across the environment
  • Identify the patient-zero endpoint
  • Correlate email delivery, identity compromise, and lateral movement paths
  • Assess backup integrity and exposure
Section 11
Communication & Coordination
  • Activate Incident Command immediately upon confirmation
  • Maintain a single communication channel for all incident updates
  • Restrict technical details to need-to-know personnel only
  • Do not communicate with the attacker without executive and legal approval
  • Align all actions with MDR partner and legal guidance
Section 12
Closure Criteria

The incident may be closed only when all of the following are confirmed:

  • All ransomware activity fully stopped
  • All affected systems isolated, rebuilt, or restored
  • All credentials reset and access paths secured
  • Backups validated and restoration completed or confirmed viable
  • Root cause identified and documented
  • Executive sign-off obtained
Section 13
Evidence to Capture
Ransomware sample hashes
Ransom note content
Encryption file extensions
Process trees and command lines
Network indicators (C2 IPs, domains)
Timeline of infection and containment
Section 14
Post-Incident Actions
  • Full root cause analysis
  • Backup and recovery capability review
  • Endpoint and identity hardening
  • Detection and prevention tuning
  • Executive and board-level reporting
  • Table-top or live exercise review
  • Update BCP / DR and ransomware runbooks
