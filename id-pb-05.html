ID-PB-05: Privileged Account Compromise

Strict, high-assurance decision and response framework for suspected or confirmed compromise of privileged identities — ensuring immediate containment, minimised blast radius, and preservation of forensic evidence.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a strict, high-assurance decision and response framework for handling suspected or confirmed compromise of privileged identities, ensuring immediate containment, minimised blast radius, and preservation of forensic evidence.

This playbook recognises that privileged compromise is a business-critical incident with enterprise-wide impact potential.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • Global / Tenant administrators
  • Privileged role holders (Entra ID, Azure, M365)
  • On-prem AD Tier-0 / Tier-1 accounts (where integrated)
  • Break-glass or emergency access accounts
  • Privileged access discovered during ID-PB-01 → ID-PB-04

❌ Out of scope

  • Service principals without human access → IAM governance
  • Non-privileged user compromise → ID-PB-01
Section 3
Trigger Conditions
  • MDR escalates suspected privileged account compromise
  • SOC identifies anomalous sign-in for a privileged role holder
  • Privileged account involved in a phishing, MFA abuse, or OAuth incident
  • Unexpected role assignment or privilege elevation detected
  • User reports unusual admin activity or unexpected alerts
Section 4
Initial Validation (Decision Logic)

Confirm the account holds privileged access:

  • Does the account hold any admin or elevated role?
  • Is the role permanently assigned or eligible via PIM?
  • Does the observed activity align with a known and approved change window?

If NO privileged role → Route to ID-PB-01 (Account Compromise)
If YES privileged role confirmed → Proceed with privileged compromise assessment immediately

Section 5
Privileged Compromise Assessment

Assess credibility and impact. Presence of any indicator = Suspected Privileged Compromise:

Successful admin sign-in from unusual or unfamiliar location
MFA abuse or bypass detected on the privileged account
OAuth consent granted by an admin account
Privilege escalation chain detected in audit logs
Admin actions performed outside an approved change window
Section 6

Section 7
Response Strategy

The following actions must be orchestrated immediately and in parallel:

Privileged session containment — revoke all admin tokensCritical
Role assignment review — audit all recent changesCritical
Credential resetCritical
MFA enforcement and re-registrationCritical
OAuth / token reviewCritical
Sign-in and admin audit log reviewCritical
Scope expansion — mandatory for all ID-PB-05Critical
Change freeze (if widespread admin activity suspected)Conditional

Section 9
Privilege Containment Logic

During response, validate and correct the privileged access posture:

Role type

Are admin roles permanent or eligible (PIM)? Eliminate permanent assignments where possible

PAM / JIT

Is Privileged Access Management or Just-In-Time access enforced for this account?

Break-glass

Are break-glass / emergency access accounts untouched and secured? Verify immediately

Role changes

Were any new roles assigned to or removed from accounts during the incident window?

⚠️ If privilege abuse is confirmed → enforce least-privilege immediately and escalate to Identity Engineering for a full role review

Section 10
Scope Expansion Logic

Scope expansion is mandatory for all ID-PB-05 incidents:

Review actions

  • Review all admin sign-ins within the incident timeframe
  • Identify lateral movement across identity accounts
  • Review all recent role assignments tenant-wide
  • Validate no changes to security controls occurred

Correlation actions

  • Correlate with email threat telemetry
  • Correlate with OAuth consent activity
  • Correlate with endpoint telemetry
  • Cross-reference with open MDR incidents
Section 11
Communication & Coordination
  • Maintain restricted, need-to-know communications throughout
  • Coordinate remediation timing carefully with IT leadership
  • Avoid alerting the attacker through premature user contact or public communications
  • Align all actions tightly with MDR and the Incident Response command structure

⚠️ Do not contact the compromised account holder until containment is confirmed — an attacker monitoring the account may destroy evidence or escalate their activity

Section 12
Closure Criteria

The incident may be closed only when all of the following are confirmed:

Privileged credentials reset and re-secured
All sessions and tokens fully revoked
Role assignments validated and corrected
No residual access observed across any channel
Forensic review completed and documented
Executive sign-off obtained where required
Section 13
Evidence to Capture
Privileged sign-in logs
Admin audit logs
Role assignment history
MFA and authentication events
OAuth consent artefacts
Timeline of all containment actions
Section 14
Post-Incident Actions
  • Mandatory root cause analysis
  • Review privileged access model — PAM, JIT, MFA strength, role hygiene
  • Update detection rules for privileged abuse patterns
  • Review and verify break-glass account protections
  • Conduct a table-top exercise or formal lessons-learned session
  • Update board-level risk reporting where applicable
