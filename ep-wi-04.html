EP-WI-04: Validate Persistence Removal & Recovery Readiness

Tool-agnostic procedure to confirm all persistence mechanisms have been removed and to assess whether the endpoint is ready to progress toward reconnection or rebuild.

💻 Endpoint threats Tier 1 / 2 Validation only No reconnection actions

Section 1
Purpose

Validate that all persistence mechanisms have been removed and assess endpoint readiness for recovery following malware eradication. Provides the evidence base for Tier-2 to authorise reconnection or rebuild.

📌 Scope boundary: Validation only. This WI does not authorise reconnection or rebuild — it produces the recommendation. Reconnection must be explicitly approved by Tier-2.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
🚫 Mandatory constraints — validation only
Endpoint must remain isolated throughout this entire WI
Do NOT reconnect the endpoint
Do NOT install new software or restore data
Do NOT notify the user
Do NOT perform additional malware removal in this WI — that belongs in EP-WI-03
Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Confirm Endpoint State
1
Verify endpoint is still isolated, stable (no crashes or boot loops), and that last seen telemetry is current. Confirm no new alerts triggered post-eradication.
2
Re-Check Known Persistence Locations
1
Review all persistence locations for absence of mechanisms:
  • Scheduled tasks
  • Services and drivers
  • Registry autorun locations
  • Startup folders
  • Login scripts
  • WMI event subscriptions
2
Confirm previously removed entries have not reappeared AND no new entries were created post-eradication.
If any persistence is detected — stop immediately and escalate to Tier-2.
3
Review Process & Startup Behaviour
1
Review recent process starts since eradication. Confirm no unknown or suspicious processes start automatically and no LOLBins are invoked at startup.
4
Validate Network Quiet State
1
While isolated, confirm no outbound connection attempts, no beaconing behaviour, and no DNS queries indicating background persistence activity.
If network activity persists while isolated — escalate to Tier-2. This indicates active persistence or a second implant.
5
Re-Scan Endpoint (Targeted)
1
Perform a targeted security scan (if available) to confirm no malware detections and no suspicious artefacts remain. Do not run aggressive remediation tools during this step.
6
Validate Identity & Credential Hygiene
1
Confirm with the incident record that user credentials have been reset (if required), OAuth/token persistence has been reviewed, and no pending identity actions remain. If identity actions are incomplete — do not progress recovery.
7
Assess Recovery Readiness

✅ Ready for reconnection

All checks passed — no persistence, quiet network, identity clean

⚠️ Requires rebuild

Persistent indicators remain or confidence is insufficient

🔴 Further investigation

New indicators found — stop and escalate to Tier-2

📌 Document the recommendation clearly with the specific evidence supporting the classification. Tier-2 makes the final reconnection or rebuild decision.

8
Preserve Evidence & Document
1
Preserve logs confirming absence of persistence, scan results, screenshots or telemetry evidence, and timeline of validation steps.
2
Update incident record with validation steps performed, findings (pass/fail per area), recovery readiness assessment, and recommendation to Tier-2.
Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Persistence mechanisms confirmed removed
✅ Endpoint state understood and documented
✅ Clear recovery or rebuild recommendation provided to Tier-2
✅ Evidence preserved for audit and IR record

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Skipping persistence locations — one missed mechanism means reinfection after reconnection
Assuming eradication equals readiness — always validate independently
Reconnecting endpoints prematurely without Tier-2 approval
Ignoring background network activity while isolated — this is a strong indicator of active persistence
Failing to verify identity actions are complete before recommending reconnection
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Persistence reappears during validation
New suspicious processes found post-eradication
Network activity continues while endpoint is isolated
Endpoint instability observed during validation
Endpoint is a server or privileged system
