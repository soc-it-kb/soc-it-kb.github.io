GRC-WI-03: Reviewer Work Instructions — On-Prem Application Support Suppliers

Practical guidance for reviewers assessing vendors and partners that support internally hosted applications.

Approved
Reviewer focus areas
Focus especially on
How access into the environment is established
Whether privileged access is controlled and attributable
Whether support actions are logged and reviewable
Whether live data may be exposed during troubleshooting
Whether patches and changes are controlled and tested
Whether subcontractors or partner engineers are used
Whether access is removed promptly when no longer required
Pre-review checks
Confirm before scoring
  • The supported application is clearly identified
  • The application owner is identified
  • Criticality and data sensitivity are known
  • The support model is understood
  • Whether production or privileged access is required
  • Whether the supplier uses subcontractors or offshore support

If this information is incomplete — return for clarification before scoring.

Review method
Completeness, Evidence & Gap Identification

📋 Completeness check — questionnaire should explain

  • Support model and access method
  • Systems and data the supplier may access
  • Engineer accountability model
  • Ticketing, approvals, and change control
  • Data handling during support
  • Subcontractor involvement
  • Incident notification and access suspension capability

📄 Acceptable evidence

Remote access standards or support security policies
Screenshots or summaries of MFA, VPN, PAM, or jump host controls
Support ticketing and approval workflow summaries
Session logging or remote support audit summaries
Endpoint security controls for engineer devices
Change control and patch communication examples
Subcontractor governance summaries

🚩 Gap indicators — look for:

Shared support accounts
Generic responses with no engineer accountability detail
Unclear access routes into production
Uncontrolled extraction of logs or data
Patching without formal testing or rollback planning
Subcontractors mentioned but not fully described
No clear route for urgent access revocation
Outcome determination
Scoring & Outcomes

Use GRC-FRM-09 and apply the 0–5 scale based on control existence, maturity and consistency, evidence strength, and appropriateness for the risk level.

✅ Approved
⚠️ Approved with Conditions
🔴 Escalate for Risk Acceptance
🚫 Not Approved

Do not rely on score alone where fundamental access or accountability weaknesses exist.

Reviewer judgement
Apply stronger scrutiny where
  • Supplier is business-critical
  • System contains personal, confidential, or sensitive data
  • Supports finance, legal, security, CCTV, or physical access functions
  • Requires remote privileged administration
  • Depends on a small number of vendor engineers or specialist subcontractors

🛡️ Internal compensating controls to consider:

Customer-controlled VPN and jump hosts
PAM or session recording on the customer side
Time-bound access approval workflows
Supervised support sessions
Internal testing before vendor-supplied patches are deployed
Suggested follow-up questions
Use where responses are weak
Please describe exactly how engineers connect to our environment.
Please confirm whether access is tied to named engineers and approved support tickets.
Please explain whether any shared admin accounts are used.
Please provide details of log retention and whether remote sessions are recorded.
Please confirm whether support staff can export customer data or diagnostics.
Please explain how vendor-provided patches are tested and rolled back if needed.
Please identify all subcontractors or partner engineers who may access our systems.
Please confirm how access is removed at contract end or when engineers leave the account.
Common pitfalls
Avoid
Accepting 'secure remote access' without understanding the actual method
Overlooking data extracted in logs, screenshots, or diagnostics
Assuming internal hosting removes supplier risk
Focusing only on the application and ignoring support engineer endpoint risk
Accepting subcontractor involvement without clarity on oversight or geography
Overlooking the need for strong access removal and exit controls
NIST CSF 2.0 alignment
Framework alignment
✅ GV — Govern: supplier accountability and approval decisions ✅ ID — Identify: support model, access profile, and third-party dependencies ✅ PR — Protect: access control, data handling, and change control ✅ DE — Detect: logging, session control, and monitoring of support activities ✅ RS — Respond: support-related incident handling and notification ✅ RC — Recover: resilience of support capability and recovery assistance
