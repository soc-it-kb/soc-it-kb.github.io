EM-PB-04: Business Email Compromise (BEC) Incident Response

Structured response framework for BEC attempts — covering executive impersonation, vendor fraud, invoice manipulation, and payment diversion scenarios.

📧 Email threats Tier 2 / 3 Finance coordination required

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a structured response to Business Email Compromise (BEC) attempts, including executive impersonation, vendor fraud, invoice manipulation, and payment diversion schemes.

Section 2
Scope
  • CEO / CFO fraud and executive impersonation
  • Vendor impersonation and supplier fraud
  • Fraudulent payment change requests
Section 3
Trigger Conditions
  • User reports a financial lure or urgent payment request
  • MDR escalation involving impersonation indicators
  • SOC analyst identifies executive or vendor impersonation
Section 4
Initial Validation

Confirm the following before proceeding to classification:

  • Does the sender display name impersonate an executive or trusted vendor?
  • Does the email convey financial urgency or pressure?
  • Does the email contain payment instructions or bank detail changes?

If YES to any → Treat as potential BEC and proceed immediately
If NO to all → Close or escalate for further review

Section 5
Classification

Classify the incident into one of the following categories:

⚠️ Attempted BECEmail received but no action taken by the recipient
🔴 Active BECRecipient has engaged with the fraudulent request
💸 Confirmed FraudFinancial transaction initiated or completed — immediate escalation required
Section 6
Escalation Criteria

Immediate escalation is required if any of the following apply:

🔴 A financial transaction has been initiated or completed
🔴 An executive or VIP user has been targeted
🔴 A repeated BEC campaign is identified targeting multiple users

Note: If a financial transaction has occurred, notify Finance immediately in parallel with the technical response — time is critical for potential transaction recall.
Section 7
Response Strategy

Execute the following response actions based on classification:

Contain and preserve email artefactsRequired
Coordinate with Finance teamRequired
Investigate mailbox for rules abuse or persistenceRequired
Initiate transaction recall (if fraud confirmed)Conditional
Reset compromised account credentialsConditional

Section 8
Runbook Invocation Matrix

Invoke the relevant execution runbooks based on the scenario:

Email header analysis EM-RB-01 › Email removal from mailboxes EM-RB-04 › Mailbox rule review & persistence check ID-RB-02 › Account reset / containment ID-RB-01 ›
Section 9
Closure Criteria

An incident may be closed when all of the following are confirmed:

  • Finance team has confirmed transaction status (cancelled, recalled, or accepted as loss)
  • All IOCs documented and shared with threat intelligence platforms
  • Security awareness update issued to relevant staff (if campaign-based)
Section 10
Evidence to Capture
Email artefacts (headers, body, attachments)
File / URL hashes
User interactions report
Finance communications record
Transaction records (if applicable)
