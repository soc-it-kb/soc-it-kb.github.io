ID-02: Privileged Account Abuse

Use this checklist to triage a privileged account alert. Answer each question and follow the decision logic — do not delay escalation on privileged account events.

🔐 Identity Tier 1 Published 20 Dec 2025
🔔 When to use this checklist
Privileged role change / admin login anomaly / MDR escalation involving a privileged or administrative account.

Step 3 — Apply decision logic
🔴
Any answer = YES — Escalate immediately to Tier-2. Do not wait for further investigation.
✅
Approval confirmed AND behaviour expected — Document and close the ticket with the change record reference attached.
Step 4 — Mandatory actions
Preserve all logs immediately — do not wait
Do NOT disable the privileged account without Tier-2 approval
Notify SOC Lead immediately
Attach all authentication evidence to the incident ticket
⚠️ Common Tier-1 mistakes to avoid
Treating admin alerts as routine noise — privileged account activity always warrants review
Assuming IT staff activity is automatically benign — staff credentials can be compromised
Delaying escalation to wait for more information — escalate first, investigate after
Performing containment actions without Tier-2 approval — disabling admin accounts can cause outages
💡
Analyst Tip
If a privileged account looks suspicious, treat it as compromised until proven otherwise. Speed matters more than certainty at Tier-1.
