IT-GOV-SOP-01: Baseline Exception & Risk Acceptance

Formal process for requesting, assessing, approving, documenting, and reviewing exceptions to approved IT Operations & Security Baselines.

ASM · IT Operations Published 12 January 2026
📋 SOP ID: IT-GOV-SOP-01
·
👤 Author: Paulo Ferreira
·
📅 Published: 12 January 2026
·
🔄 Version: v1.0
Section 1
Purpose

This SOP defines the formal process for requesting, assessing, approving, documenting, and reviewing exceptions to approved IT Operations & Security Baselines, and for the acceptance of residual risk where baseline controls cannot be fully implemented.

⚠️ The objective is to ensure that deviations from baseline security requirements are explicit, time-bound, risk-assessed, and approved — rather than implicit or undocumented. Unapproved deviations are considered non-compliant.

Section 2
Scope
  • All security baselines — endpoints, servers, infrastructure
  • All IT-managed and security-managed assets
  • All deviations from recommended baseline controls including technical limitations, operational constraints, legacy systems, and environmental constraints
Section 3
Guiding Principles

🔒 Security by default

Baselines are mandatory. Exceptions are temporary, not permanent design choices.

📋 Risk must be explicit

No implicit risk acceptance. All deviations must be knowingly accepted and documented.

🛡️ Compensating controls preferred

Risk reduction through alternative controls is preferred over pure risk acceptance.

👤 Accountability is mandatory

Every exception must have a named owner and an expiry date.

Section 4
When an Exception Is Permitted

Exceptions may be requested only when a baseline control cannot be implemented due to technical or operational constraints, implementation would cause unacceptable service disruption, a system is end-of-life or awaiting replacement, or external dependencies prevent compliance.

⚠️ Exceptions should never be used for convenience or to bypass security controls without justification.

Section 5
Exception Request Process
1
Request Submission
Requestor provides: asset identification, affected baseline control(s), reason for exception, business impact, proposed compensating controls, and requested expiry date.
2
Risk Assessment
Cyber Security assesses threat likelihood, impact of compromise, exposure duration, and existing controls. Outcome documented as Low, Medium, or High risk.
3
Approval Authority
Low risk → Cyber Security. Medium risk → Cyber Security + IT Owner. High risk → Cyber Security Governance Board.
4
Documentation
Approved exceptions recorded in the Exception Register, linked to affected assets, assigned an owner and expiry date.
Section 6
Expiry, Review & Renewal
  • All exceptions must have an expiry date
  • Exceptions are reviewed prior to expiry — renewal requires re-justification and re-approval
  • Expired exceptions without renewal revert to non-compliant status
Section 7
Roles & Responsibilities
RoleResponsibility
RequestorSubmit accurate exception request
IT OwnerOwn remediation and timeline
Cyber SecurityRisk assessment and approval
SOCMonitoring and validation
Governance BoardApprove high-risk exceptions
Section 8
Governance & Review

Owner: Cyber Security / IT Operations  ·  Review Cycle: Annual  ·  Triggers: Major incidents, audit findings, baseline updates
