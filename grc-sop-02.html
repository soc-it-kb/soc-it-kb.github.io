GRC-SOP-02: On-Prem Application Support Supplier Security Assessment Procedure

Steps for initiating, reviewing, scoring, approving, and tracking security assessments for vendors and partners that support internally hosted applications.

Procedure triggers
When to initiate this procedure
Onboarding a new application support supplier Renewing an existing support contract Introducing a new support model, partner, or subcontractor Enabling new remote access or privileged support access Making a material change to application architecture or support scope Responding to a support-related incident or assurance concern Carrying out a scheduled reassessment
Procedure steps
Step-by-step process
1
Establish Supplier Support Profile
Record supplier name, supported application, business/application owner, application criticality, data sensitivity, support type and hours, production access requirement, privileged/database access requirement, and subcontractor/offshore use
2
Triage Assessment Track
Determine whether the supplier should remain under the on-prem application support model or be redirected to the cloud/SaaS or general supplier assessment model
3
Issue Questionnaire
Issue GRC-FRM-08 — On-Prem Application Vendor and Support Partner Security Questionnaire with a return deadline and evidence request
4
Review Responses and Evidence
Review responses for completeness, consistency, technical credibility, and adequacy of evidence
5
Identify Gaps and Clarifications
Record any unclear, generic, unsupported, or incomplete responses and request clarification
6
Complete Scored Assessment
Complete GRC-FRM-09 — On-Prem Application Support Supplier Scored Assessment Record and calculate the weighted score
7
Determine Outcome
Recommend outcome based on scored assessment — see outcomes section below
8
Record Conditions and Actions
Where relevant, record access restrictions, monitoring requirements, remediation actions, contractual requirements, and reassessment dates

Mandatory escalation criteria
Escalate if any of the following apply
No MFA for remote or privileged support access
Shared or unattributable administrative access
No viable logging of remote support activity
No acceptable control over live data accessed during troubleshooting
Use of subcontractors without clear oversight or notification
No credible incident notification process
No formal change control for application patches or upgrades
Unresolved material incidents affecting customer environments or support tooling
Evidence expectations
Evidence may include
Support security policies or remote access standards
Independent assurance reports or certification summaries
Screenshots or summaries of VPN, jump host, PAM, MFA, or remote support controls
Sample change control process or maintenance workflow
Incident response or support continuity summaries
Subcontractor governance summaries
Secure support tooling and engineer endpoint control summaries

Records & maintenance
Records Management & Review

Assessment records shall be retained in the appropriate register in accordance with internal retention requirements. This procedure shall be reviewed annually or after material changes to supplier assessment requirements.
