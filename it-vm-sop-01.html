IT-VM-SOP-01: Vulnerability Management — Process & Procedures

Consistent, risk-based, and auditable vulnerability management process to identify, assess, prioritise, remediate, and validate security vulnerabilities across all in-scope systems.

Section 1
Purpose

This SOP establishes a consistent, risk-based, and auditable Vulnerability Management process to identify, assess, prioritise, remediate, and validate security vulnerabilities across all in-scope information systems. Vulnerabilities are addressed in a timely manner, aligned with business risk, and governed through clear ownership and accountability.

Section 2
Scope
  • End-user endpoints (workstations, laptops)
  • Servers (on-premises and cloud)
  • Network devices and security appliances
  • Operating systems, applications, and middleware
  • Internet-facing services and externally exposed assets

Out of scope: Zero-days prior to public disclosure (handled under Incident Response), assets under approved risk acceptance, unsupported legacy with documented exceptions.

Section 3
Vulnerability Management Lifecycle
1
Asset Discovery & Coverage Validation
Maintain authoritative asset inventory. Validate scanning and telemetry coverage. Identify and document visibility gaps.
2
Vulnerability Identification
Authenticated vulnerability scanning, EDR/XDR telemetry, configuration checks, threat intelligence and vendor advisories, penetration testing.
3
Assessment & Enrichment
Enrich with CVSS severity, asset criticality, exposure level, known exploitation status, and business impact. Risk assessed using risk-based approach — not CVSS alone.
4
Risk-Based Prioritisation
Threat intelligence and active exploitation status override raw CVSS scoring. Critical = actively exploited or externally exposed with high business impact.
5
Remediation Planning & Execution
Define action (patch, config change, control), assign owner, define SLA, reference change management. Emergency remediation may follow emergency change procedures.
6
Validation & Closure
Validate via re-scan or control verification. Confirm vulnerability no longer present. Close with evidence.
7
Exception & Risk Acceptance
Document risk, identify compensating controls, define expiry date, obtain business owner approval. All exceptions reviewed periodically.
Section 4
Remediation SLAs

Critical

≤ 7 days

High

≤ 30 days

Medium

≤ 90 days

Low

Best effort / scheduled

⚠️ Stricter SLAs apply to internet-facing or mission-critical assets.

Section 7
Metrics & Governance

Metrics monitored: MTTR, vulnerabilities by severity, SLA compliance, coverage percentage, re-opened vulnerabilities, top recurring root causes. Reviewed annually or following major incidents.
