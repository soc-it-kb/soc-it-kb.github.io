GRC-FRM-14: Incident Response Communication Templates

Approved templates for internal and external communications during cyber incident response. Each template specifies its audience, when to use it, the sign-off chain, draft text, and post-send actions. Use as starting points — never send without sign-off from CISO, Legal, and Privacy.

📌 How to use these templates. Each template is a draft starting point — not a finished message. Before any communication is sent: (1) replace all [bracketed placeholders] with verified facts; (2) confirm draft accuracy against the incident journal; (3) obtain the sign-offs listed for that template; (4) send through the channel the template specifies; (5) record the send in the incident journal.

Universal rule (per GRC-IRP-01 §13): No external communication is sent without CISO, Legal, and Privacy/DPO sign-off. CEO approves for Sev 1. PR holds the pen for external statements.

Holding statements: Where facts are still being established, use the holding-statement template (T7) rather than speculate.

Templates in this pack

T1 — Internal Staff Notification

GRC-FRM-14.T1
Audience

All staff, contractors, volunteers in affected operating unit(s)

When to use

Sev 3 with broad service impact; all Sev 1/2; whenever staff need to act or refrain from action

Channel

HR-controlled mass-mail + intranet banner; for Sev 1, also town-hall by senior leader

Frequency

Initial within 4h of incident becoming staff-visible; updates at agreed cadence; closure at end

Sign-off required

CISO confirms factual accuracy
Legal reviews wording (privilege, regulatory exposure)
Privacy/DPO reviews data-subject implications
HR signs off on tone, distribution list, accessibility
Sev 1: CEO/Agency Lead approves before send

Draft — Sev 3 (short)

Subject: Service issue affecting [service] — what to do Dear colleagues, We are responding to a service issue affecting [service / system] that started at approximately [time, timezone] on [date]. Our Cyber Security Operations team is investigating. What to do right now: • [specific action — e.g. avoid using X / use the manual fallback in Y / report any unusual emails to phishing@…] • Continue with normal duties otherwise. We will update you by [time]. If you have any concerns, contact [helpdesk / SOC channel]. [Sender — typically Head of IT or Comms lead]

Draft — Sev 2 (standard)

Subject: Important — cyber security incident affecting [service / area] Dear colleagues, We are responding to a cyber security incident that has affected [service / area] since [time, date]. We are taking it seriously and our specialist teams are responding under our incident response plan. What we know: [2–4 sentences of confirmed facts only — no speculation] What we are doing: [Containment / investigation / restoration steps in plain language] What we ask of you: • [Specific actions] • Do not share information about this incident outside the organisation, including on social media. • Forward any media enquiries to [PR contact]. • Report any suspicious activity to [SOC contact]. We will provide our next update by [time]. Your cooperation matters. Thank you. [Sender — typically CISO or CIO]

Draft — Sev 1 (extended)

Subject: Cyber security incident — message from [CEO / Agency Lead] Dear colleagues, I am writing to inform you that we are responding to a serious cyber security incident affecting [scope]. The incident was identified on [date] and our specialist teams are responding alongside external experts under our incident response plan. What we know: [Confirmed facts only — services affected, time period, what we know about the cause if we know it; otherwise say "the cause is being investigated"] Has any of our information been affected? [Honest current state: "we do not yet know" / "we have no evidence of data exposure" / "we have evidence that some data has been affected and are working to understand the extent"] What we are doing: • Specialist incident response teams have been engaged and are working alongside our internal teams. • The relevant authorities [have been / are being] informed in line with our legal obligations. • [Any service-restoration commitments — e.g. "we expect [service] to be restored by [time]; in the meantime, please use [alternative]"] • External communications about this incident are being handled centrally — please direct any media enquiry to [PR contact]. What we ask of you: • Continue with your work as normal where possible. • Do not discuss this incident outside the organisation or on social media. • Be alert for phishing or impersonation attempts referencing this incident. • Report anything suspicious to [SOC contact]. I know this is unsettling. We have prepared for this and are responding professionally. I will write again by [time], and at every significant development. [CISO / spokesperson] will be available at [time] to answer questions. Thank you. [CEO / Agency Lead]

Post-send actions

Record send time, channel, audience, and approver in the incident journal
Monitor staff queries through the helpdesk; capture FAQs for the next update
Schedule the next update before this one is sent

T2 — Executive Briefing (active Sev 1/2)

GRC-FRM-14.T2
Audience

CEO, CIO, CFO, COO, General Counsel, Head of HR, Head of PR

When to use

Every Sev 1; every Sev 2 with material exposure; cadence agreed at incident start

Channel

Secure email or controlled bridge; never general mail

Frequency

Sev 1: every 4–8 hours. Sev 2: twice daily. Plus on material change.

Sign-off required

CISO is the named author of every executive briefing
Legal reviews if briefing touches on liability, regulator status, or ransom

Draft

Subject: EXEC BRIEF — Incident [ID] — [time, date] Severity: [Sev N — descriptor] Status: [Active / Contained / Recovering / Closed] Phase: [Detect / Contain / Eradicate / Recover / Report] Incident Commander: [Name] Headline: [One sentence that, on its own, conveys the situation] What we know: • [Confirmed facts: scope, affected systems, affected data] • [Current containment status] • [Initial assessment of cause if known] What we don't yet know: • [Open questions being investigated] Decisions taken since last brief: • [Each decision with time and decision-maker] Decisions needed from this group: • [Pending decisions — ransom posture, public statement, regulator engagement, etc.] Regulator / insurer status: • Privacy authority: [notified / not yet / not required, with rationale] • Sector regulator: [status] • Cyber insurer: [notified at time / not yet] External engagement: • MDR / IR retainer: [engaged / on standby] • External counsel: [engaged / on standby] • Law enforcement: [engaged / under consideration / no] Communications status: • Internal: [last comm sent at time; next at time] • External: [holding statement issued / customer notification drafted / no external comm] Next update: [time] Bridge / war-room: [link / room] [CISO]

Post-send actions

File brief in the incident journal as a numbered version
Confirm receipt and any decisions back into the journal as decision records
Schedule next brief before this one is sent

T3 — Risk Committee / Board Notification

GRC-FRM-14.T3
Audience

Risk Committee Chair (Sev 1: also Board Chair)

When to use

Sev 1: within 1h of declaration. Sev 2: within 4h. Material updates thereafter.

Channel

Direct call followed by written summary

Format

Brief, factual, governance-framed; not technical

Sign-off required

CISO is the named sender
Legal informed of the brief content

Draft

Subject: Notification — Cyber Security Incident [ID] Dear [Chair name], I am notifying you under GRC-IRP-01 §8 of an active [Sev 1 / Sev 2] cyber security incident. Summary: [One paragraph in plain English] Risk register cross-reference: [RISK-NNN if the incident materialises a known risk; "new — to be added on closure" if not] Material implications under consideration: • Regulatory: [notification status] • Financial: [initial estimate or "being assessed"] • Reputational: [external visibility status] • Operational: [service-impact status] Response posture: • Incident Commander: [CISO / Deputy CISO] • External support engaged: [IR retainer / counsel / insurer] • Executive engagement: [CEO / GM brief at time] Decisions reserved to the Committee: [Any decisions that require Risk Committee endorsement — material acceptance, ransom posture, public statement] Next update: [Daily for Sev 1; on material change for Sev 2] I am available at [24/7 contact]. The full incident bridge is at [bridge details] if you wish to join. [CISO]

Post-send actions

Record receipt and any committee direction in the incident journal
Schedule a formal Risk Committee briefing within 30 days of incident closure

T4 — Regulator Notification (GDPR-pattern, 72h)

GRC-FRM-14.T4
Audience

Data Protection Authority (per local annex §6) and any sector regulators

When to use

Confirmed or reasonably suspected notifiable breach (per GRC-IRP-01 §9.2)

Channel & timing

Authority's prescribed portal/method per local annex; within 72h of awareness for GDPR; per local annex for others

Format

Authority-mandated form where one exists; otherwise this template

Sign-off required

Privacy / DPO drafts and signs
Legal reviews
CISO confirms factual content
CEO informed before submission for Sev 1

Draft

To: [Authority name] — [Notification portal / address] From: [Organisation legal name and registration] Reference: [Internal incident ID] Date of awareness: [Date and time the organisation became aware] Personal Data Breach Notification This notification is made under [Article 33 GDPR / equivalent local provision]. 1. Nature of the breach [Type of breach — confidentiality, integrity, availability — and a factual description of what occurred] 2. Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected [Categories — staff, customers, beneficiaries, etc. — and approximate count, with explanation if a precise figure is not yet known] 3. Categories and approximate number of personal data records affected [Types of personal data — names, contact details, financial, special category data, etc. — and approximate volume] 4. Likely consequences for data subjects [Risk assessment — financial loss, identity theft, distress, reputational damage, etc.] 5. Measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate adverse effects [Containment measures, eradication actions, monitoring, support to affected individuals] 6. DPO / contact for further information Name: [Name] Role: [Title] Phone: [Number] Email: [Email] 7. Where information is incomplete This notification is provided within the 72-hour period required by Article 33. The investigation is ongoing. We will provide further information as it becomes available, no later than [expected date]. Submitted on behalf of [Organisation] by: [DPO name and signature] [Date]

Post-send actions

Capture the authority's acknowledgement and reference number in the incident journal
Schedule the follow-up update before any deadline imposed by the authority
For NIS2 and other sector regimes, file separate notifications per local annex §7 with their respective timelines
Record the notification in the breach register

T5 — Customer / Beneficiary Breach Notification

GRC-FRM-14.T5
Audience

Affected individuals (customers, beneficiaries, partners)

When to use

Where breach is likely to result in high risk to data subjects' rights and freedoms (GDPR Art. 34) or where contractual / sector obligation requires

Channel

Individual letter / email; for very large breaches: public communication permitted under GDPR Art. 34(3)(c) where individual notification would be disproportionate

Language

Required language(s) per local annex §13

Sign-off required

Privacy/DPO drafts
Legal reviews
CISO confirms facts
PR reviews tone and accessibility
CEO/Agency Lead approves for material breaches

Draft

Subject: Important — information security incident affecting your data Dear [name], I am writing to let you know about an information security incident that may have affected information you have entrusted to us. What happened On [date], we identified [plain-language description]. We responded immediately and have taken steps to contain the incident. What information was involved The incident may have affected the following information about you: • [Specific data categories — be precise but plain] We have no evidence that the following information was involved: [any reassuring exclusions if true — passwords, payment cards, etc.]. What we are doing • [Containment / investigation / external support] • We have notified [relevant data protection authority]. • We are [providing X support — e.g. credit monitoring, password resets] to affected individuals. What you can do • [Specific recommended actions — change password, watch for unusual activity, etc.] • Be alert for unsolicited contact referencing this incident: we will not ask you for passwords, financial details, or credentials by phone or email. • If you have concerns, contact us at [dedicated contact] — we have set up a dedicated channel for this incident. I am sorry that this has happened. We take the security of your information seriously and we are working hard to make sure it does not happen again. We will write to you again if there is significant new information. Yours sincerely, [Senior leader — CEO or DPO] [Organisation] For more information about your rights regarding your personal data, you may contact our Data Protection Officer at [DPO contact], or your local data protection authority.

Post-send actions

Stand up a dedicated helpline / mailbox before sending — anticipate volume
Brief frontline staff on FAQs and approved answers
Track responses (acknowledgements, complaints, escalations) for trend analysis
Record the notification in the breach register and incident journal

T6 — Supplier / Partner Notification

GRC-FRM-14.T6
Audience

Suppliers, partners, or sub-processors whose data, systems, or services are or may be affected

When to use

Where our incident has affected a counterparty, or a counterparty needs to take protective action

Channel

Account-relationship channel; copy in their security contact if known

Note

For suppliers under contract, contractual notification clauses take precedence over this template

Sign-off required

CISO confirms facts
Legal reviews contractual obligations
Account/relationship owner sends

Draft

Subject: Notification of cyber security incident — action recommended Dear [contact], We are writing to notify you of a cyber security incident at [Organisation] that affects, or may affect, our relationship with [Their Organisation]. What happened [Plain-language summary, sized to what they need to act on] How this may affect you [Specific to their relationship: data they sent us, data they receive from us, shared system access, etc.] What we recommend • [Concrete recommended actions — rotate credentials, treat correspondence with caution, review specific records, etc.] What we are doing [Brief — containment, investigation, regulator status] Single point of contact For questions about this incident, please contact [named lead] at [contact]. We have established this single channel to ensure consistent and accurate information. We will provide a further update by [date], and earlier if there is material new information. Thank you for your understanding. [Senior account owner — CISO or designate]

Post-send actions

Track which suppliers/partners have been notified and their acknowledgements
Anticipate reciprocal notifications from them where the relationship is mutual
Capture their responses for the after-action report

T7 — Public Statement & Holding Statement

GRC-FRM-14.T7
Audience

Media, public, social-media followers

When to use

Public-facing service disruption; media enquiry; social-media exposure; regulatory or contractual public-disclosure obligation

Channel

Newsroom / press release; website banner; verified social channels

Spokesperson

Per local annex §12 — never an unbriefed spokesperson

Sign-off required

PR drafts
Legal reviews — every word
Privacy/DPO reviews any reference to data
CISO confirms factual accuracy
CEO/Agency Lead approves before release for Sev 1

Draft — Holding statement (use first, when facts are still being established)

[Organisation] — Statement on [date] We are aware of a cyber security incident affecting [service / area]. We are taking it seriously and are responding under our incident response plan, supported by external specialists. We will share more information as soon as we are able to do so accurately. In the meantime, [any specific public guidance — e.g. "customers should use our backup channel at X" / "no action is required from the public" / "we ask the public to be alert for impersonation attempts"]. Media enquiries: [PR contact]

Draft — Substantive public statement (once facts are confirmed)

[Organisation] — Statement on cyber security incident On [date], [Organisation] identified a cyber security incident affecting [scope]. What we know [Confirmed facts only — no speculation about cause or attribution unless cleared] What we are doing We responded immediately. [Containment / investigation / restoration]. We are working with external cyber security specialists and have notified [relevant authorities, where confirmed]. What this means for those affected [Specific to audience — customers, beneficiaries, partners. If individual notifications are being sent, say so.] What we ask [Be alert to phishing/impersonation; specific actions] We are sorry for any concern this incident causes and we are working hard to resolve it. We will update this statement as significant new information becomes available. Media enquiries: [PR contact] Customer enquiries: [dedicated channel] [Date and time of statement; version number]

Post-send actions

Brief the social-media team and the helpdesk before publication
Monitor coverage and reaction; agree the trigger for the next update
Update the statement at every material change — version every revision

T8 — Incident Closure ("All Clear") Notification

GRC-FRM-14.T8
Audience

Same audiences previously notified — staff, executives, regulators, customers, suppliers, public as applicable

When to use

Once Recover phase is complete and the IC formally closes the response phase

Channel

Same channels used during the response — match each prior recipient

Note

Closing the response phase is not the same as closing the incident — the Report & Remediate phases continue; closure communication acknowledges that

Sign-off required

CISO authorises closure
Same sign-off chain as the original notification for each audience

Draft — Internal staff "all clear"

Subject: Update — incident affecting [service / area]: services restored Dear colleagues, I am pleased to confirm that the incident I wrote to you about on [date] has been contained and the affected services have been restored as of [time, date]. What happened — final summary [Plain-language, factual summary now that facts are settled. Avoid the temptation to over-share investigative detail — be useful, not exhaustive.] What we have done [Concrete remediation completed; any residual restrictions; what we expect to be different] What we are still doing The response phase is closed; the formal post-incident review is underway. Lessons learned will be acted on through the normal cyber improvement programme. [Risk Committee briefing scheduled]. Thank you [Brief, sincere acknowledgement of teams that responded — SOC, IT, HR, PR, Legal, business stakeholders] Resume normal communications channels and processes. [CISO / CEO]

Draft — External "all clear" (where a public statement was made)

[Organisation] — Closing statement on cyber security incident On [date] we reported a cyber security incident affecting [scope]. We are now able to confirm that affected services have been restored and the incident has been contained. Final summary [Settled facts only] Outcome for those affected [Specific, e.g. "All affected customers have been individually notified and supported through [channel]" or "We have no evidence of any data exposure"] What happens next A formal post-incident review is underway. We will share what we have learned where it is in the public interest to do so. The dedicated channel at [contact] remains open until [date]. We are grateful to those who supported us — [as appropriate: authorities, partners, customers] — for their patience and cooperation. [Date]

Post-send actions

Send to every audience previously notified — match each prior recipient
Stand down the dedicated helpline / mailbox at the agreed date, with a redirect
Trigger the formal after-action report (AAR template below)
Update the breach register and risk register cross-references
