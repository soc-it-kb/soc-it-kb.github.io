SOP-EP-01: Endpoint Threat Operations

Standard Operating Procedure defining how the SOC detects, triages, investigates, escalates, and responds to endpoint-based threats — ensuring rapid containment, minimal spread, and effective recovery.

Cross-domain Tier 1 / 2 / 3 NIST CSF 2.0 ISO 27035

Section 1
Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines how the Security Operations Centre (SOC) detects, triages, investigates, escalates, and responds to endpoint-based threats, ensuring rapid containment, minimal spread, and effective recovery across all organisational endpoints.

Endpoints are treated as primary attack surfaces and initial access vectors. Compromise of an endpoint is assumed to present high lateral-movement and privilege-escalation risk until proven otherwise.

Section 2
Scope

Applies to

  • Global SOC (Tier 1–3)
  • Regional SOCs
  • MDR provider
  • Endpoint / Workplace IT teams
  • Local IT teams supporting remediation

In-scope threat categories

  • Malware (file-based and fileless)
  • Ransomware
  • Living-off-the-Land (LOLBins)
  • Exploitation of vulnerabilities
  • Persistence mechanisms
  • Lateral movement originating from endpoints

In-scope endpoint assets

  • User workstations (Windows, macOS, Linux)
  • Laptops — remote and high-risk locations
  • Servers where endpoint protection is deployed
  • Virtual desktops (VDI)

Section 6
Operating Procedure (High-Level)
1
Detection & Alert Intake

Endpoint threats may be detected via EDR/XDR alerts, SIEM correlations, MDR escalations, threat hunting activities, or reports from IT and users. All alerts must be logged in the incident management platform.

2
Initial Triage & Classification

The SOC must determine endpoint type (user, server, VDI), user context (standard, privileged, VIP), threat class (malware, ransomware, exploit, persistence), execution status (blocked, active, contained), and potential lateral movement indicators. Classification must follow the applicable EP-PB-xx playbook.

3
Investigation & Analysis

Mandatory investigation steps include: process tree and command-line analysis, file and hash reputation review, detection of persistence artefacts, network connections initiated by the endpoint, and correlation with identity and network telemetry. Evidence must be preserved for audit, IR, and potential legal review.

4
Containment & Mitigation

Containment actions may include endpoint isolation, process termination or quarantine, blocking indicators across the environment, preventive rule deployment, and temporary user access restriction. For ransomware or active exploitation, immediate isolation is mandatory.

5
Escalation & Communication

Escalation is mandatory when: ransomware is suspected or confirmed, lateral movement indicators are present, privileged accounts are involved, or multiple endpoints show correlated activity. Communication must follow the SOC escalation and incident communication procedures.

6
Recovery & Lessons Learned

For confirmed endpoint incidents: decide between reimage vs clean-up, validate endpoint patch and configuration state, confirm no residual persistence remains, and update detections and prevention policies where gaps are identified. Lessons learned must be documented for continuous improvement.
