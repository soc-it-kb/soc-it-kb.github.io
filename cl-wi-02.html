CL-WI-02: Rotate Cloud Access Keys and Secrets

Controlled procedure for rotating potentially exposed cloud secrets, access keys, certificates, tokens, and application credentials while preserving availability and audit evidence.

Section 1
Purpose

Define how to rotate suspected or confirmed exposed cloud credentials without losing evidence, breaking dependent services unnecessarily, or leaving old credentials usable after remediation.

📌 Authority boundary: Rotation can affect production services. Confirm approval and dependency ownership before disabling or deleting any existing credential.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
Mandatory checks before rotation
Do not delete the old credential before consumers are updated and monitored unless emergency containment requires it.
Confirm whether the credential is used by production services, automation, third parties, or break-glass procedures.
Preserve current metadata: permissions, last-used time, owner, expiry, and affected resources.
Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Confirm Dependency Owner
1
Identify the consuming application, pipeline, integration, workload, automation, or third party.
2
Confirm service criticality and acceptable validation or cutover window.
2
Preserve Current Metadata
1
Record credential ID, creation date, last-used timestamp, permissions, owner, storage location, and affected resources.
2
Export relevant audit logs before changing the credential.
3
Create Replacement and Update Consumers
1
Generate a replacement secret, certificate, key, or token using approved naming, expiry, storage, and vaulting standards.
2
Update application configuration, automation pipeline, vault reference, managed identity mapping, or third-party integration.
4
Disable Old Credential and Validate
1
Disable the old credential first where supported, monitor for failures, then delete after the agreed validation period.
In active compromise, immediate disablement may be required; record the decision and approval.
2
Confirm successful service authentication, no use of the old credential, and evidence of rotation in the ticket.
Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Replacement credential created and stored correctly
✅ Consumers updated and validated
✅ Old credential disabled or deleted according to risk decision
✅ Rotation evidence attached to incident/change record

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Deleting a secret before identifying all consumers.
Rotating without preserving old credential metadata and last-used evidence.
Creating replacement credentials with the same excessive permissions or no expiry.
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Credential has privileged or cross-tenant access.
Credential was used after suspected exposure.
Rotation risks production outage or third-party service failure.
