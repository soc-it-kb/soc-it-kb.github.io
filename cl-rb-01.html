CL-RB-01: Triage Cloud Control Plane Alert

Step-by-step triage for cloud control-plane alerts before escalation into a confirmed incident.

Section 1
Purpose

Step-by-step triage for cloud control-plane alerts before escalation into a confirmed incident.

This runbook provides repeatable execution steps for analysts and platform operators while preserving evidence and approval traceability.

Section 2
Required Access
Cloud audit logsCloud IAM / identity logsSIEM or XDR alertChange / deployment recordsSOC ticketing system
Section 3
Inputs Required
Alert source and detection ruleTenant, subscription, project, or account IDPrincipal, resource, action, IP, user agent, and timestampKnown change or maintenance reference, if available
Section 4
Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Open the alert
1
Record alert source, timestamp, affected tenant/subscription, principal, resource, action, IP, user agent, and detection rule.
2
Attach raw alert evidence to the incident ticket.
2
Validate identity context
1
Check whether the principal is human, service principal, managed identity, break-glass, automation account, or third-party integration.
2
Confirm whether the source location, authentication method, and device context are expected.
3
Check change legitimacy
1
Compare against approved change, deployment pipeline, maintenance window, ticket, or known administrator activity.
2
If legitimate, document the evidence and close as benign or approved change.
4
Review surrounding activity
1
Look back at least 24 hours for role assignment, policy change, login anomaly, app consent, key creation, or resource deletion.
2
Expand to adjacent subscriptions or tenants if privileged activity is confirmed.
5
Classify severity
1
Escalate as Critical if privileged control-plane abuse, persistence, mass modification, or unknown admin activity is confirmed.
2
Escalate to CL-PB-01 with all artefacts attached.
6
Document outcome
1
Close as benign with evidence or escalate to CL-PB-01 with all artefacts attached.
Section 5
Expected Outcome

A validated triage decision: benign/approved change, suspicious activity requiring monitoring, or confirmed cloud control-plane incident requiring CL-PB-01 activation.

Section 6
Validation Checks
  • Alert source and raw event are attached.
  • Principal, resource, action, and timeframe are documented.
  • Change legitimacy was checked.
  • Severity and escalation decision are recorded.
Section 7
Common Errors & Tips
Do not assume automation activity is benign without confirming the pipeline or change record.
Preserve evidence before removing role assignments or credentials.
Look for app consent, federation, and credential creation around the same timestamp.
Section 8
Output Artefacts
Incident ticket
Audit log extract
Change validation evidence
Severity decision
Escalation package
