Scope & relationship to Business Continuity

IT Disaster Recovery is the technology counterpart to Business Continuity. Where BCP says “this business process must resume within 4 hours”, DR says “to meet that, this IT service must be recovered within 2 hours, with no more than 15 minutes of data loss”. The two domains share methodology, terminology, and exercise programme (in GRC-BCDR-SOP-01, WI-01, WI-02); they are distinct in policy, audience, and operational ownership.

This domain is IT-authored and IT-owned. It governs how IT services are designed, operated, backed up, replicated, and recovered to meet the RTOs and RPOs that flow down from business impact assessment. It does not govern business-process workarounds (that is BCP’s remit) and it does not govern cyber incident response (that is the IRP’s remit).

Recovery tiers at a glance

Detailed criteria, backup minima, and replication requirements per tier are defined in GRC-DR-WI-01. At a high level:

TierRTORPOTypical example services
Tier 1 — Mission-critical ≤ 4 hours ≤ 15 minutes Identity provider (Entra ID), core hospital systems (where in scope), critical clinical systems
Tier 2 — Business-critical ≤ 24 hours ≤ 4 hours Email, file services, key administrative applications, EDR / SIEM
Tier 3 — Important ≤ 72 hours ≤ 24 hours Internal applications, training systems, non-critical SaaS
Tier 4 — Standard ≤ 7 days ≤ 7 days Development & test environments, archival systems, low-impact internal tools

Tier assignment is driven by BIA findings, not by IT preference. The methodology in GRC-BCDR-SOP-01 defines how the RTO/RPO derived from business impact flows into tier assignment.

Cloud platform DR as a first-class concern

The estate is hybrid with significant cloud-resident services across most agencies. Cloud platforms reduce some traditional DR concerns (hardware loss, single-site outage) and introduce others (region-level cloud outage, tenant compromise, cloud provider sustained service degradation, certificate-driven lockout). This domain treats cloud DR as a first-class scenario rather than an afterthought.

The scenarios specifically addressed in GRC-DR-WI-02 include:

  • Azure region failure — primary region unavailable; recovery to paired region; what survives by default and what requires deliberate design.
  • Microsoft 365 sustained outage — Exchange Online, SharePoint, Teams unavailable for an extended period; communication fallbacks and data continuity.
  • Entra tenant lockout — loss of administrative access to the tenant through credential compromise, MFA cascade failure, certificate expiry, or accidental policy lockout; recovery paths.
  • Entra tenant compromise — suspected or confirmed tenant-level compromise requiring isolation, forensics, or migration to a clean tenant; recovery to operations.
  • SaaS provider disruption — critical SaaS provider unavailable; alternative-access patterns and business workaround coordination with BCP.

Cloud platform resilience design (multi-region, paired regions, availability zones, backup vault configuration) is governed by the Cloud Baselines domain, particularly IT-CL-WI-03 Azure Subscription and IT-CL-WI-04 Workload Patterns. This domain uses those patterns; it does not re-author them.

Roles & accountabilities at domain level

Detailed role responsibilities are defined in GRC-DR-SOP-01 and GRC-BCDR-SOP-01. At domain level:

  • CIO / IT Leadership — accountable for the technology recovery posture; receives portfolio reporting on tier compliance, exercise outcomes, and gaps.
  • BCDR Steering Committee — cross-agency governance; shared with BCP domain; chairs methodology and exercise schedule.
  • IT DR Coordinator (per agency) — named role; owns the agency’s DR plan inventory, runbook currency, and exercise outcomes.
  • IT Service Owner — named individual per IT service; owns the service’s recovery tier, backup configuration, replication design, and runbook.
  • Backup & Replication operators — execute backup, replication, and recovery procedures according to the runbook.
  • Cloud Platform team — owns the cloud platform DR procedures (region failover, tenant recovery); coordinates with IT service owners for service-level recovery.
  • GRC (central) — maintains methodology, templates, and portfolio inventory; reports completeness and currency; coordinates with BCP.
  • Internal Audit — independently tests recovery design and exercise outcomes; relies on exercise evidence and runbook documentation.

Key metrics tracked at portfolio level
  • Tier classification coverage — percentage of in-scope IT services with an assigned recovery tier. Target: 100%.
  • Runbook currency — percentage of Tier-1 and Tier-2 services with a current DR runbook reviewed in the last 12 months.
  • Backup success rate — percentage of scheduled backup jobs completing within SLA per tier. Tier-specific targets: T1/T2 99.5%, T3 99%, T4 98%.
  • RPO compliance — percentage of services where last successful backup or replication snapshot is within the tier RPO at any check.
  • Exercise compliance — percentage of Tier-1 services exercised within the last 12 months; percentage of Tier-2 within the last 24 months.
  • Recovery exercise success — percentage of exercises meeting the documented RTO and RPO targets.
  • Cloud tenant break-glass health — break-glass account quarterly access validation; secondary tenant standby status where applicable.
  • Open DR-related exceptions — entries in the exception register with overdue remediation dates.
