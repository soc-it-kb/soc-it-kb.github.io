NW-05: Data Exfiltration Indicators

Answer each question YES or NO and apply the decision logic. Escalate with evidence attached — do not reboot or alter the endpoint before escalating.

🌐 Network threats Tier 1 Published 28 April 2026
🔔 When to use this checklist
DLP, CASB, NDR, or SIEM alert suggesting unusual or large-volume data transfer from internal systems to external destinations — potentially indicating data theft or exfiltration activity.
DLP large upload alert CASB data exfiltration rule Unusual outbound data volume Upload to cloud storage / paste site Compressed archive sent externally NDR data volume anomaly

Step 2 — Apply decision logic
✅
Known destination, legitimate business use case confirmed, normal volume — Document and close. Consider adding destination to approved list.
⚠️
YES to questions 1, 2, or 5 — Escalate to Tier-2. Block destination if suspicious. Review data content and transfer logs.
🔴
YES to question 3, 4, 6, or 8 — Escalate to Tier-3 immediately. Invoke NW-PB-04. Preserve transfer logs. Do not alert the user.
🔴
Confirmed exfiltration of sensitive data — Notify SOC Manager immediately. Legal and management notification may be required.
Step 3 — Capture evidence before escalating
Source endpoint or server hostname and user
Destination IP, domain, or cloud service
Volume of data transferred (bytes/files)
Timestamps and duration of transfer
File types or names transferred if visible
DLP or CASB alert details and policy triggered
💡
Analyst Tip
Do not alert the user before escalating to Tier-2 or Tier-3 — if this is an insider threat or an attacker using compromised credentials, alerting them prematurely destroys the investigation. Preserve all transfer logs before taking any blocking action. Volume alone is not confirmation — always check the destination and whether the user has a legitimate reason for the transfer.
