NW-PB-02: Command-and-Control (C2) Communication

Standardised framework for detecting, investigating, and responding to C2 communication — where compromised systems communicate with attacker-controlled infrastructure. Focuses on rapid containment and attribution.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a standardised decision and response framework for detecting, investigating, and responding to command-and-control (C2) communication, where compromised systems communicate with attacker-controlled infrastructure.

This playbook focuses on rapid containment and attribution, as C2 presence confirms active attacker control.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • Outbound connections to known or suspected C2 infrastructure
  • Beaconing or periodic outbound traffic patterns
  • C2 via DNS, HTTP(S), TLS, or custom protocols
  • Network indicators discovered during endpoint, malware, or lateral movement incidents
  • MDR network escalations
Common C2 protocols
DNS tunnelling HTTPS beaconing TLS to unknown HTTP Custom binary

❌ Out of scope

  • One-off outbound connections without persistence → NW-PB-01
  • Pure data exfiltration without control channel → NW-PB-04
Section 3
Trigger Conditions
  • NDR or IDS flags beaconing behaviour
  • Firewall detects repeated outbound connections to suspicious IPs or domains
  • DNS logs show algorithmic or rare domain lookups (DGA patterns)
  • MDR escalates suspected C2 activity
  • SOC identifies network indicators linked to known malware families
Section 4
Initial Validation (Decision Logic)

Validate whether the traffic could be legitimate:

  • Is the destination a known business service, CDN, or update server?
  • Does the traffic align with normal application behaviour and scheduling?
  • Is the frequency and timing human-driven or automated/scripted?
  • Is the destination recently registered or reputation-poor?

If YES — legitimate confirmed → Document and close as Benign Network Traffic
If NO or UNCLEAR → Proceed with C2 assessment (Section 5)

Section 5
C2 Communication Assessment

Assess characteristics and confidence of C2 activity. Presence of any indicator = Suspected C2:

Periodic or beaconing outbound traffic at regular intervals
Communication to a low-reputation or recently registered domain
Encrypted traffic to an unknown or unrecognised service
DNS tunnelling patterns or high-entropy DNS queries
Traffic linked to known malware IOCs or threat intelligence
Section 6
Severity & Escalation Criteria

Escalate to High Severity if any of the following apply:

🔴 C2 communication is persistent or bidirectional
🔴 Endpoint compromise already confirmed
🔴 Multiple hosts communicate with the same C2 infrastructure
🔴 Privileged systems or critical servers are involved
🔴 MDR confirms attacker-controlled channel

Notify immediately

Section 7
Response Strategy

Execute the following actions rapidly and in parallel:

C2 traffic analysis — protocol, destination, timingCritical
Source endpoint identificationCritical
Containment decision — block and isolateCritical
Endpoint compromise escalation — invoke EP playbooksCritical
Scope expansion — identify all beaconing hostsCritical
Identity exposure assessmentConditional

⚠️ C2 confirmation implies active compromise — endpoint playbooks must be engaged immediately and in parallel with network containment

Section 9
Containment Decision Logic

Choose the appropriate containment approach based on confidence and operational risk:

🔴 Immediate blocking

  • Known C2 infrastructure confirmed by threat intelligence
  • Active malware execution on the source endpoint
  • Multiple hosts beaconing to same destination

🟡 Coordinated containment

  • Blocking may alert attacker prematurely
  • Forensic or scope discovery still ongoing

Containment must be synchronised with endpoint isolation to avoid re-infection after blocking. Coordinate timing with the network and endpoint response tracks simultaneously.

Section 10
Scope Expansion Logic

Scope expansion is mandatory for all NW-PB-02 incidents:

Identification actions

  • Identify all hosts communicating with the same C2 indicators
  • Search historical traffic for earlier C2 activity
  • Identify the potential patient-zero system

Correlation actions

  • Correlate with endpoint telemetry (processes, hashes)
  • Correlate with identity activity (credential use post-compromise)
  • Cross-reference with open MDR incidents
Section 11
Communication & Coordination
  • Coordinate all blocking actions with the network team before execution
  • Align blocking timing tightly with endpoint isolation
  • Avoid any external communication that may alert the attacker
  • Maintain MDR ticket alignment and update at each containment milestone
Section 12
Closure Criteria

The incident may be closed when all of the following are confirmed:

  • All C2 communication blocked or terminated at the network layer
  • All compromised endpoints isolated and remediated
  • No further beaconing observed across network telemetry
  • Root cause identified (malware family, initial vector)
  • All actions documented and incident record completed
Section 13
Evidence to Capture
C2 IPs, domains, and URLs
Beaconing intervals and protocols
DNS query logs
Firewall / NDR alert exports
Correlated endpoint and identity artefacts
Timeline of containment actions
Section 14
Post-Incident Actions
  • Update firewall, DNS, and proxy block lists with confirmed C2 indicators
  • Tune NDR beaconing detection rules
  • Feed indicators into threat hunting queries and SIEM use cases
  • Review and tighten outbound network controls and egress filtering
  • Improve DNS logging and DGA detection capabilities
  • Update endpoint prevention rules to detect the malware family identified
