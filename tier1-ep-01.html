EP-01: Suspicious Endpoint Activity

Answer each question YES or NO and apply the decision logic. Escalate with evidence attached — do not reboot or alter the endpoint before escalating.

💻 Endpoint threats Tier 1 Published 28 April 2026
🔔 When to use this checklist

Step 2 — Apply decision logic
✅
All NO — Document findings, monitor, and close if no further indicators emerge.
⚠️
YES to questions 1, 2, 4, or 5 — Escalate to Tier-2 with evidence attached. Do not reboot the endpoint.
🔴
YES to question 3 or 8 — Escalate to Tier-3 immediately. Invoke EP-PB-02 or EP-PB-05. Do not reboot.
🔴
YES to question 6 or 7 — Mark as High Priority and escalate immediately regardless of other answers.
Step 3 — Capture evidence before escalating
Endpoint hostname and assigned user
EDR alert name, severity, and timestamp
Screenshot of alert or process details
Last seen time and isolation status
💡
Analyst Tip
Never reboot a suspected compromised endpoint before investigation — rebooting destroys volatile memory evidence (running processes, network connections, encryption keys) that cannot be recovered. Isolate first via EP-WI-02, then investigate.
