ID-PB-06: Impossible Travel / Anomalous Sign-In Activity

Standardised framework for detecting, investigating, and responding to impossible travel and anomalous authentication patterns — ensuring rapid differentiation between legitimate travel scenarios and active identity compromise.

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a standardised decision and response framework for detecting, investigating, and responding to impossible travel and anomalous sign-in activity, where authentication patterns indicate likely credential compromise, token theft, or proxy/VPN abuse.

This playbook ensures rapid differentiation between benign travel scenarios and active identity compromise.

Section 2
Scope

✅ In scope

  • Impossible travel detections from the identity platform
  • Sign-ins from geographically distant locations within short timeframes
  • Sign-ins from anonymisation infrastructure (VPN, TOR, proxy)
  • Anomalous device or location sign-ins
  • MDR or SOC escalations for abnormal authentication patterns

❌ Out of scope

  • MFA abuse scenarios → ID-PB-03
  • OAuth-only access without sign-ins → ID-PB-04
  • Privileged account anomalies → ID-PB-05
Section 3
Trigger Conditions
  • Identity platform flags an impossible travel detection
  • MDR escalates anomalous sign-in behaviour
  • SOC identifies rapid geo-velocity changes in sign-in logs
  • Successful sign-ins from high-risk countries
  • Sign-ins originating from known proxy, VPN, or TOR infrastructure
Section 4
Initial Validation (Decision Logic)

Before escalating, validate whether the sign-in anomaly could be legitimate:

  • Is the user known to be travelling or working remotely from a different location?
  • Is a corporate VPN or an approved proxy service in use?
  • Is the device previously registered and compliant?
  • Is the IP address associated with trusted or known infrastructure?

If YES — legitimate explanation confirmed → Document and close as Benign Anomaly
If NO or UNCLEAR → Proceed with compromise assessment (Section 5)

Section 5
Anomalous Sign-In Assessment

Assess the likelihood of compromise. Presence of any indicator = Suspected Account Compromise:

Impossible travel within an unrealistic timeframe
Successful sign-in from an unfamiliar or high-risk country
Use of anonymised IP (VPN / TOR / proxy)
Sign-in from a new or unmanaged device
Sign-in coincides with phishing or MFA events
Section 6
Severity & Escalation Criteria

Escalate to High Severity if any of the following apply:

🔴 Successful sign-in confirmed from anomalous location
🔴 Multiple anomalous locations observed for the same account
🔴 User confirms they did not initiate the travel or VPN session
🔴 Anomaly directly follows a phishing, MFA fatigue, or password reset event
🔴 MDR confirms the source IP is associated with malicious infrastructure

Notify immediately
🔐
Identity Team
Account investigation support
👔
SOC Leadership
High severity awareness
🤝
MDR Partner
Infrastructure analysis
Section 7
Response Strategy

Response actions scale based on whether compromise is suspected or confirmed:

Sign-in activity review — mandatory in all casesRequired
Session containmentConditional
Credential resetConditional
MFA enforcement or re-registrationConditional
Persistence checks (mailbox rules, OAuth)Conditional
Scope expansionConditional

If compromise is confirmed at any point → escalate immediately to ID-PB-01 (Account Compromise) and execute the full response workflow

Section 9
Decision Logic — Escalation to ID-PB-01

Escalate to ID-PB-01 (Account Compromise) immediately if any of the following are confirmed:

  • User confirms they did not initiate the sign-in
  • Successful sign-in from a high-risk or impossible location confirmed
  • Anomaly correlates with phishing, MFA abuse, or a credential theft event
  • Persistence mechanisms discovered during investigation
  • Additional suspicious activity detected after the anomalous sign-in
Section 10
Scope Expansion Logic

Triggers for expansion

  • Same IP or ASN targets multiple users
  • Similar anomalies detected across the tenant
  • Sign-ins originate from known attack infrastructure
  • MDR indicates campaign-level behaviour

Expansion actions

  • Search for other users signing in from the same IP
  • Correlate geo-location anomalies across sign-in logs
  • Review Conditional Access outcomes tenant-wide
Section 11
Communication & Coordination
  • Contact the affected user directly to validate travel or remote work activity
  • Avoid premature password resets until legitimacy is confirmed or ruled out
  • Coordinate closely with MDR for infrastructure and IP reputation analysis
  • Align all response actions with the SOC incident handling workflow
Section 12
Closure Criteria

The incident may be closed when one of the following outcomes is reached:

✈️ Benign outcome

  • Legitimate travel or VPN use confirmed by the user
  • No further anomalies observed
  • Activity documented and justified in the incident record

🔒 Security outcome

  • Credentials reset where required
  • All sessions revoked
  • MFA enforced or re-registered
  • Sign-in activity reviewed and confirmed clean
Section 13
Evidence to Capture
Sign-in log entries
IP addresses and geolocation data
Device identifiers
Conditional Access results
User confirmation statements
Timeline of sign-in events
Section 14
Post-Incident Actions
  • Tune impossible travel detection thresholds to reduce false positives
  • Review Conditional Access location-based policies
  • Improve VPN and proxy identification capabilities
  • Feed confirmed malicious IPs into threat intelligence platforms
  • Update analyst guidance for travel validation workflows
