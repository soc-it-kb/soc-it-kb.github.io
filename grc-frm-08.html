Personnel Security and Support Staff Assurance

Ref Question Supplier Response Evidence Reference Reviewer Notes
PS-01 Are background screening checks performed for staff or contractors who may support customer environments, subject to local legal requirements?
PS-02 Do contracts or equivalent terms include confidentiality, acceptable use, and customer data handling obligations?
PS-03 Is security awareness training mandatory at induction and at least annually?
PS-04 Do engineers and support personnel receive role-specific training for secure support practices, privileged access, remote administration, and handling of customer data?
PS-05 Is there a formal joiner, mover, leaver process ensuring timely removal of engineer access to customer support tools and customer environments?
PS-06 Are third-party contractors or partner engineers subject to equivalent screening and control requirements?

Remote Access and Privileged Access Management

Ref Question Supplier Response Evidence Reference Reviewer Notes
RA-01 Describe how remote access to customer environments is established, including VPN, jump server, bastion host, remote support tooling, or other methods.
RA-02 Is multi-factor authentication enforced for all remote access and privileged access used to support customer systems?
RA-03 Are engineer administrative accounts separate from standard user accounts?
RA-04 Is customer environment access granted on a named-user basis and restricted to authorised personnel only?
RA-05 Is access enabled only when required — just-in-time, time-bound, approval-based, or ticket-linked access?
RA-06 Are privileged actions in customer environments logged and attributable to an individual engineer?
RA-07 Are shared accounts prohibited or tightly controlled and monitored where unavoidable?
RA-08 Is remote support access reviewed periodically and removed when no longer required?
RA-09 Are break-glass or emergency access arrangements controlled, logged, and periodically reviewed?

Support Operations, Change and Maintenance

Ref Question Supplier Response Evidence Reference Reviewer Notes
OM-01 Describe how support requests are logged, authorised, and tracked, including ticketing and approval requirements.
OM-02 Are changes to customer-hosted applications formally assessed, tested, approved, and documented before implementation?
OM-03 Are emergency fixes or urgent changes subject to retrospective review and approval?
OM-04 Are changes first tested in non-production where feasible before being applied to production?
OM-05 Are maintenance windows, outage risks, rollback steps, and customer communications defined for planned support activities?
OM-06 Do you maintain secure configuration guidance or hardening recommendations for the supported application?

Data Handling During Support

Ref Question Supplier Response Evidence Reference Reviewer Notes
DH-01 May support personnel view, query, export, copy, or otherwise access live Institutional data during troubleshooting or maintenance? If yes, explain under what conditions.
DH-02 Are there controls to minimise access to live data — masking, redaction, supervised access, or use of test data where possible?
DH-03 Are customer data extracts, screenshots, log files, or diagnostic files classified, protected, and deleted when no longer required?
DH-04 Is customer data prohibited from being stored on engineer endpoints, local devices, email, removable media, or unauthorised collaboration platforms?
DH-05 If diagnostic data must leave the customer environment, how is it transferred, encrypted, stored, and deleted?
DH-06 Are non-production environments prevented from using live production data unless explicitly authorised and protected?

LM-03 Are support tool logs retained for a defined period and protected from tampering or deletion?
LM-04 Are privileged or remote access logs reviewed periodically for misuse, unauthorised activity, or anomalous behaviour?
LM-05 Are session recording, command logging, or equivalent enhanced controls used for high-risk administrative support where appropriate?

Ref Question Supplier Response Evidence Reference Reviewer Notes
VP-01 Do you maintain a vulnerability management process covering your support tooling, remote access platforms, engineer endpoints, and any software components provided to us?
VP-02 Are security updates and patches for the supported application communicated to customers in a timely manner, with risk and implementation guidance?
VP-03 If you provide application patches, hotfixes, scripts, or binaries, are they tested, version-controlled, and integrity-protected before release?
VP-04 Are engineer workstations and support platforms protected by anti-malware, EDR, disk encryption, and secure configuration controls?
VP-05 Do you conduct security testing of product updates, support tools, or scripts before customer deployment?

IR-03 Are support-related security incidents classified, investigated, and tracked through corrective actions to closure?
IR-04 Are incident response processes tested periodically, for example tabletop or simulation exercises?
IR-05 Do you maintain procedures for rapid suspension of engineer access if compromise or misuse is suspected?

BC-04 Are your support continuity arrangements tested or exercised periodically?

PC-04 Can you support audit, investigation, or evidence preservation requirements if a security issue affects the supported application?

Ref Question Supplier Response Evidence Reference Reviewer Notes

PH-03 Are supplier laptops or support devices used on customer premises securely configured and protected?

Ref Question Supplier Response Evidence Reference Reviewer Notes
EX-01 At contract end or service termination, how is all supplier access to customer systems, VPNs, jump hosts, support tools, and documentation removed?
EX-02 How are retained data, logs, extracts, tickets, or troubleshooting artefacts containing customer information securely deleted or archived?
EX-03 Can you support orderly knowledge transfer, handover, or transition to another support provider if required?
EX-04 Are there contractual commitments covering access removal, confidentiality survival, and post-contract data handling?
