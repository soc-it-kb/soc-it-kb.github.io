🧠 Threat Intelligence

Actionable intelligence about adversaries, tactics, techniques, and active exploitation that informs prioritisation decisions across all ASM domains and feeds directly into SOC detection and response operations.

NIST CSF 2.0 — Identify & Detect MITRE ATT&CK aligned SOC integration Risk-driven prioritisation
Section 1
Purpose

Threat Intelligence (TI) transforms raw data about cyber threats into actionable knowledge that security teams can use to make better, faster decisions. In the context of ASM, threat intelligence answers a critical question: which of our vulnerabilities and exposure gaps are being actively targeted right now?

This page establishes the foundational concepts, intelligence types, recommended sources, and SOC integration points for the Threat Intelligence capability. Formal SOPs and Work Instructions will be developed as the capability matures.

Section 3
Intelligence Types

Effective threat intelligence programmes produce intelligence at four levels — each serving a different audience and decision type.

📊 Strategic Intelligence

High-level threat landscape trends, geopolitical context, and sector-specific threats. Used by leadership for resource allocation and risk acceptance decisions. Long time horizon.

⚔️ Tactical Intelligence

Adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) mapped to MITRE ATT&CK. Used by SOC analysts and detection engineers to improve detection rules and response playbooks.

🎯 Operational Intelligence

Specific details about planned or active campaigns targeting the sector or organisation. Enables proactive defensive posture changes before an attack is launched.

🔩 Technical Intelligence

Machine-consumable indicators of compromise (IOCs) — IPs, domains, hashes, URLs. Feeds directly into SIEM, EDR, and firewall block lists for automated detection and blocking.

Section 4
How Threat Intelligence Informs ASM

Threat intelligence is the connective tissue between ASM domains — it answers which vulnerabilities matter most right now based on what attackers are actively doing.

🔍 Vulnerability prioritisation

Vulnerabilities with active exploitation (CISA KEV, threat actor campaigns) are elevated regardless of CVSS score. Intelligence-driven triage replaces score-only prioritisation.

🩹 Patch urgency

Active exploitation in the wild triggers emergency patching (IT-PM-SOP-02) immediately — bypassing standard change windows. Intelligence confirms when a CVE is being weaponised.

🔒 Baseline hardening

TTP analysis reveals which misconfigurations attackers are exploiting in campaigns. Baseline controls are updated to harden against observed attacker behaviour.

📋 Playbook improvement

New adversary TTPs mapped to MITRE ATT&CK trigger updates to SOC playbooks, detection rules, and runbooks. Intelligence drives continuous improvement of response capability.

🛡️ Detection enrichment

Technical IOCs (IPs, domains, hashes) are ingested into SIEM and EDR to create alerts before an attacker reaches the endpoint. Threat intel feeds become detection controls.

📊 Risk decisions

Strategic intelligence informs risk acceptance decisions — whether a specific threat actor targets the sector, what their typical objectives are, and what controls are most effective.

Section 6
CISA KEV — The Most Actionable Free Source

The CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalogue is the single most actionable free threat intelligence source available. It lists CVEs that have been confirmed as actively exploited in the wild — meaning real attackers are using them right now.

  • Any CVE on the KEV list should be treated as an emergency patch priority regardless of CVSS score
  • Cross-reference your vulnerability scanner output against the KEV list as part of every triage cycle
  • KEV is updated frequently — subscribe to CISA alerts or automate the comparison
  • Available free at: cisa.gov/known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog

Section 8
Capability Maturity Roadmap

Threat intelligence capability development is a progressive journey. The current focus is on consuming free and open-source intelligence to support prioritisation decisions.

⚡ Current — Consume

Use CISA KEV, MITRE ATT&CK, and open feeds to inform vulnerability prioritisation and patch urgency decisions

🎯 Near-term — Integrate

Ingest technical IOC feeds into SIEM and EDR. Build TI-driven detection rules. Establish a formal TI review cadence.

🔭 Future — Produce

Generate organisation-specific intelligence, build threat actor profiles relevant to the sector, and share intelligence with sector peers.
