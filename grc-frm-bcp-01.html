GRC-FRM-BCP-01 — Business Process Continuity Plan Template

Template populated by agencies for each critical business process per GRC-BCP-SOP-01. The plan describes how the process continues to operate during disruption: workarounds, alternative sites, alternative tools, communications, decision-making authority. Owned by the named Business Process Owner; reviewed annually.

Per critical process Operating during disruption Annual review
How to use this template. Complete one plan per Tier-1 (Mission-critical) and Tier-2 (Business-critical) business process. Drives from the BIA worksheet (GRC-FRM-BCDR-01) which establishes the RTO/RPO and dependencies this plan must address. Fields shown as [Fill] are placeholders the agency replaces. The named Business Process Owner is accountable; the agency BCP Coordinator maintains the plan inventory.

Plan completeness checklist

Before signing off a plan, the Business Process Owner confirms:

  • The plan covers all disruption scenarios identified as plausible in the BIA (Section 6 of BIA worksheet).
  • The RTO and RPO match the BIA findings — no silent downgrade to current capability.
  • The workarounds are concrete — named people, named locations, named procedures, not aspirational language.
  • Communications fallback does not depend on services that may fail in the same disruption.
  • Key contacts are current (verified within the last review cycle).
  • The plan has been exercised at least once within its required cadence; first plans require an exercise within 6 months of publication.
  • For Tier-1: an offline / out-of-band copy has been produced and is held by the named role.
  • Recent material changes (personnel, dependencies, sites) are reflected.
