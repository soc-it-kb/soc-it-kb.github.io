CL-WI-01: Review Cloud Audit Logs

Tool-agnostic procedure for reviewing cloud audit, activity, sign-in, and resource logs during cloud investigations — investigation only, no containment actions.

☁️ CloudOps Tier 1 / 2 Investigation only Evidence collection

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a consistent procedure for reviewing cloud audit evidence in order to reconstruct activity, identify suspicious control-plane actions, support incident classification, and preserve defensible evidence for escalation or closure.

📌 Scope boundary: This WI is read-only. Do not revoke sessions, change permissions, rotate credentials, delete resources, or notify users while following this procedure.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
Read-only evidence handling
Preserve original timestamps, time zone, correlation IDs, and query parameters.
Export evidence before any containment playbook changes configuration or access.
Record gaps in retention, missing logs, or disabled audit sources as findings.
Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Define Investigation Window
1
Start with the alert timestamp minus 24 hours and extend if persistence, privilege escalation, or suspicious automation is found.
2
Record the exact time zone and source of time used for the timeline.
2
Collect Log Sources
1
Collect control-plane activity logs, identity sign-ins, admin audit, resource diagnostics, storage access logs, network flow logs, and SaaS audit events.
2
Preserve raw exports and screenshots where exports are unavailable.
3
Filter and Correlate
1
Filter by user, service principal, managed identity, automation account, app ID, IP, user agent, resource, and correlation/request ID.
2
Correlate cloud activity with identity, endpoint, network, and ticket/change evidence.
4
Identify High-Risk Actions
1
Flag role assignment, policy modification, key creation, consent grant, public exposure, firewall change, resource deletion, export, and logging disablement.
2
Build a timeline ordered by timestamp, source, principal, action, resource, result, and linked ticket/change if legitimate.
Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Cloud activity timeline created
✅ Suspicious principals, resources, and actions identified
✅ Evidence exported and attached to the incident record
✅ No containment actions performed

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Reviewing only the alerting log source and missing identity, resource, or SaaS audit context.
Losing correlation IDs, request IDs, or time zone context when exporting evidence.
Assuming a change is authorised without matching it to an approved ticket or deployment.
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Privileged role assignment or consent grant cannot be tied to an approved change.
Logging, retention, diagnostic settings, or security controls were disabled.
Data access, export, or public exposure affects sensitive or regulated information.
