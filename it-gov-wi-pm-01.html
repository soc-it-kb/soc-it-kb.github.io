IT-GOV-WI-PM-01: Endpoint Patch Management

Step-by-step process for deploying, validating, and managing security patches on end-user endpoints — operationalising the requirements defined in IT-PM-SOP-01.

Patch Management Procedure
Scope
Windows endpoints — laptops and desktops Centrally and locally managed devices Institution-owned and approved shared-use endpoints

This WI does not cover servers or network devices — see IT-GOV-WI-PM-02.

Required access & prerequisites
Administrative access to endpoint or management platform Endpoint inventory visibility (device name, OS, owner) Awareness of approved patch windows Backup or recovery mechanism available where feasible

📌 Patches must originate from trusted vendor sources only — OS vendors, approved application vendors, and globally approved update repositories. Unverified or third-party patch sources are not permitted.

Patch prioritisation SLAs

Critical

≤ 72 hours

High

≤ 14 days

Medium

≤ 30 days

Low

≤ 90 days

Patch management workflow
1
Identify Patches
Review available OS security updates, browser updates, and common third-party applications (PDF readers, runtimes)
Identify patches addressing Critical or High severity vulnerabilities and actively exploited vulnerabilities
2
Assess Risk & Priority
Classify patches by severity: Critical / High / Medium / Low
Identify affected high-priority endpoints: privileged users, high-risk locations, internet-exposed or frequently mobile devices
Confirm applicable SLA based on severity classification
3
Plan Deployment
Confirm approved patch window — standard maintenance or emergency window for critical risks
Identify deployment approach: automatic deployment or manual/assisted deployment
Communicate expected impact to users if interaction is required
4
Deploy Patches
Ensure endpoint is powered on and connected before initiating
Monitor installation progress: Successful / Pending reboot / Failed
Reboot endpoint where required
⚠️ Do not defer Critical patches without formal approval.
5
Validate Patch Installation
Confirm patch successfully installed
Confirm endpoint is operational — logon, network access, basic functionality
Verify OS version/build and patch status
6
Handle Failures & Exceptions
Attempt reinstallation and identify error cause: disk space, user interruption, compatibility issue
Escalate unresolved failures to IT Operations
Initiate Patch Exception & Risk Acceptance (IT-PM-SOP-03) if patch cannot be applied
7
Record & Report
Record patch status: Compliant / Pending / Failed / Exception granted
Update tracking systems or compliance reports
Flag overdue Critical or High risk patches for escalation
Emergency / zero-day patching

⚠️ For Critical or actively exploited vulnerabilities: standard patch windows may be bypassed, immediate deployment is authorised, and user disruption is acceptable where risk justifies it. Post-deployment validation remains mandatory. Follow IT-PM-SOP-02 for the full emergency patching workflow.

Patch drift management
Patch drift occurs when patches are rolled back, devices miss updates, or systems fall outside compliance thresholds
When drift is detected: re-apply missing patches, investigate root cause, escalate repeat drift cases, apply compensating controls if required
Expected outcome
Endpoints remain protected against known vulnerabilities
Exposure window for Critical exploits is reduced within SLA
Operational stability maintained post-patching
Patch compliance is measurable and auditable
