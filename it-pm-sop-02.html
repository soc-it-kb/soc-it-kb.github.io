IT-PM-SOP-02: Patch Management — Emergency / Zero-Day SOP

Process for identifying, authorising, deploying, and validating emergency patches when critical vulnerabilities present an immediate and material risk.

👤 Author: Paulo Ferreira

🔄 Version: v1.0
Section 1
Purpose

This SOP defines the recommended process for identifying, authorising, deploying, and validating emergency or zero-day security patches when critical vulnerabilities present an immediate and material risk. The objective is to minimise exposure time, contain active exploitation, and maintain service resilience while ensuring decisions are governed, documented, and auditable.

Section 2
Trigger Conditions

This SOP is invoked when any of the following conditions are met:

  • Confirmed active exploitation in the wild
  • CVSS ≥ 9.0 with credible exploit availability
  • Direct impact to internet-facing systems, authentication / identity services, or privileged access infrastructure
  • Vendor, CERT, or threat intelligence advisory indicating urgent risk
  • Direction from Cyber Security leadership during an active incident
Section 3
Emergency Patch Principles

⚡ Speed with control

Reduce exposure time without bypassing accountability

🔴 Risk overrides convenience

Business disruption is acceptable where justified by risk

📋 Document what you do

Emergency does not mean undocumented — record all actions

✅ Validate always

Post-deployment validation is required even in emergencies

Section 4
Emergency Patch Workflow
1
Identify & Confirm Threat
Validate vulnerability scope, affected systems, exploit status, severity. Correlate with internal exposure — asset inventory, internet exposure, privilege level.
2
Rapid Risk Assessment
Time-boxed assessment: likelihood of exploitation, business impact, asset criticality, available mitigations. If risk is high or critical — proceed immediately.
3
Authorise Emergency Action
Record decision: date/time, authorising role, justification. Notify IT Operations, platform owners, and Incident Response team. Authority: Global Cyber Security Lead / CISO delegate / IR Lead / SOC Manager with Cyber Security approval.
4
Prepare for Deployment
Identify affected systems, confirm backups or snapshots (highly recommended for servers), define rollback method, confirm monitoring visibility.
5
Deploy Emergency Patch
Deploy outside normal maintenance windows if required. Prioritise internet-facing systems, identity and access systems, and privileged endpoints and servers. Reboot where required.
6
Post-Deployment Validation
Confirm patch applied, systems operational, critical services functioning, no unexpected degradation. Follow endpoint or server compliance validation checklist.
7
Record, Communicate & Close
Record affected assets, deployment status, and issues. Communicate completion status and any follow-up actions. Close once all applicable assets addressed or formal exceptions in place.
Section 5
Emergency SLAs

Critical / Active Exploit

As soon as technically feasible (hours, not days)

High with credible exploit

≤ 72 hours

Patch unavailable

Immediate compensating controls

Section 6
Compensating Controls (If Patch Not Available)
  • Network isolation or segmentation
  • Temporary service disablement
  • Configuration hardening
  • Access restriction or privilege reduction
  • Increased monitoring and alerting

⚠️ Compensating controls must be documented, time-bound, and reviewed until the patch is applied.

Section 7
Governance & Review

SOP Owner: Global Cyber Security Team  ·  Review Frequency: Annual or post-major incident  ·  Emergency patching is subject to audit — evidence must demonstrate justification, authorisation, validation, and closure.
