ID-04: OAuth Application Abuse

Use this checklist to triage a new OAuth consent alert. Focus on the permissions requested — even "read-only" access to email or files can enable serious data exfiltration.

🔐 Identity Tier 1 Published 20 Dec 2025
🔔 When to use this checklist
New OAuth consent granted / SIEM detection of unusual app consent / MDR escalation for OAuth abuse.

Do NOT remove the app as Tier-1 — wait for Tier-2 approval before any remediation
⚠️ Common Tier-1 mistakes to avoid
Closing based on the app name alone — attackers use names that look legitimate (e.g. "Microsoft OneDrive Helper")
Ignoring "read-only" permissions — Mail.Read and Files.ReadAll can exfiltrate all email and documents silently
Assuming MFA protects against OAuth abuse — once consent is granted, the app has persistent access regardless of MFA
Removing the app without Tier-2 escalation — premature removal destroys forensic evidence
💡
Analyst Tip
MFA does not protect against OAuth app abuse. Once a user consents, the app has persistent access to their data — even after a password reset — until the consent is explicitly revoked.
