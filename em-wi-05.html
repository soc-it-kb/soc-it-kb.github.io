EM-WI-05: Block Sender / Domain

Tool-agnostic procedure for applying sender and domain blocks at the email gateway — stopping ongoing phishing delivery, malware campaigns, and repeat malicious sources once approved.

📧 Email threats Tier 1 / 2 Requires approval Containment action

Section 1
Purpose

Provide a consistent, tool-agnostic procedure to block malicious email senders or domains to prevent ongoing phishing delivery, malware campaigns, email-based harassment or fraud, and repeat delivery from known malicious sources.

📌 Scope boundary: This WI does not authorise blocking decisions. It defines how to apply sender/domain blocks safely once approved by Tier-2 or via SOAR.

Section 5
Pre-Checks & Safety Notes
🚫 Mandatory constraints
Do NOT block high-volume public email providers (Gmail, Outlook.com etc.) without explicit approval
Do NOT expand scope beyond what is approved
Do NOT implement user-level inbox rules as a substitute for gateway blocking
Do NOT apply permanent blocks without documented approval

⚠️ Prefer domain-level blocking over single sender addresses when a campaign is confirmed. If potential business impact exists — pause and escalate to Tier-2 before proceeding.

Section 6
Step-by-Step Actions
1
Validate Sender / Domain Indicator
1
Review the approved indicator — confirm exact sender address, domain, or subdomain
2
Confirm the indicator matches the analysed samples and gateway or SOC investigation findings
If the domain is shared infrastructure (SendGrid, Mailchimp etc.) — pause and escalate. Block at sender level only.
2
Confirm Blocking Method & Scope
Indicator typeTypical blocking control
Sender addressEmail gateway / tenant anti-spam policy
DomainEmail gateway / tenant anti-spam policy
SubdomainEmail gateway (preferred)

⏱️ Temporary block

Incident-scoped — review and remove after incident closure

🔒 Permanent block

Threat-intel validated — requires documented approval

3
Apply the Block
1
Add the sender or domain to the approved block list at the approved enforcement point
2
Record policy/rule name, scope, duration, timestamp, and operator
4
Validate Blocking Effectiveness
1
Confirm new emails from the sender/domain are rejected and block events are logged
2
If delivery continues — do not add overlapping rules. Escalate to Tier-2 for reassessment.
5
Monitor for Variants
1
After blocking, watch for attacker adaptation:
  • Look-alike or typo-squatted domains
  • Sender address spoofing attempts
  • Display-name abuse from different domains
2
Report any variants immediately — rapid infrastructure rotation may indicate a coordinated campaign
6
Preserve Evidence
1
Retain sample emails, header analysis, and indicator rationale. Do not remove preserved evidence when cleaning up rules.
7
Document Actions
1
Update incident record with sender/domain blocked, blocking control and scope, timestamp, validation outcome, and any observed side effects. Attach screenshots or admin confirmation.
Section 7
Expected Outcome

✅ Malicious sender or domain blocked at the email gateway
✅ Further delivery from the source prevented
✅ Clear audit trail with rule reference, scope, and timestamp

Section 8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Blocking the display name instead of the actual sending domain
Blocking a single sender address when domain-level blocking is more appropriate for a campaign
Blocking shared or partner domains without validating business impact
Applying permanent blocks without documented approval
Failing to monitor for look-alike domain variants after blocking
Section 9
Escalation Conditions
Blocking affects legitimate business communication
New sender or domain variants appear rapidly after the block is applied
Emails continue to bypass the block
Campaign scope expands significantly
