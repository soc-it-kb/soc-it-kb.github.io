NW-01: Suspicious Outbound Traffic / C2 Indicators

Answer each question YES or NO and apply the decision logic. Escalate with evidence attached — do not reboot or alter the endpoint before escalating.

🌐 Network threats Tier 1 Published 28 April 2026

SIEM alert, EDR network detection, or NDR/firewall alert suggesting an endpoint or server is communicating with an external C2 server or beaconing to an unknown destination.

Step 2 — Apply decision logic
✅
Single connection, no malicious reputation, legitimate process — Document, whitelist if confirmed legitimate, monitor and close.
⚠️
YES to questions 3, 4, or 5 — Escalate to Tier-2. Block destination indicator via NW-WI-02. Investigate originating process.
🔴
YES to question 1, 2, 7, or 8 — Escalate to Tier-3 immediately. Invoke NW-PB-02. Isolate affected endpoint(s).
🔴
YES to question 6 — Escalate immediately regardless of other answers. Tier-0/1 C2 is a critical incident.
Step 3 — Capture evidence before escalating
Source endpoint/server hostname and IP
Destination IP, domain, and port
Process responsible for the connection
Traffic volume and timing pattern (screenshot or log)
Threat intelligence reputation of destination
First seen and last seen timestamps
💡
Analyst Tip
Beaconing is one of the clearest C2 indicators — look for connections at consistent intervals (every 30s, 60s, 5min) rather than random timing. Regular interval traffic from a process that has no reason to connect externally is almost always malicious. Don't dismiss low-volume traffic — some C2 frameworks deliberately use low data volumes to avoid detection.
