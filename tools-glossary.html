Acronym Glossary

Curated glossary of the acronyms used across this knowledge base. Each entry expands the acronym and explains in plain language what it means in our context. Designed for readers across all skill levels — entry-level practitioners can build vocabulary, and experienced readers can confirm conventions used here when they differ between sources.

140 entries A–Z navigable Domain-tagged All skill levels

This glossary covers the most-used and most-confusing acronyms across the KB rather than every acronym used. Entries are alphabetised, with each entry showing the full expansion, a one-sentence plain-English explanation, and one or more domain tags so you can see where the term is principally used.

Some acronyms have multiple expansions across disciplines (e.g. CA means Conditional Access in identity, Certificate Authority in PKI, Configuration Audit elsewhere). Where this occurs, the glossary defines the meaning as used in this KB; if you encounter the acronym in external material, check context.

Document-class prefixes used in KB filenames — SOP, WI, FRM, POL, REG — are included since they are foundational to navigation. Domain prefixes embedded in document codes (BCDR, BCP, DR, ASM, GRC, etc.) are also defined.
