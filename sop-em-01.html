SOP-EM-01: Email Threat Operations

Standard Operating Procedure defining how the SOC detects, triages, investigates, escalates, and responds to email-borne threats — establishing mandatory operating standards for consistent, auditable, and risk-based response.

Section 1
Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines how the Security Operations Centre (SOC) detects, triages, investigates, escalates, and responds to email-borne threats, ensuring a consistent, auditable, and risk-based response across the organisation.

This SOP establishes mandatory operating standards for handling malicious, suspicious, and policy-violating email activity.

Section 2
Scope

Applies to

  • Global SOC (Tier 1–3)
  • Regional SOCs
  • MDR provider
  • Local IT / Cyber teams
  • Email platform administrators

In-scope threat categories

  • Phishing
  • Malware attachments
  • Business Email Compromise (BEC)
  • Spam and nuisance emails
  • QR-code and AI-assisted phishing

Section 6
Operating Procedure (High-Level)
1
Detection & Intake

Email threats may be detected via Secure Email Gateway alerts, SIEM correlations, user-reported emails (phish button / ticket), or MDR escalations. All detections must be logged in the incident management platform.

2
Initial Triage & Classification

The SOC must determine threat type (phishing, malware, BEC, spam), delivery method (link, attachment, QR code), targeted users (VIP, finance, IT, executives), and potential credential exposure. Emails are classified according to the aligned playbook.

3
Investigation & Analysis

Mandatory analysis steps include: header inspection, sender reputation assessment, URL and attachment detonation where available, similar message identification across mailboxes, and review of user interaction. Evidence must be preserved for audit and IR purposes.

4
Containment & Mitigation

Actions may include blocking sender domains/IPs, quarantining or purging messages, disabling malicious URLs, credential resets for affected users, and tenant-wide remediation rules. Containment actions must follow least-disruption principles unless risk justifies aggressive response.

5
Escalation & Communication

Escalation is mandatory when: credential compromise is suspected, financial fraud indicators are present, VIP or executive users are targeted, or multiple users are impacted. Communications must follow the SOC communications SOP and incident severity model.

6
Recovery & Lessons Learned

For confirmed incidents: validate account security posture, ensure user awareness follow-up, document indicators of compromise, and update detection rules where gaps are identified. Lessons learned must be recorded for continuous improvement.
