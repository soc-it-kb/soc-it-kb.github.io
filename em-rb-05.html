EM-RB-05: Block Sender / Domain

Block confirmed malicious senders or domains at the email security gateway to prevent future delivery of malicious emails.

📧 Email threats Tier 1 / 2 Containment
📋 ID: EM-RB-05
·
👤 Author: Paulo Ferreira
·
📅 Published: 22 December 2025
·
🏷️ Category: Email Threat
·
Active
Section 1
Purpose

Block confirmed malicious senders or domains to prevent future delivery.

Section 2
Required Access
🔑 Email security admin access
Section 3
Inputs Required
📧 Sender email or domain ✅ Malicious verdict reference
Section 4
Step-by-Step Procedure
1
Validate the malicious verdict — confirm the sender or domain is not shared/legitimate infrastructure
Check if the domain is a shared platform (e.g. SendGrid, Mailchimp) — if so, block at sender level only
2
Add the sender email address or domain to the block list in the email security platform
3
Apply the policy globally or scoped to affected users as appropriate
For campaign-based threats, prefer a global domain block
4
Confirm policy propagation — verify the block rule is active
5
Monitor for repeat delivery attempts from the same or related senders
Section 5
Expected Outcome

✅ Future emails from the blocked sender or domain are rejected at the gateway
✅ Block policy confirmed active and propagated

Section 6
Validation Checks
  • Test delivery from the blocked sender is rejected
  • Block policy is confirmed active in the email security platform
Section 7
Common Errors & Tips
Avoid blocking shared sending platforms (SendGrid, Mailchimp, etc.) at domain level — block the specific sender address only to avoid collateral impact
Prefer domain-level blocking for campaigns where the attacker controls the full domain
Section 8
Output Artefacts
Block rule reference / ID
