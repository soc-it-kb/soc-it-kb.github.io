🎯 Cyber & IT Risk Register

The central register of identified cyber and IT risks — with inherent and residual scoring, treatment plans, named owners, and mappings to NIST CSF 2.0 and ISO 27001:2022 Annex A. Methodology defined in GRC-SOP-04; new risks submitted via GRC-FRM-13.

ISO 31000 ISO 27001:2022 NIST CSF 2.0 5×5 matrix Audit evidence

📌 How to use this register. Click any risk row to expand full detail. Use the filter pills to focus by category, severity, or treatment status. Inherent score is the risk before controls; residual score is the risk after current controls are applied.

Risks are reviewed at the cadence defined in GRC-SOP-04 §8 (Critical: monthly · Very High & High: quarterly · Medium: semi-annually · Low: annually). Out-of-cycle reviews are triggered by control changes, incidents, and threat-landscape shifts.

› RISK-001 Ransomware compromise of Tier-0 systems via unpatched edge appliance Cyber Operational Critical · 25 Critical · 20 Mitigate Active
Description

External actor exploits an unpatched internet-facing edge appliance (VPN / firewall) to gain initial access, escalate via stolen credentials, and deploy ransomware to domain controllers and backup infrastructure. Worst-case scenario for an organisation of this scale.

Threat & vulnerability

Source: External ransomware affiliate / IAB · Vulnerability: Delayed patching cycle on edge devices; flat network admin paths to Tier-0

Asset / process · tier

Edge VPN appliance → Domain Controllers → Backup infrastructure Tier 0

Inherent & residual scores
Inherent L × I5 × 5 = 25
Residual L × I4 × 5 = 20
Existing controls

Edge appliance patching SLA; perimeter monitoring; EDR on Tier-0; immutable backup tier; SOC 24×7 monitoring; tier-0 admin segregation.

Treatment plan

Reduce edge appliance patching SLA from 14 to 7 days for KEV-listed CVEs; deploy network micro-segmentation between admin tiers; quarterly tabletop exercises. Target residual: 3 × 5 = 15 by Q3.

› RISK-002 Privileged credential exposure via shared admin accounts Cyber Operational Very High · 16 Very High · 15 Mitigate Active
Description

Legacy shared administrative accounts on critical infrastructure cannot be tied to an individual, defeating accountability and enabling lateral movement if compromised. Persistent through historical operational practice.

Threat & vulnerability

Source: External actor post-breach / insider · Vulnerability: Shared accounts; weak authentication on legacy systems

Asset / process · tier

Legacy server administration accounts Tier 0/1

Inherent & residual scores
Inherent L × I4 × 4 = 16
Residual L × I3 × 5 = 15
Existing controls

Privileged Access Workstation (PAW) for new admin sessions; quarterly password rotation; SOC monitoring of admin activity.

Treatment plan

Migrate all shared admin accounts to per-individual accounts in PAM solution within 6 months; enforce MFA for all admin sessions. Target residual: 2 × 4 = 8.

Inherent L × I3 × 5 = 15
Residual L × I3 × 5 = 15

› RISK-005 Inadequate log retention prevents post-incident investigation Cyber Operational High · 12 High · 12 Mitigate Active
Description

SIEM and key application log retention is shorter than the typical attacker dwell time. A stealthy intrusion would not be fully reconstructible, hindering response, regulatory reporting, and lessons-learned.

Threat & vulnerability

Source: Latent post-breach forensic limitation · Vulnerability: Cost-driven retention policy; immature log archival pipeline

Asset / process · tier

SIEM, application logs, network telemetry Tier 1

Inherent & residual scores
Inherent L × I4 × 3 = 12
Residual L × I4 × 3 = 12
Existing controls

30-day hot SIEM retention; selected critical logs in cold storage; documented log sources and retention requirements.

Treatment plan

Extend hot retention to 90 days for security-relevant log sources; establish 1-year cold retention for authentication, EDR, and proxy logs. Target residual: 3 × 3 = 9 by Q4.

› RISK-006 Untested backup recovery prevents timely restoration after destructive event Business Continuity Very High · 15 High · 12 Mitigate Active
Description

Backups exist for all in-scope systems, but full end-to-end restore exercises have not been performed in the last 12 months for several Tier-0/1 platforms. Risk of recovery failing or running over RTO during a real event.

Threat & vulnerability

Source: Destructive cyber attack / catastrophic outage · Vulnerability: Untested restore procedures; potential silent backup corruption

Asset / process · tier

Backup infrastructure for Tier-0/1 systems Tier 0/1

Inherent & residual scores
Inherent L × I3 × 5 = 15
Residual L × I3 × 4 = 12
Existing controls

Automated daily backups; immutable backup tier; documented backup runbooks; partial restore tests on selected systems.

Treatment plan

Quarterly tabletop including DR for one Tier-0 system; annual full-stack restore exercise from immutable tier; document RTO/RPO per system. Target residual: 2 × 4 = 8 by Q1 2027.

Description

Users grant OAuth consent to third-party applications that subsequently access sensitive data (mail, files, calendar). Whether malicious or accidental, this can lead to data exfiltration outside the visibility of standard DLP.

Threat & vulnerability

Source: Malicious OAuth app developer / careless insider · Vulnerability: User-driven consent without admin review

Asset / process · tier

M365 / Google Workspace tenant data Tier 1

Inherent & residual scores
Inherent L × I4 × 3 = 12
Residual L × I3 × 3 = 9
Existing controls

Admin-consent workflow for high-risk scopes; SOC monitoring of OAuth consent grants; ID-RB-05 runbook for revoking OAuth consent; user awareness training.

Treatment plan

Move to admin-consent-required for all third-party apps; quarterly review of consented applications; deploy CASB for granular OAuth visibility. Target residual: 2 × 3 = 6 by Q4.
