SOP-IM-01: Incident Severity Classification & Escalation

Standardised method used by the SOC to classify, prioritise, and escalate security incidents based on business impact and risk — ensuring consistent handling across all SOC tiers and correct playbook activation.

Section 1
Purpose

This SOP defines the standardised method used by the SOC to classify, prioritise, and escalate security incidents based on business impact and risk, ensuring consistent handling across all SOC tiers.

This SOP supports consistent runbook execution, correct playbook activation, and timely escalation and decision-making.

Section 2
Scope

Applies to

  • All SOC analysts (Tier 1, 2, and 3 / IR)
  • All confirmed security incidents managed by the SOC

Does NOT cover

  • Alert triage steps → covered by Runbooks
  • Incident response actions → covered by Playbooks
  • Tool-specific actions → covered by Work Instructions
Section 3
Definitions
Incident
A confirmed security event impacting confidentiality, integrity, or availability.
Severity
A business-impact-based classification used to prioritise response, playbook activation, and escalation.
Escalation
Formal transfer of incident ownership or visibility to a higher tier or role.
Incident Manager
Role assigned to manage High or Critical incidents — responsible for coordination, communication, and resolution.
Section 4
Procedure
1
Incident Severity Overview

All confirmed incidents must be assigned a severity level as early as possible. Severity must be reassessed throughout the incident lifecycle as new information becomes available. Severity assignment directly influences which playbook is activated, escalation requirements, and communication obligations.

2
Severity Levels

🔴 Critical

Widespread impact, core services affected, regulatory or reputational risk

🟠 High

Significant impact — multiple users or systems affected

🟡 Medium

Limited impact to a single user or system

⚪ Low

No material impact — informational or benign

3
Severity Assignment Criteria

Analysts must consider the following when assigning severity:

  • Number of users or systems affected
  • Type and sensitivity of data involved
  • Presence of an active threat or attacker
  • Business criticality of impacted assets
⚠️ When in doubt, default to the higher severity and escalate. Downgrading is always easier than under-responding.
4
Roles & Responsibilities
RoleResponsibilities
Tier 1 SOCAssign initial severity during triage
Tier 2 SOCValidate or adjust severity based on investigation findings
Incident ManagerApprove High / Critical severity; own communication and coordination
SOC LeadOversight, governance, and post-incident review
5
Escalation Rules
Medium
May be handled at Tier 1 unless escalation criteria within the relevant Runbook or Playbook are met.
High
Must be escalated to Tier 2 immediately.
Critical
Requires Incident Manager assignment, Playbook activation, and Management notification immediately.

Specific escalation criteria are defined within the relevant Runbooks and Playbooks for each threat category.

Section 5
Exceptions

Any deviation from this SOP must be documented in the incident record, include justification, and be approved by the SOC Lead or Incident Commander.

Section 6
Compliance & Audit
✅ Incident tickets with severity history ✅ Escalation timestamps ✅ Activated playbook records ✅ Post-incident reports

This SOP supports internal assurance and external audit activities.
