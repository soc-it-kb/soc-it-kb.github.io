IT-GOV-WI-PM-02: Server Patch Management

Step-by-step process for planning, deploying, validating, and governing security patches on servers — maintaining service availability and recoverability while reducing vulnerability exposure.

Patch Management Procedure
Scope
Windows and Linux servers — physical and virtual On-premises, hosted, and cloud-based servers Production and non-production infrastructure Centrally and locally managed servers

This WI does not cover end-user endpoints or network devices — see IT-GOV-WI-PM-01.

Required access & prerequisites
Administrative access to server or management platform Server inventory (hostname, OS, role, environment) Business criticality and service dependencies confirmed Approved maintenance window confirmed Backup or snapshot completed and verified

⚠️ Production systems must not be patched without a verified rollback option — backup restore, snapshot revert, or service failover.

Patch prioritisation SLAs

Critical

≤ 72 hours

High

≤ 14 days

Medium

≤ 30 days

Low

≤ 90 days

Server patch management workflow
1
Identify Applicable Patches
Review available patches for OS, installed services and roles, and middleware and runtimes
Identify patches addressing Critical / High severity and actively exploited vulnerabilities
Confirm applicability to server role and OS version before proceeding
2
Assess Risk & Criticality
Classify patch severity: Critical / High / Medium / Low
Identify server criticality: Production/Non-Production, Tier-0/1/2 services
Assess exposure: internet-facing, privileged or authentication systems
Confirm required SLA based on severity and server role
3
Plan Deployment
Confirm approved maintenance window
Coordinate with application owners and business owners where required
Define deployment method: automated or manual/controlled
Confirm rollback approach: backup restore, snapshot revert, or service failover
4
Pre-Patch Validation
Confirm last backup or snapshot completed successfully
Validate server health and available disk space
Ensure monitoring and alerting are active
Confirm access for post-patch validation is in place
5
Deploy Patches
Initiate patch installation during approved maintenance window
Monitor installation status: Successful / Pending reboot / Failed
Reboot server if required — observe system behaviour during restart
6
Post-Patch Validation
Confirm server boots successfully and required services are running
Verify application functionality where applicable
Confirm OS version and patch level
Review logs for unexpected errors or alerts
Validation is mandatory — do not close the task without confirming service health.
7
Handle Failures & Exceptions
Attempt controlled remediation if patching fails or causes instability
Roll back using approved recovery method if required
Escalate to platform or application owner
Initiate Patch Exception & Risk Acceptance (IT-PM-SOP-03) if patch cannot be applied
Unresolved failures must not remain untracked.
8
Record & Report
Record patch outcome: Compliant / Failed / Deferred / Exception granted
Update tracking and reporting systems
Flag overdue Critical or High risk servers for escalation
Notify stakeholders of completion or rollback status
Emergency / zero-day patching

⚠️ For Critical or actively exploited vulnerabilities: emergency patching may bypass standard windows, business disruption may be accepted where risk justifies it. Backup and rollback remain mandatory — post-deployment validation is required without exception. Follow IT-PM-SOP-02 for the full emergency patching workflow.

Patch drift management
Patch drift may occur due to snapshot reverts, missed maintenance windows, or configuration conflicts
When drift is detected: re-apply missing patches, investigate root cause, escalate repeat drift, apply compensating controls if required
Expected outcome
Servers remain protected against known vulnerabilities
Production risk reduced and controlled within SLA
Recovery and rollback are predictable and tested
Patch compliance is measurable and auditable
