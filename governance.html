Governance & Change Log

Ensuring transparency, traceability, and continuous improvement of SOC procedures. This page records all changes to knowledge base content and defines the contribution and review process.

Transparency Traceability Continuous improvement
Governance principles
  • All content changes must be reviewed and approved before publication
  • SOPs are mandatory — deviations require a documented exception approved by the SOC Manager
  • Content is reviewed at minimum annually, and following any major incident or significant tooling change
  • All playbooks, runbooks, and SOPs must reference the NIST CSF function they support
  • Feedback and improvement suggestions are welcomed and should follow the contribution process below
Contribution & change process
1

Identify the change

Raise a change request — this could be a gap identified during an incident, a lessons learned action, a new threat type, or a tooling update.

2

Draft the content

Draft the new or updated procedure following the existing format and naming convention (e.g. EM-RB-XX for email runbooks). Use existing published content as a template.

3

Peer review

The draft is reviewed by at least one other SOC analyst or subject matter expert. Feedback is incorporated before escalating for approval.

4

SOC Manager approval

The SOC Manager reviews and approves the content. For SOPs and significant procedural changes, CISO-level sign-off may be required.

5

Publish & log

Content is published to the knowledge base and a change log entry is created below recording the change, owner, and date.
