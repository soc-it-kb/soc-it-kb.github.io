Governance principles
  • All content changes must be reviewed and approved before publication
  • SOPs are mandatory — deviations require a documented exception approved by the SOC Manager or GRC owner
  • Content is reviewed at minimum annually, and following any major incident or significant change
  • All playbooks, runbooks, and SOPs reference the NIST CSF 2.0 function they support
  • All Work Instructions are tool-agnostic — platform-specific procedures are maintained separately
  • ASM SOPs and WIs define what should be done, not which tool to use
  • GRC documents are governed by GRC-POL-01 and the Document Review Register
  • This knowledge base is publicly accessible and intended as a reference for SOC teams, NGOs, not-for-profits, and SMBs
Contribution & change process
1

Identify the change

Raise a change request — gap from incident, lessons learned, new threat, tooling update, baseline change, or GRC requirement.

2

Draft the content

Draft following the existing format and naming convention. Use published content as a template.

3

Peer review

Review by at least one other SOC analyst or subject matter expert. Feedback incorporated before escalating.

4

Approval

SOC Manager approves SOC content. IT Operations / Cyber Security Governance Board approves ASM SOPs. GRC owner approves GRC policies, SOPs, and forms.

5

Publish & log

Published via GitHub Pages with a signed commit. Change log entry created below.

Identity Governance & Lifecycle domain published — new ASM domain covering hybrid identity (Entra + on-prem AD) and non-human identity governance with hub + SOP + 5 Work Instructions
Closed the gap the Cloud Baselines domain explicitly deferred: there were no organisation-level controls for identity lifecycle (JML), recertification, role design, non-human identity governance, or the eligibility layer above PIM activation. Published as the eighth ASM sub-domain, sized as a tight scope (single SOP + 5 WIs) rather than a maximalist treatment, to round out the ASM coverage without expanding scope further. asm-identity-governance.html — new ASM domain hub. Scope (in): five identity classes (employees / contractors, students / alumni, guests / external collaborators, on-prem service accounts, non-human cloud identities); hybrid AD + Entra; privileged eligibility governance. Scope (out, explicit boundaries): tenant-level identity platform configuration (Conditional Access, PIM activation, MFA enforcement) — covered by IT-CL-WI-01; PAM platform mechanics — covered by IT-SI-WI-04; PAW build and lifecycle — covered by IT-EP-SOP-PAW-01; Tier-0 AD enforcement — covered by IT-SRV-WI-DC-01; identity incident response — covered by SOC SOPs (SOP-ID-01) and ID-PB / ID-RB content; HR system internal processes — out of scope (the integration interface is in scope, the HR processes themselves are not); application-internal authorisation — system-owner responsibility. IT-ID-SOP-01 (Identity Governance & Lifecycle) — 7-section policy SOP. Purpose, Scope (with explicit boundaries against IT-CL-WI-01, IT-SI-WI-04, IT-EP-SOP-PAW-01, IT-SRV-WI-DC-01, SOC IR content), 7 Governance Principles (authoritative source — every identity originates from HR / SIS / sponsor workflow; least privilege by default — clone-previous-user forbidden; standing privilege is time-bounded — eligibility not assignment; recertification is a control not a chore — cadence per tier, reviewer accountability; every identity has a named owner — including non-human; non-human identities are first-class — same rigour as human; governance separate from incident response — boundary maintained), Minimum Requirements across 5 requirement domains (4.1 JML Lifecycle 13 items; 4.2 Access Recertification 10 items; 4.3 Role Design 9 items; 4.4 Non-Human Identity 11 items; 4.5 Privileged Identity Governance 10 items) each operationalised by a dedicated WI, Roles & Responsibilities (8 roles including Head of IT, Identity & Access Engineering, HR Information Systems, Line Managers, System Owners, Privileged Access Authority, Non-Human Identity Owners, GRC), Framework Alignment, Governance & Review with metrics covering JML SLA conformance, orphaned account count, recertification completion, standing-privilege count target ≤4, non-human identity coverage, service principal secret hygiene, role catalogue health. Five Work Instructions: IT-ID-WI-01 Joiner / Mover / Leaver Lifecycle (7 steps: joiner provisioning from authoritative source — HR / SIS / sponsor-driven; joiner access bundle with clone-previous-user forbidden; mover with revoke-then-grant discipline and ≤30-day dual-role exception; leaver with same-day disable, time-bounded retention 30–90 days, clean termination including forwarding-rule and service-account-ownership handover; student / alumni cohort transitions per academic calendar; guest sponsor-driven lifecycle with 180-day default and quarterly recert; monthly identity-source reconciliation. SLA dashboard table — joiner ≤1 day, mover ≤5 days, leaver same-day). IT-ID-WI-02 Access Recertification & Attestation (8 steps: cadence per tier — Tier-0 quarterly, Tier-1 quarterly for privileged / semi-annual for resource access, Tier-2/3 annual, guest quarterly; named reviewers per scope; campaign execution via Entra Identity Governance access reviews where licensed or manual workflow where not; non-response handling — Tier-0/1 suspend pending re-attestation, Tier-2/3 revoke as default; forbid bulk-attest at high tiers with sampling acceptable at Tier-2/3 only; apply decisions for real — auto-apply preferred with ≤7-day SLA manual; evidence retention 3 years minimum; orphan account remediation 30 days). IT-ID-WI-03 Role Design & Entitlement Management (9 steps: establish role catalogue with 100% T0/T1 coverage day-one and 95% T2/T3 by FY26 end; naming convention discipline — domain-scope-function-environment-tier; least-privilege role design — built-in preferred, custom when needed, scope at lowest meaningful level; group hygiene — max 3 nest levels, no circular references, empty-group cleanup, dynamic-group rule audit; entitlement packages via Entra IG for common access bundles; separation of duties enforcement with detection where prevention not supported; role-change workflow with direct-edit detection and backfill; quarterly catalogue review covering retirement / splitting / consolidation; monthly T0/T1 drift check against catalogue). IT-ID-WI-04 Non-Human Identity Governance (10 steps: inventory across all platforms — AD service accounts, gMSA, sMSA, Entra service principals, app registrations, system / user-assigned managed identities, workload identity federation, certificates; named human owner per identity with backup owner; scope justification at creation including "why not managed identity"; credential rotation discipline per type — gMSA auto, AD password annual / 60d target, service principal secret <1yr expiry, certs per policy; prefer managed identities and workload federation over secrets; secret expiry tracking dashboard with 30 / 14 / 7 / 1-day pre-expiry alerts; quarterly OAuth permission and admin-consent grant review with high-risk-permission focus; decommissioning lifecycle — 90d stale → 180d disabled → 365d deleted with test-disable; flag interactive sign-in on service accounts as compromise indicator; privileged non-human identities follow eligibility pattern). IT-ID-WI-05 Privileged Identity Governance (10 steps: privileged role catalogue with per-individual eligibility; eligibility-not-assignment principle with break-glass as the documented exception only; time-bound eligibility — T0 max 12 months, T1 max 18 months; approval workflow — Tier-0 two-person, Tier-1 single, no self-approval, deputy chain for absence; separation of duties at Tier-0 across functional domains — cloud identity, on-prem identity, security operations, audit / compliance; track and minimise standing privilege with target = break-glass count; quarterly eligibility review with activity correlation — zero-activation eligibilities scrutinised; privileged access request workflow with out-of-band-grant detection; Tier-0 activation correlated with PAW usage per IT-EP-SOP-PAW-01; Tier-0 administrative activity to immutable SIEM with monthly SOC review. Boundary maintained: activation = IT-CL-WI-01 §7 mechanics; eligibility = this WI. Hybrid + non-human discipline: the domain treats five identity classes with distinct lifecycle patterns rather than a one-size-fits-all "users" approach. Non-human identities — service accounts, service principals, managed identities — get a dedicated WI (WI-04) as a first-class concern, addressing the largest under-governed surface in most estates where non-human identities outnumber humans 5-30x and frequently lack named owners, credential rotation discipline, and decommissioning lifecycle. Deferred to future deliveries: none specifically for this domain — the boundary with adjacent domains (IT-CL-WI-01 platform config, IT-SI-WI-04 PAM mechanics, IT-EP-SOP-PAW-01 PAW, IT-SRV-WI-DC-01 Tier-0, SOC SOP-ID-01 incident response) is deliberate rather than deferred. Cloud Baselines domain published — new ASM domain covering Microsoft 365 and Microsoft Azure tenants with SOP + 5 Work Instructions, licensing-tier-aware throughout
Closed the gap the Security Infrastructure Baselines domain explicitly deferred: there were no organisation-level security baselines for the cloud tenants (M365 and Azure) used across the institution and its agencies. Published a complete tenant-level baseline as a seventh ASM sub-domain. Unlike the on-prem domains, the cloud baseline is explicitly licensing-tier-aware — each requirement specifies the minimum licence at which it applies (A1/E1 / A3/E3 / E5), so tenants on different tiers are reported against achievable expectations rather than against an aspirational universal standard. asm-cloud-baselines.html — new ASM domain hub. Scope (in): M365 productivity tenants across all agencies (Education A1/A3/A5 and Enterprise/Business E1/E3/E5); Azure subscriptions where the institution or an agency operates resources; Entra ID at tenant-configuration level. Scope (out, explicit deferrals): AWS / GCP (deferred to future multi-cloud extension); identity governance — JML, recertification, eligibility curation (deferred to future Identity domain SOP; tenant-level Conditional Access / PIM / break-glass in scope); SOC operational use of Defender tooling — alert triage, incident response, hunt queries (covered by SOC SOPs; tenant-side configuration in scope); third-party cloud-WAFs — Cloudflare, AWS WAF, Akamai (deferred to future vendor-specific extension; Azure-native WAF in scope); cloud architecture reference design — landing zone hierarchies, hub-spoke topology (deferred to future Cloud Architecture document; Azure Policy as enforcement in scope). IT-CL-SOP-01 (Cloud Security Baseline) — 7-section policy SOP. Purpose, Scope (with the five explicit deferrals above), 7 Baseline Principles (shared-responsibility model is the framing — vendor administers the platform, we administer the tenant; licensing-tier-aware not tier-blind; identity is the perimeter — AAL3-grade for Global Administrators; default-deny applies at the tenant boundary — external sharing, federation, cross-tenant; centralised logging is non-negotiable — baseline target 100% central SIEM integration; Microsoft Secure Score as quantitative complement with per-tier targets; central SIEM platform is under transition — baseline requires the integration, not a specific vendor), Minimum Security Requirements across 5 requirement domains (4.1 Tenant Identity & Conditional Access 9 items; 4.2 M365 Tenant Hardening 11 items; 4.3 Azure Subscription Baseline 10 items; 4.4 Azure Workload Patterns 10 items; 4.5 Defender Suite Configuration 9 items) each operationalised by a dedicated WI and tier-tagged (All / A3/E3+ / E5), Roles & Responsibilities (8 roles including Cloud Engineering, Identity & Access Engineering, M365 Tenant Administrators per agency, Azure Subscription Owners per agency), Framework Alignment, Governance & Review with Secure Score tier targets (M365: A1/E1 reported only, A3/E3 ≥70%, E5 ≥80%; Azure: T0/1 ≥75%, T2/3 ≥60%). Includes a callout flagging that on initial publication of this baseline, every tenant other than the currently SIEM-integrated one is in a deviation state — these are batch-tracked as a single category of exception against the 12–18-month SIEM migration roadmap and become the management view of the remediation programme. Five Work Instructions: IT-CL-WI-01 Tenant Identity & Conditional Access (9 steps cross-cutting M365 and Azure: break-glass / emergency-access accounts — 2 cloud-only, vaulted off-tenant, excluded from every CA policy, annual test, SOC monitoring rule on any sign-in — flagged as the single most important step; eliminate legacy authentication tenant-wide via Conditional Access at A3/E3+ or Security Defaults at A1/E1; MFA enforcement with phishing-resistant FIDO2/WHfB/cert-based for Global Administrators; CA baseline policies CA-001 through CA-007 deployed in report-only-then-enable pattern; named locations — trusted IPs and country sets, but never used to skip MFA; Identity Protection risk policies at E5; PIM with time-bounded eligibility, activation approval for Global Admin, quarterly access reviews; guest / external access governance with default-deny cross-tenant; SSPR with combined registration). IT-CL-WI-02 M365 Tenant Hardening (10 steps tier-aware: Exchange Online anti-spam / anti-malware / anti-phishing baseline at all tiers; SPF / DKIM / DMARC with progression to p=reject over 90–180 days and parked-domain treatment; SharePoint and OneDrive external sharing restrictions with anonymous links off and allow-list domains; Teams external access / federation / meeting policies with lobby for externals; Unified Audit Log enable with tier-aware retention — 90d A1/E1, 1yr A3/E3 with add-on, 1yr default E5 — and central SIEM forwarding; Purview sensitivity labels with default label and mandatory labelling at A3/E3+, auto-labelling at E5; Purview DLP policies Test → Enforce with Endpoint DLP at E5; Defender for Office 365 P2 with Safe Attachments Dynamic Delivery and Safe Links and Attack Simulator at E5; Insider Risk Management and Communication Compliance at E5 with HR / Legal coordination on lawful-basis; Customer Lockbox and PAM for Exchange at E5). IT-CL-WI-03 Azure Subscription Baseline (10 steps: management group hierarchy — Tenant Root → Platform → Landing Zones → environments — with policy assignment at management group scope not subscription; Defender for Cloud enablement with plans per subscription tier — Tier 0/1 = paid Defender plans, Tier 2/3 = Free acceptable only with documented justification; Azure Policy MCSB initiative plus custom deny set — storage public, secure transfer, Key Vault purge protection, public IP in prod, region restrictions — in Audit-before-Deny progression; diagnostic settings via DeployIfNotExists on every resource type routed to Log Analytics + Event Hub; Activity Log forwarding with high-value events alerted — Owner role assignment, diagnostic-setting deletion, NSG 0.0.0.0/0, Defender plan downgrade; Defender for Cloud continuous export to central SIEM; subscription RBAC least-privilege — no standing Owner outside named platform admins, Contributor RG-scoped, managed identities preferred over service principals; resource locks CanNotDelete on critical RGs; cost-management alerting — cryptomining and egress spikes are leading indicators of compromise; tagging policy enforced for CostCentre / Owner / Environment / SensitivityClassification / Agency). IT-CL-WI-04 Azure Workload Patterns (10 steps: storage account baseline — secure transfer HTTPS, public blob anonymous off, TLS 1.2, network restricted, CMK + soft-delete + immutable for Tier 0/1; Key Vault baseline — RBAC mode, soft-delete and purge protection non-disablable as the single most important Key Vault setting, Private Endpoint, HSM-backed keys at Tier 0/1; VNet / NSG baseline — NSG every subnet, default-deny inbound, no 0.0.0.0/0 to sensitive ports, flow logs to Log Analytics, ASGs for role-based references; Private Endpoints preferred over public for PaaS data services with private DNS zone architecture and VNet links; Azure WAF on Application Gateway / Front Door with managed rule set OWASP Top 10 in Prevention mode within 90 days for Tier 0/1, bot manager rules, custom rules — geo-restriction, rate limiting — and log streaming — this step absorbs the Azure-native cloud-WAF scope previously deferred from IT-SI-WI-02; SQL / Cosmos protection — Entra auth, Defender plans, TDE with CMK at Tier 0/1; VM baseline — Defender for Servers, JIT for management ports, managed identities, Trusted Launch where supported; App Service / Functions — HTTPS-only, managed identity, Easy Auth, Key Vault references for secrets, slot-swap discipline; backup posture with Recovery Services Vault soft-delete and multi-user authorisation; DDoS Protection Standard on VNets containing Tier 0/1 public IPs). Security Infrastructure Baselines domain published — new ASM domain covering WAFs, load balancers, and PAM platforms with SOP + 4 Work Instructions
Closed the gap the Network Device Baselines domain explicitly deferred: there were no organisation-level security baselines for the high-value security-infrastructure platforms (WAFs, load balancers, PAM). Published a complete vendor-agnostic baseline as a sixth ASM sub-domain. Unlike the network domain (where switches, routers, firewalls, wireless APs share a common control-domain structure), WAF/LB/PAM have distinct architectures; the domain is therefore organised by device type with a shared common-management-plane WI rather than by control domain. asm-security-infrastructure-baselines.html — new ASM domain hub. Scope (in): WAFs both on-prem (F5 ASM, Imperva, FortiWeb, Barracuda) and cloud-managed (Azure Front Door / Application Gateway WAF, Cloudflare, AWS WAF); load balancers and ADCs (F5 LTM, Citrix ADC, HAProxy, NGINX Plus, A10, AVI); PAM platforms (CyberArk, BeyondTrust, Delinea, HashiCorp Vault). Scope (out): reverse proxies and API gateways deferred to a future Application Delivery sub-domain; cloud-WAF tenant-platform integration deferred to the future Cloud Baselines domain; PAM identity governance deferred to a future Identity domain SOP. IT-SI-SOP-01 (Security Infrastructure Baseline) — 7-section policy SOP. Purpose, Scope (with explicit deferrals), 7 Baseline Principles (high-value targets need stronger baseline; authentication strength matches what's protected — AAL3 for Tier 0 PAM admin; default-deny applies to everything including the platforms themselves; cryptography in critical path non-negotiable; PAM has its own recovery path — vault-independent break-glass; WAF tuning continuous; comprehensive logging and audited privileged actions), Minimum Security Requirements across 4 req-domains (4.1 Common Management 10 items; 4.2 WAF 8 items; 4.3 Load Balancer 10 items; 4.4 PAM 10 items) each operationalised by a dedicated WI, Roles & Responsibilities (8 roles including Identity & Access Engineering co-owner for PAM aspects), Framework Alignment, Governance & Review.
